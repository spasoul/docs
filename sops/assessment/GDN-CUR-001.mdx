## Purpose

Standardize the spa arrival and check-in experience so every guest is greeted by name, identified, oriented to the facilities, provided with appropriate attire, and escorted to the correct area — creating a calm, welcoming sense of arrival from the moment they enter.

## Scope

1. [S1] Applies to all spa reception staff, spa coordinators, and any team member involved in the guest arrival process.
2. [S2] Covers the sequence from guest entry through escort to the Relaxation Lounge or locker room, including the pre-arrival late-call protocol.
3. [S3] Treatment room handoff and orientation are covered in SOP-GDN-CUR-002.

## Responsibility

1. [R1] **Spa Receptionist** — executes the full arrival and check-in sequence; must never leave the reception desk unattended.
2. [R2] **Spa Reception Manager / Supervisor** — takes over the facility tour after the receptionist completes initial intake.
3. [R3] **All Spa Staff** — when guests arrive at the spa, everyone should warmly greet guests.

## Procedure

**Reception Readiness**

1. [P1] The reception desk must never be left unattended. Under no circumstance is any untrained member of staff to answer telephones, take bookings, or greet guests, or be left in control of reception.
2. [P2] A designated employee must be present upon arrival. [LQA 21.10]
3. [P3] When entering the spa, there must be a sense of arrival that is calm and serene — through light, sound, and scent. [LQA 21.9]

**Pre-Arrival Late Call**

4. [P4] If the guest has not arrived five (5) minutes before their treatment, call their room to enquire about their whereabouts. Leave a voicemail if unanswered: state your name, where you are calling from, remind them of their spa treatment in five minutes, and request they contact you on the spa extension.

**Greeting**

5. [P5] Be ready to greet the guest by name — anticipate names from the booking sheet before the guest speaks. Recognize repeat guests by name without prompting.
6. [P6] Establish eye contact, smile, and stand. Leave the desk and greet the guest in front of the desk so there is no barrier between you and the guest.
7. [P7] Use the standard arrival greeting: *"Welcome to [SPA NAME] at [PROPERTY], Mr./Mrs. [GUEST NAME]."*
8. [P8] If you are on the telephone when a guest arrives, ask the caller if you may put them on hold, then attend to the guest in front of you.

**Check-In**

9. [P9] Confirm the guest's appointment details — type and length of treatment(s), and the scheduled time(s). [LQA 21.11]
10. [P10] Name the therapist who will perform each treatment.
11. [P11] Secure the method of payment and the room number at check-in — not at check-out.
12. [P12] Ask the guest to complete the health consultation card (capturing touch preference, style, oil/lotion preference, and general health information) if not already on file.
13. [P13] Multiple guests arriving at the same time must be checked in one by one.

**Attire & Locker Assignment**

14. [P14] Provide appropriate-size bathrobes and slippers, clean and in good condition. [LQA 21.13]
15. [P15] Make the guest aware of how they should be attired for the selected treatment. [LQA 21.14]
16. [P16] Do not give guests arriving at the same time lockers next to each other.

**Escort & Orientation**

17. [P17] If the guest arrives in a robe, escort directly to the Relaxation Lounge. If not, escort to the locker room first.
18. [P18] The escort must be the same gender as the guest.
19. [P19] For first-time guests, escort to the changing rooms and explain the layout and facilities. [LQA 21.12]
20. [P20] Introduce all amenities along the way: relaxation area, hammam, vitality pool, refreshment station, restrooms.
21. [P21] Advise the guest of the sequence of events — where to go once changed, and that the therapist will collect them from the Relaxation Lounge. [LQA 21.15]
22. [P22] Offer 10 minutes to refresh before the treatment. Serve the welcome beverage.
23. [P23] Ensure masks and automatic hand disinfection are available at spa reception per current hygiene protocols.

**Therapist Running Late**

24. [P24] If the therapist is not available at the booking time, communicate immediately. Assure the guest that their treatment will not be cut short. If the delay affects the next guest, contact them to adjust timing. [LQA 21.16, 21.17 — cross-ref SOP-SVC-SPA-001]

**Handoff**

25. [P25] Once the guest is settled, offer a farewell: *"Enjoy your treatment."*
26. [P26] Note: maximum occupancy in the spa relaxation area is as posted by the property (e.g., 6 guests). Monitor and manage flow accordingly.
