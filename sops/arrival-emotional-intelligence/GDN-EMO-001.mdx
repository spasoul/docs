## Purpose

Establish observable behavioral standards for all guest-facing associates in the Guidance department — Curators and Concierge staff. These standards define the emotional intelligence and service quality behaviors audited under LQA Section 19 (Fitness, standards 19.49–19.62) and LQA Section 21 (Spa, standards 21.41–21.53). The behavioral content of standards 19.49–19.61 / 21.41–21.53 is mirror-identical across departments; this single SOP satisfies both sets.

This SOP is the reference destination for EI cross-references in:
- SOP-GDN-CUR-001 through SOP-GDN-CUR-006
- SOP-GDN-CCG-001 through SOP-GDN-CCG-009

---

## Scope

All guest-facing associates in:
- **Curator (CUR)** — Longevity Concierges performing assessments, guest orientation, programme design
- **Concierge (CCG)** — Sales Representatives handling reservations, reception, billing, scheduling

Applies from first guest contact through final farewell, in every type of interaction: in-person, phone, and written guest communication where observable behavior is assessable.

## Procedure

#### Section A — Presentation

**1.** [P1] **Grooming & Presentation** [LQA 19.49 / 21.41]
   - Be well groomed and neatly presented in a clean, well-fitted uniform at all times during shift.
   - Wear a name badge unless the property has formally adopted an alternative name-introduction method (see Brand Notes).
   - The overall appearance must result in a positive first impression on the guest.

---

#### Section B — Communication

**2.** [P2] **Speech Clarity & Language** [LQA 19.50 / 21.42]
   - Speech must be clear; English must be satisfactory to enable genuine two-way conversation with the guest.
   - Language limitations must not prevent full service delivery. Associates who identify a gap must seek support — translation assistance, a colleague, or alternative communication — without making the guest feel the limitation is their problem.

**3.** [P3] **Natural & Engaged Manner** [LQA 19.51 / 21.43]
   - Engage guests in a well-paced, natural, friendly, and interested manner.
   - Scripted recitation, jargon, and slang are non-compliant. Interactions must feel genuine — not procedural or rehearsed.
   - A service script used as a knowledge framework is acceptable; delivering it verbatim without responsiveness to the guest is not.

**4.** [P4] **Guest Name Use** [LQA 19.52 / 21.44]
   - Use the guest's name naturally and discreetly.
   - Do not overuse the guest's name — frequency must feel personal, not rehearsed.
   - If uncertain of correct pronunciation, confirm quietly before using the name. Do not guess.

---

#### Section C — Service Quality

**5.** [P5] **Confidence & Knowledge** [LQA 19.53 / 21.45]
   - Display a high level of confidence when carrying out duties.
   - Be knowledgeable when answering questions about other hotel facilities.
   - If the answer is not immediately known: offer to find out promptly and follow through. Do not guess, deflect, or send the guest to another team without resolving the query first.

**6.** [P6] **Service Anticipation** [LQA 19.54 / 21.46]
   - Service must be anticipatory and intuitive: think ahead and act on future needs and opportunities of the guest before being asked.
   - Reactive service alone is non-compliant. Associates must read the situation and pre-empt needs. Training must include at least three concrete anticipation scenarios per role (see Brand Notes).

**7.** [P7] **Adaptability** [LQA 19.55 / 21.47]
   - Adapt to changing situations and/or the guest's evolving need.
   - Rigid procedural application when a guest's situation clearly calls for flexibility is non-compliant.

**8.** [P8] **Meeting Guest Requests** [LQA 19.56 / 21.48]
   - Make every effort to meet the guest's request.
   - Where a request cannot be fulfilled: offer a suitable alternative. A plain refusal with no alternative is non-compliant.

**9.** [P9] **Personalization** [LQA 19.57 / 21.49]
   - Personalize the interaction in at least one meaningful way; engage the guest as an individual.
   - Generic, purely transactional interactions with no individualization are non-compliant.

---

#### Section D — Team Conduct & Environment

**10.** [P10] **Seamless Collaboration** [LQA 19.58 / 21.50]
   - Collaborate seamlessly with colleagues: service must be organized and professional, not intrusive or repetitive.
   - Handoffs between team members must be invisible to the guest.
   - Associates must not ask the guest to repeat information already shared with another team member.

**11.** [P11] **Active Listening** [LQA 19.59 / 21.51]
   - Actively listen; avoid interrupting; give the guest undivided attention.
   - The guest must never be required to repeat themselves.
   - No divided attention (phone, colleague conversation, other task) during a live guest interaction.

**12.** [P12] **Posture & Alertness** [LQA 19.60 / 21.52]
   - Maintain alert postures at all times.
   - Respect the guest's presence when interacting with colleagues — associates must not speak, laugh, or behave in a manner that excludes or disregards a nearby guest.

---

#### Section E — Challenging Situations & Farewell

**13.** [P13] **Self-Control & Empathy** [LQA 19.61 / 21.53]
   - In challenging interactions: display self-control and empathy.
   - Offer a suitable alternative or resolution. Do not match guest escalation with emotional escalation.
   - The associate's demeanor must remain calm, warm, and professional regardless of the guest's tone.
   - **Escalation threshold:** If a situation is genuinely unsafe or verbally abusive, escalate to a supervisor immediately. This standard does not require associates to absorb abuse; it requires that they do not escalate it.

**14.** [P14] **Sincere Farewell** [LQA 19.62] *(Fitness only — see note)*
   - Offer a sincere farewell at the end of the conversation and show appreciation.
   - The farewell must feel genuine — warm, personal, and not scripted.
   - **Spa note:** The equivalent farewell standard for Spa associates is LQA 21.40, incorporated in SOP-SVC-SPA-001. Both standards require identical behavior; the LQA section placement differs by department (TRT block for Spa, EI block for Fitness).

<Accordion title="Brand Notes" icon="building">
*(Deployment guidance for property teams. Not part of the auditable standard.)*

**LQA structure note:** LQA assesses emotional intelligence under Section 19 (Fitness) as standards 19.49–19.62 and under Section 21 (Spa) as standards 21.41–21.53. The behavioral content is mirror-identical for 19.49–19.61 / 21.41–21.53. This single shared SOP satisfies both blocks and should be cited as the reference document in audit evidence for both sections.

**Farewell placement quirk:** In LQA, the Spa farewell sits at 21.40 — the final standard of THE TREATMENT block — not in the EI block. The Fitness farewell sits at 19.62 — the final standard of the EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE block. Both require the same behavior. For internal training and coaching, this SOP covers farewell under P.14 for Fitness; Spa farewell is covered by SOP-SVC-SPA-001. LQA auditors will score each under their respective section numbers.

**Name badge exception:** Some ultra-luxury properties (e.g., properties that use introduction-by-name without a physical badge) may satisfy LQA 19.49 / 21.41 through a formally documented alternative. If the property operates this model, document the substitute method and brief LQA auditors in advance — the standard is satisfied by known-name delivery, not specifically by badge presence.

**Natural vs. scripted manner (P.3):** The natural manner standard is one of the most commonly failed LQA standards across both Spa and Fitness. The distinction: using a script as a knowledge structure is compliant; reciting it verbatim without responsiveness to the guest is non-compliant. Training must drill responsive, in-the-moment delivery, not memorization.

**Service anticipation (P.6):** Consistently cited as a top Forbes and LQA gap in luxury spa/fitness operations. Each role must have at least three concrete, role-specific anticipation scenarios built into training. Examples: Fitness Attendant offering a cool towel before the guest finishes their set; Pool Attendant refilling water without being asked; Spa Therapist pre-warming the bed to the guest's stated temperature preference.

**Challenging interactions (P.13):** LQA evaluates this standard even when the guest is the aggressor. The self-control requirement applies universally. Where a situation escalates to genuine abuse or safety concern, the correct action is supervisor escalation — not continued exposure. This must be communicated clearly in training so associates do not feel the standard requires them to accept abusive behavior.

**Coaching cadence:** Department Directors should conduct at least one structured EI observation per associate per quarter using the Checklist section. Non-compliant scores on any single standard should trigger a coaching conversation within 48 hours. Two consecutive NC ratings on the same standard should trigger a formal development plan.
</Accordion>
