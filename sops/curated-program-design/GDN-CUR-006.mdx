## Purpose

To ensure all guest health, biometric, and programme data collected by the Longevity department is processed, stored, accessed, and disposed of in full compliance with KVKK (Law No. 6698) and applicable Turkish health data regulations.

---

## Scope

Covers all data types collected across the Longevity department: health questionnaires, assessment results, biomarker data, treatment records, programme documents, consent forms, and any wearable or device-generated data.

---

## Responsibility

[Data Protection Officer] owns the data governance framework. [Longevity Director] is accountable for departmental compliance. All staff are responsible for following this SOP when handling any guest data.

---

## Procedure

### Section A — Consent & Data Collection

1. [P1] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Obtain explicit written KVKK health data consent before collecting any health or biometric data. Consent must specify: data types collected, purpose, retention period, who has access, right to withdraw.
2. [P2] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Obtain separate informed consent for each clinical procedure (IV, ozone, diagnostics) per SOP-GDN-CUR-003.
3. [P3] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Never collect health data from a guest who has not provided explicit consent; defer all assessments until consent is confirmed.

### Section B — Data Storage & Access Control

4. [P4] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Store all health data in the [Longevity Management System] — a KVKK-compliant, encrypted platform with role-based access controls.
5. [P5] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Access restricted to: [Physician], [Longevity Specialist] (own guest files only), [Longevity Director] (all files), [Data Protection Officer] (audit access).
6. [P6] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Paper records (signed consents, hand-completed forms) stored in locked filing cabinet; access logged.
7. [P7] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — No health data to be stored on personal devices, shared drives, or messaging applications (WhatsApp, email) without encryption.

### Section C — Data Use Restrictions

8. [P8] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Health data used exclusively for: designing guest programmes, delivering treatments, clinical safety monitoring, and legal/regulatory compliance.
9. [P9] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Health data must not be shared with other hotel departments, third parties, or contractors without explicit guest consent and Data Protection Officer approval.
10. [P10] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Emergency exception: health data may be shared with emergency services without consent if life is at risk (document immediately after).

### Section D — Retention & Disposal

11. [P11] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Retain health records for [X years] per Turkish health record retention requirements; clinical records for [Y years].
12. [P12] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — At end of retention period: digital data permanently deleted (confirmed by IT); paper records shredded (certificate obtained).
13. [P13] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Guest data deletion requests: process within [X working days]; confirm deletion in writing to guest; log in the KVKK request register.

### Section E — Breach Response

14. [P14] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Any suspected data breach: notify [Data Protection Officer] immediately; preserve evidence; do not attempt to contain breach independently.
15. [P15] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Data Protection Officer assesses breach severity; if notifiable, report to KVKK Board (Kişisel Verileri Koruma Kurumu) within [72 hours].

## MEVZUAT Note

> **KVKK Law No. 6698** (Personal Data Protection Law): Article 6 governs collection and processing of sensitive health data — requires explicit written consent, role-based access controls, and secure storage; Article 8 mandates access logging and audit trails; Article 12 requires technical and organisational security measures including encryption; breaches reported to KVKK Board within 72 hours; Article 9 (Kişisel verilerin yurt dışına aktarılması — Cross-border data transfer conditions); Article 10 (Aydınlatma yükümlülüğü — Transparency); Article 11 (İlgili kişinin hakları — Data subject rights: access, correction, deletion, objection); Article 17 (Kabahatler — Criminal liability for data security breach); Article 18 (İdari para cezaları — Administrative penalties: 5,000–1,000,000 TL). **Law No. 6331** (Occupational Health and Safety Law): Articles 4 and 10 require safe storage and access protocols for health records in the facility.
