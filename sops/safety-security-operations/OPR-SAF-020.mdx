## Purpose

Ensure all associates are protected from struck-by object injuries through situational awareness, correct storage, and safe handling practices.

---

## Scope

All departments. All associates working or moving anywhere on [the property].

---

## Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Training on struck-by hazard awareness; oversee storage and shelving safety |
| Department Heads | Reinforce procedures in storage, kitchen, and corridor areas; ensure shelving is earthquake-secured |
| All associates | Maintain situational awareness at all times; follow right-of-way, storage, and knife safety rules |

---

## Procedure

#### Section A — Situational Awareness

**1.** [P1] **Struck-by hazard types** — Struck-by injuries are caused by forcible contact or impact between a person and an object or piece of equipment. The four types are: struck-by a flying object; struck-by a falling object; struck-by a swinging object; struck-by a rolling object.

**2.** [P2] **Know your surroundings** — You must always be aware of your total surroundings. Be cautious of sconce lighting in guest hallways. Be cautious of electrical boxes and other objects mounted on walls in back-of-house hallways. Always look in the direction you are walking.

---

#### Section B — Corridors and Moving Vehicles

**3.** [P3] **Designated routes for wheeled equipment** — Pedestrian-operated pallet trucks, racks, trolleys, and similar equipment should use designated routes away from other workers wherever possible. The person pushing or pulling must maintain good visibility.

**4.** [P4] **Right-of-way rule** — Large, non-self-propelled rolling vehicles (carts, housekeeping bins, Queen Mary carts, and similar) have the right of way in back-of-house corridors, as they are difficult to stop quickly. Always move to the side when approached by these vehicles. Exception: in guest hallways, external guests always have the right of way.

**5.** [P5] **Do not stop in corridors** — Do not stop and talk in the centre of main corridors. Move to the side to keep the corridor clear for vehicles passing through.

**6.** [P6] **Parking and loading areas** — Always be aware of moving vehicles in parking lots and loading docks. Stay within crosswalks and sidewalks whenever possible.

---

#### Section C — Wind and Weather Hazards

**7.** [P7] **Wind tunnels** — Wind tunnels in guest corridors and front-of-house areas can cause serious injury. Open patio doors combined with a guestroom entry door open during high winds create a wind tunnel. Fingers, hands, and other body parts can be seriously injured by a hard and fast-slamming door.

**8.** [P8] **High wind precautions** — Take extra safety precautions when weather conditions involve high winds. High winds can cause doors to slam, objects to fly, beach chairs to flip, and reduce hearing. Always be prepared to protect yourself from wind-related hazards.

---

#### Section D — Shelving and Storage Safety

**9.** [P9] **Shelf arrangement** — Always use caution when removing objects from shelving. Arrange stored objects as follows: heavy objects on the centre shelves; medium objects on the bottom shelves; light objects on the top shelves. Heavy objects falling from the top shelf can cause serious injury.

**10.** [P10] **Secure tall objects** — Ensure tall, self-standing objects (e.g. gas cylinders) or objects leaning against walls are either stable if knocked, or physically secured.

**11.** [P11] **Elevated storage stability** — Ensure items stored above ground level (e.g. on storage shelving) are stable and will not fall easily if disturbed.

**12.** [P12] **Earthquake securing** — Shelving must be fixed and secured against toppling to provide protection in the event of seismic activity.

---

#### Section E — Construction Areas and Knives

**13.** [P13] **Construction areas** — Exercise heightened caution in all construction areas. Always wear a hard hat. Watch where you are walking. Look above, below, to the left, to the right, and to the rear. Be especially cautious around heavy equipment and machinery moving objects.

**14.** [P14] **Hand knife storage** — Hand knives must be safely stored or sheathed when not in use. Hand knives cause the greatest number of cut injuries.

**15.** [P15] **Knife-resistant PPE** — When hand knives are in regular use, knife-resistant protective clothing must be worn as determined by the risk assessment (e.g. for butchering: apron and forearm guard or glove for the non-knife hand).

**16.** [P16] **Hand tool maintenance** — Hand tools must be maintained in good condition so that excessive force is not required to use them.

---

#### Section F — Doors

**17.** [P17] **Open doors slowly** — Be aware that people may be on the other side of a door. Open doors slowly. Announce yourself before opening. Where possible and appropriate, add a window to doors in high-traffic areas.
