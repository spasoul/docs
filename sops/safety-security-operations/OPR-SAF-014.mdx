## Purpose

Ensure all associates follow correct procedures for repetitive motion tasks in order to prevent Cumulative Trauma Disorders (CTDs) and related injuries.

---

## Scope

All departments. All associates performing tasks that involve repetitive motion, repeated use of tools, keyboard work, or sustained grip.

---

## Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Training on CTD prevention; workstation safety oversight |
| Department Heads | Assess workstations; respond to associate requests for workstation improvement |
| All associates | Follow CTD prevention techniques; report symptoms early; perform recommended exercises |

---

## Procedure

#### Section A — Understanding Cumulative Trauma Disorders

**1.** [P1] **CTD awareness** — Fatigue or tiredness in muscles or joints is the body's signal to change its pattern of working. Performing the same motion repeatedly, or using certain sustained positions or grips, can cause pain and inflammation. The best form of prevention is to limit the time spent doing the same motion repeatedly — whether lifting, using a keyboard, or operating a hand or power tool.

**2.** [P2] **Carpal Tunnel Syndrome** — Painful squeezing of the median nerve in the wrist, causing loss of grip, muscle pain, weakness, and numbness in the thumb and first two fingers. If rest and medication do not resolve symptoms after an appropriate period, surgery may be required.

**3.** [P3] **Tenosynovitis** — Inflammation of a tendon and its covering, which can be caused by improper or repetitive bending of the wrist.

**4.** [P4] **Tendinitis** — Inflammation of the tendons, which can be caused by performing repeated motions incorrectly or in an awkward position.

---

#### Section B — Prevention Techniques

**5.** [P5] **Limit repetitive motion** — Avoid or minimise physically straining repetitive activities. Vary tasks where possible to reduce sustained repetition.

**6.** [P6] **Change hands** — Change hands or sides periodically to reduce stress on one hand or limb.

**7.** [P7] **Hand exercises** — Perform proper hand exercises regularly to maintain flexibility and reduce strain.

**8.** [P8] **Wrist braces** — Use wrist braces or other supportive devices to help reduce the stress caused by repetitive motion.

**9.** [P9] **Exercise before symptoms appear** — Begin preventive exercise and protective practices before symptoms develop — do not wait for pain or discomfort to start.

**10.** [P10] **Workstation improvement** — If workstation improvements are needed to reduce repetitive strain, consult your Department Head and request the necessary changes.

**11.** [P11] **Manual handling technique** — Follow correct manual handling techniques at all times as an integrated part of repetitive motion prevention.
