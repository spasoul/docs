## Purpose

Ensure associates who use display screen equipment are protected from work-related upper limb disorders, eye strain, and stress caused by prolonged or improper use.

---

## Scope

All departments. All associates who regularly use display screen equipment including monitors, keyboards, and associated workstation components.

---

## Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Oversee workstation assessments; training on DSE safety |
| Department Heads | Ensure workstation assessments are carried out for associates using display screen equipment |
| All associates | Set up workstations correctly; follow posture and break guidance; report discomfort |

---

## Procedure

#### Section A — Workstation Assessment

**1.** [P1] **Workstation assessment** — Where associates use display screen equipment, an assessment of the workstation must be carried out. The assessment covers the screen, chair, desk, keyboard, mouse, and associated equipment including document holders.

---

#### Section B — Screen and Lighting

**2.** [P2] **Screen settings** — Adjust screen controls so that the display is comfortable. Use a clear background colour with dark, legible fonts in an adequate size to reduce eye strain.

**3.** [P3] **Adequate lighting** — Ensure lighting in the work area is adequate.

**4.** [P4] **Avoid glare** — Reduce glare by using indirect or filtered lighting (standing lamps, filtered sunlight), introducing a flat screen, enabling positive polarity, applying glare filters to the screen, or wearing computer glasses.

---

#### Section C — Workstation Setup

**5.** [P5] **Leg space** — There must be sufficient leg space under the desk to allow free movement of the feet.

**6.** [P6] **Chair adjustment** — Adjust the chair so you are comfortable. The chair must be adjustable in three ways: seat height, backrest height, and backrest angle. The seat pan must not press behind the knees.

**7.** [P7] **Sit back** — Sit back in the chair for full support — not on the front edge. Adjust the backrest for lumbar support.

**8.** [P8] **Screen position** — Position the screen appropriately for the work being carried out. Do not sit too close. The top of the monitor should be at or just below eye level.

**9.** [P9] **Keyboard and mouse** — Place the keyboard and mouse on the same surface.

---

#### Section D — Seated Posture

**10.** [P10] **Head and neck** — Keep the head and neck upright, facing forward. Keep the head balanced naturally over the shoulders — not protruding forward.

**11.** [P11] **Shoulders and arms** — Keep shoulders relaxed and close to the body, perpendicular to the floor. Keep upper arms close to the body.

**12.** [P12] **Forearms, wrists, and hands** — Keep forearms and thighs parallel to the floor. Keep forearms, wrists, and hands in a straight line. Wrists should be straight with hands at or below elbow level. Avoid bending wrists while typing.

**13.** [P13] **Feet** — Settle feet flat on the floor or on a footrest.

---

#### Section E — Breaks and Wrist Care

**14.** [P14] **Wrist stretches** — Stretch wrists each hour by pressing the palm flat against a wall, or pressing palms together for 30 seconds, to help prevent tendinitis.

**15.** [P15] **Eye rest** — Look away from the screen for a few seconds every 20 minutes to rest your eyes.

**16.** [P16] **Regular breaks** — Take regular breaks when using display screen equipment for extended periods.
