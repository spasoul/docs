## Purpose

Ensure all associates are protected from accidents caused by work equipment through proper guarding, inspection, maintenance, and safe operating practices.

---

## Scope

All departments. All associates using any work equipment on [the property].

---

## Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Training on work equipment safety; oversight of compliance |
| [Technical Director] | Oversee maintenance programme; ensure maintenance and repairs are recorded |
| Department Heads | Ensure equipment is maintained, inspected regularly, and that associates are adequately trained; complete monthly Work Equipment Control Form |
| All associates | Visually inspect equipment before each use; report faults immediately; follow all operating procedures |

---

## Procedure

#### Section A — Equipment Scope and Hazard Types

**1.** [P1] **What constitutes work equipment** — Work equipment includes anything used at work: trolleys; high chairs; hand tools (knives, utensils); power tools; vacuum cleaners; staging; access equipment (ladders, footstools); shelving; wheelchairs; tables and chairs; and general office equipment.

**2.** [P2] **Four main types of equipment danger** — When assessing any piece of equipment, the following four dangers must be identified and assessed: ejection of parts, material, or object; unintentional contact with a moving part; entanglement of clothing; and entrapment of a finger or arm.

---

#### Section B — Guarding Requirements

**3.** [P3] **Guard types** — The main types of guard are: fixed, interlock, trip, and automatic. Having identified the dangers of the equipment, guarding is the primary control measure.

**4.** [P4] **Equipment suitability** — All work equipment must be suitable for the purpose for which it is to be used and for where it is to be used. Equipment is only purchased if it is suitable for the purpose intended.

**5.** [P5] **Guard quality** — Machine guards must be of good quality and must not be easily disabled.

**6.** [P6] **Warning devices and stop buttons** — Warning devices must be fitted where appropriate. Equipment with sudden-stop buttons should be preferred. Emergency stop controls must be maintained, unobstructed, and easily reachable. They are not a substitute for safeguarding.

**7.** [P7] **Energy isolation** — All work equipment must be provided with suitable means to isolate it from all sources of energy.

**8.** [P8] **Stabilisation** — Work equipment that operates in a fixed position must be suitably stabilised, for example by clamping.

**9.** [P9] **Lighting** — There must be suitable and sufficient lighting to enable safe use of equipment at all times.

---

#### Section C — Maintenance and Records

**10.** [P10] **Service per guidelines** — All equipment must be serviced in accordance with manufacturers' and/or suppliers' guidelines.

**11.** [P11] **Maintenance records** — Maintenance and repairs are monitored and recorded. Departmental managers ensure that equipment is maintained and inspected regularly. Maintenance records kept electronically in accordance with Electronic Signature Law No. 5070 are considered valid maintenance records.

**12.** [P12] **Visual inspection before use** — All equipment must be visually inspected before each use. Follow warning signs and information.

**13.** [P13] **Monthly equipment control** — Check and record equipment condition on a monthly basis using the Work Equipment Control Form created for your department.

**14.** [P14] **Report faults immediately** — Report any faults — electrical or mechanical — immediately to your Supervisor or Head of Department.

**15.** [P15] **Equipment under repair** — When a machine is under repair or being serviced, those responsible must ensure it cannot be used accidentally. The machine must be isolated by disconnecting the power supply and removing the plug fuse. Where practicable, the equipment should be removed from the work area. A sign indicating the fault must remain on the equipment until it has been repaired.

---

#### Section D — Operation and Cleaning

**16.** [P16] **Training and instructions** — Associates must receive adequate information, instruction, and training for the equipment they use. Each machine or piece of electrical equipment must have a Using Instructions document, which must be brought to the attention of all operators. Warning signs and markings must be clearly visible and unambiguous.

**17.** [P17] **Before starting** — Before commencing use, ensure equipment is correctly reassembled. Check all guarding mechanisms. Under no circumstances is dangerous equipment to be operated unless all prescribed guards are in position.

**18.** [P18] **Cleaning dangerous machines** — When cleaning dangerous machines, ensure the machine is switched off and the plug removed from the mains socket, or the electrical supply isolated, before cleaning. Take extra care handling sharp or dangerous parts during cleaning.

---

#### Section E — Dangerous Equipment

**19.** [P19] **What is dangerous equipment** — Certain work equipment has moving parts and is mechanically driven and therefore requires special attention. Examples include: meat slicers, food processors, blenders, compactors, wood-working machines, and motorised pumps within plant rooms.

**20.** [P20] **Age restriction** — No person under the age of 18 may use dangerous equipment. No cleaning of such machines may be undertaken by anyone under the age of 18.

**21.** [P21] **No alcohol** — Under no circumstances may alcohol be consumed by associates operating dangerous machinery or driving company vehicles.

**22.** [P22] **No contractor use** — Under no circumstances may any contractor use [the property's] dangerous machines.

**23.** [P23] **Additional dangerous equipment rules** — The following rules must be observed in addition to the general rules above: never switch on a machine if you do not know how it works; always switch off and unplug before cleaning; do not assemble a machine without having been shown how to do so by a supervisor or manager; check the rated capacity of a machine before loading; if a machine makes a noise or sparks, switch it off immediately and inform your supervisor/manager and Engineering without delay; always follow the cleaning and operating procedures for each machine; if you are not sure — ask.
