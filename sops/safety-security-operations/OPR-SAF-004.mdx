### Purpose

Ensure safe inspection, selection, positioning, use, and storage of ladders.

---

### Scope

All departments. All associates using ladders for any work task.

---

### Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Oversee ladder safety compliance; ensure risk assessments are in place |
| Department Heads | Ensure associates are trained and fit before ladder use |
| [Technical Department] | Inspect, repair, and maintain ladders; manage Ladder Control Form |
| All associates | Complete pre-use inspection; follow usage rules; report defects |

---

### Procedure

#### Section A — Before Use

**1.** [P1] **Fitness to use** — Ensure you are fit to use a ladder. Certain medical conditions, medication, alcohol, or drug use make ladder use unsafe.

**2.** [P2] **Risk assessment** — Complete a risk assessment before using a ladder in accordance with applicable legislation.

**3.** [P3] **Contractors** — Contractors may only use ladders under a Permit to Work procedure with a suitable risk assessment.

**4.** [P4] **Inspection on delivery** — Inspect the ladder after delivery and before first use to confirm the condition and operation of all parts.

**5.** [P5] **Daily visual check** — On each day the ladder is to be used, visually verify it is undamaged and safe.

**6.** [P6] **Monthly inspection** — Professional users must carry out regular periodic inspections, recorded monthly in the Ladder Control Form.

**7.** [P7] **Defect management** — If a defect is found, immediately withdraw the ladder from service and attach a notice indicating the dangerous condition. If repair is not practicable, cut or break the ladder before disposal. Record in the Ladder Control Form.

**8.** [P8] **Suitability** — Confirm the ladder is appropriate for the task. Do not use a damaged ladder. Remove any contamination (wet paint, mud, oil, snow) before use.

**9.** [P9] **Transport** — When transporting ladders on roof bars or in a truck, secure them to prevent damage.

---

#### Section B — Positioning and Erecting

**10.** [P10] **Correct angle** — Erect at the correct position: leaning ladder at approximately 1:4 inclination angle with rungs level; standing ladder fully opened. Ladder must stand on its feet, not on rungs or steps.

**11.** [P11] **Extend above landing** — The ladder must rise at least 1 metre above the landing point unless an equivalent handhold is available. Extending ladders require a minimum overlap of three rungs.

**12.** [P12] **Locking devices** — If locking devices are fitted, fully secure them before use.

**13.** [P13] **Stable base** — Ladders must stand on an even, level, and unmoveable surface. Do not place on slippery surfaces (ice, shiny or significantly contaminated surfaces) unless effective additional measures prevent slipping.

**14.** [P14] **Securing the ladder** — Leaning ladders must lean against a flat, non-fragile surface and be secured before use (tied or using a suitable device). Secure ladders against slipping by tying at the top; alternatively, secure at the sides or front.

**15.** [P15] **Footing** — Secure the base using cleats fixed to the floor, sandbags, or stakes embedded in the ground. A second person footing the base is effective only for ladders under 6 metres. Where a ladder requires footing, it is always a two-person task.

**16.** [P16] **Height limits** — Ladders must not be used for heights above 9 metres. Above 3 metres, the ladder must be anchored/lashed or footed. Top lashing uses quick-release ladder ties secured to fixed eyebolts.

**17.** [P17] **Never reposition from above** — Do not reposition a ladder from the top.

**18.** [P18] **Collision risk** — When positioning, account for pedestrian, vehicle, and door collision risks. Secure doors (not fire exits) and windows where possible in the work area.

**19.** [P19] **Electrical hazards** — Identify electrical risks in the work area (overhead lines, exposed equipment). Use non-conductive ladders for any unavoidable work near live electrical sources.

**20.** [P20] **Exclusion zone** — Where a footed ladder is used for high work, or a step ladder is used in a high-traffic area, establish a safe barrier area around the base using signs or barriers.

---

#### Section C — Using the Ladder

**21.** [P21] **Ascending and descending** — Face the ladder when ascending and descending. Maintain a secure grip at all times.

**22.** [P22] **Both hands** — Use both hands when climbing or descending. Do not carry tools or materials by hand on the ladder. Use a tool belt or holster; raise or lower materials by rope if needed.

**23.** [P23] **Do not overreach** — Keep the belt buckle (navel) inside the stiles. Keep both feet on the same rung/step throughout the task.

**24.** [P24] **Hard hat** — Wear a hard hat when working at a height over 2 metres.

**25.** [P25] **Weight limit** — Do not exceed the maximum total load for the ladder type.

**26.** [P26] **Prohibited positions** —
   - Do not stand on the top three steps/rungs of a leaning ladder.
   - Do not stand on the top two steps/rungs of a standing ladder without a platform and hand/knee rail.
   - Do not stand on the top four steps/rungs of a standing ladder with an extending section at the top.

**27.** [P27] **Short-duration use only** — Use ladders only for light work of short duration. Do not remain on a ladder for extended periods without regular breaks (fatigue is a risk).

**28.** [P28] **Adverse conditions** — Do not use a ladder outdoors in strong wind or adverse weather. Take precautions to prevent children accessing ladders.

**29.** [P29] **Side loading** — Avoid excessive side loads (e.g., side-on drilling through brick or concrete).

**30.** [P30] **Maintain handhold** — Maintain a handhold while working from a ladder. If this is not possible, take additional safety precautions.

**31.** [P31] **No bridge use** — Do not use a ladder as a bridge.

**32.** [P32] **Footwear** — Wear suitable footwear when climbing.

**33.** [P33] **If overreach is felt** — If at any point the user feels they are about to overreach or the ladder moves, cease the task immediately, carefully descend, and reposition or select an alternative access method (scaffold, cherry picker).

**34.** [P34] **Access to higher levels** — Do not step off a leaning ladder at a higher level without additional security (tied off or suitable stability device). Do not use standing ladders for access to a different level.

**35.** [P35] **Mobile ladders with platform (EN131-7)** — Use only with activated brakes and stabilisers if available. Exercise additional caution in outdoor use due to wind.

---

#### Section D — Repair, Maintenance, and Storage

**36.** [P36] **Repairs** — Repairs and maintenance must be carried out by a competent person (trained by the manufacturer) in accordance with producer's instructions. For parts replacement, contact the producer or distributor.

**37.** [P37] **No painting** — Do not paint ladders — paint may obscure defects. Transparent varnish or linseed oil may be applied to wooden ladders.

**38.** [P38] **Storage** — Store ladders per producer instructions:
   - Wooden ladders: dry place; do not coat with opaque or vapour-tight paints.
   - Thermoplastic, thermosetting, or reinforced plastic ladders: out of direct sunlight.
   - Store horizontally where possible. Protect from sliding, crashing, and falling during transport from storage.
