### Purpose

Ensure all associates are protected from electrical accidents, including electric shocks and electrical fires, through safe practices and proper maintenance.

---

### Scope

All departments. All associates using any electrical equipment or working in areas with electrical installations on [the property].

---

### Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Training on electrical safety; oversight of procedures |
| [Technical Director] | Oversee periodic inspections of electrical installations; ensure only qualified electricians carry out electrical work |
| Department Heads | Ensure all electrical faults in their areas are reported immediately; enforce safe practices |
| All associates | Report all electrical faults and hazards immediately; never interfere with electrical supply or equipment |

---

### Procedure

#### Section A — Risks and General Rules

**1.** [P1] **Two main risks** — There are two main risks with electricity: electric shock (when electricity passes through the body it causes shock, burns, and can kill) and fire (electrical appliances start approximately 19% of all workplace fires).

**2.** [P2] **Report faults immediately** — All electrical faults and hazards must be reported immediately to your Head of Department.

---

#### Section B — Routine Checks

**3.** [P3] **Trailing cables** — Ensure that all exposed cables trailing across floors from sockets to equipment are properly secured with tape or cable covers.

**4.** [P4] **Damaged plugs** — Loose, cracked, or broken plugs must be taken out of use immediately and the damaged part replaced.

**5.** [P5] **Socket outlets** — Working areas must have sufficient socket outlets to avoid the use of multiple socket adaptors, extension leads, or trailing cables.

---

#### Section C — Periodic Inspections and Maintenance

**6.** [P6] **Annual inspections** — Electrical installations, grounding systems, and lightning conductors must be periodically inspected by an authorised firm once per year.

**7.** [P7] **Accumulators and transformers** — Accumulators and transformers must be periodically inspected every year under the conditions specified by the manufacturer.

**8.** [P8] **Regular checks** — Regular checks must be undertaken on all electrical equipment throughout [the property].

**9.** [P9] **Qualified electrician only** — All electrical equipment must be properly installed, serviced, and maintained by a qualified electrician.

---

#### Section D — Wet Environments

**10.** [P10] **Wet processes** — Great care must be taken when using pressure washers and steam cleaners. Sockets must not be sited where they can become wet. Where appropriate, watertight or splash-proof electrical equipment and sockets must be used. An RCD device (earth leakage circuit breaker) must be fitted to pressure washers and steam cleaners.

---

#### Section E — Equipment Isolation and Interference

**11.** [P11] **Individual isolation** — Each piece of equipment supplied through a permanent cable must have its own isolator or plug-and-socket arrangement so that it can be disconnected from the electrical supply for cleaning and repair.

**12.** [P12] **No interference** — Under no circumstances must the electrical supply or equipment be interfered with by non-authorised persons.

**13.** [P13] **Qualified engineer for faults** — Where faults exist, only a qualified electrical engineer may carry out electrical work. All work must be carried out in accordance with applicable local legislation.

---

#### Section F — Lockout / Tagout

**14.** [P14] **Lockout / Tagout procedure** — Provide locks, locking devices, and tags for the proper isolation of hazardous energy. Train authorised associates on the correct use of these devices. Where necessary, assign one associate to guard duty during isolation.
