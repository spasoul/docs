### Purpose

Ensure all associates know how to identify, address, and prevent slip and fall hazards across the property.

---

### Scope

All departments. All associates working or moving anywhere on [the property].

---

### Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Training on slip/fall prevention; ensure wet floor signs available throughout property |
| Department Heads | Reinforce procedures in high-risk areas (kitchen, service areas, bathrooms) |
| All associates | Own wet/slippery areas immediately; deploy signage; clean up hazards |

---

### Procedure

**1.** [P1] **Own the hazard** — Any time you encounter a wet or slippery floor, own that area until it is hazard-free. Placing a wet floor sign does not remove your responsibility to clean up the spill or hazard.

**2.** [P2] **New associates** — Associates in their first days of employment are especially at risk due to unfamiliarity with the property and its surroundings. Pay extra attention to floor surfaces.

**3.** [P3] **Wet floor signs** — Know where wet floor signs are located in your area. Place them so they are visible from all angles. Always deploy them when mopping.

**4.** [P4] **High-risk areas** — Kitchens, service areas, and bathrooms are especially prone to spills. Exercise heightened caution in these areas at all times.

**5.** [P5] **Stairs and ramps** — Stairs and ramps are more dangerous when wet. Place a wet floor sign at both the top and the bottom of any wet stair or ramp.

**6.** [P6] **External entrances** — Entrances adjoining outdoor areas are especially hazardous in wet or inclement weather. Exercise extra caution and deploy signage.

**7.** [P7] **Footwear** — Associates working in kitchens or other slip-prone areas must wear non-skid footwear. Anti-slip tape for standard shoes is available from [Laundry / designated department].
