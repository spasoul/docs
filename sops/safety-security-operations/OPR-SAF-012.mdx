### Purpose

Ensure all associates follow correct safety procedures when stocking shelves, to prevent injury to themselves and colleagues.

---

### Scope

All departments. All associates performing shelf stocking or retrieval tasks.

---

### Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Oversee shelf stocking safety; training |
| Department Heads | Ensure associates are trained before performing stocking tasks; enforce shelf organisation standards |
| All associates | Follow correct lifting and stocking technique at all times; request assistance when needed |

---

### Procedure

#### Section A — Lifting Technique: Traffic Light Method

**1.** [P1] **Traffic Light lifting method** — Always use the "Traffic Light" method when lifting, reaching, or retrieving items from shelves, and follow all manual handling safety rules. Get help if an item is too bulky or heavy to handle safely.

**2.** [P2] **Green zone** — Arms tucked in, close to the body. This is the safest lifting position.

**3.** [P3] **Yellow zone** — Arms approximately 9 inches (22 cm) away from the body. Exercise increased caution in this position.

**4.** [P4] **Red zone** — Arms fully extended. This position places maximum strain on the body. Avoid lifting heavy items from the red zone.

---

#### Section B — Shelf Organisation

**5.** [P5] **Three-tier shelf arrangement** — Organise a three-tier shelf as follows: light-weight items on the top shelf; heavy-weight items on the middle shelf; medium-weight items on the bottom shelf.

**6.** [P6] **Shelving with more than three tiers** — Apply the same weight-distribution principle: lightest items highest, heaviest items in the middle range, medium items at the lowest positions.

---

#### Section C — Shelf Safety

**7.** [P7] **Items secured** — Ensure all items are secured and not overhanging from shelves.

**8.** [P8] **Weight capacity** — Ensure the shelf can support the weight of the items placed on it, especially when the shelf is fully loaded.

**9.** [P9] **Sprinkler clearance** — Top shelf items must not be within 18 inches (46 cm) of any fire sprinkler head, as items blocking the spray area will restrict fire containment.

**10.** [P10] **No obstruction of safety equipment** — Shelves and items upon them must not block any firefighting equipment, electrical panels, or other electrical controls.

---

#### Section D — Stock Rotation and Assistance

**11.** [P11] **FIFO stock rotation** — Always keep old stock items at the front of the shelf and place new stock at the back.

**12.** [P12] **Request assistance** — Always ask your Supervisor for assistance if an item is too heavy for you to carry safely.
