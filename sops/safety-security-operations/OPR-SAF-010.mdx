### Purpose

Ensure the safety of associates working at night or alone, through appropriate controls for fatigue, violence risk, and lone working.

---

### Scope

All departments. All associates performing night work or lone working, and the supervisors and managers responsible for them.

---

### Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Oversee night and lone worker safety controls; ensure assessments are in place |
| [Security Director] | Receive weekly shift schedules; maintain Night Workers Status Checklist; oversee hourly check-ins |
| Department Heads | Assess each night worker role; send weekly schedules to [Security Director]; ensure controls are applied |
| All associates | Follow sign-in/out procedures; conduct hourly check-ins when required; report concerns |

---

### Procedure

#### Section A — Definition and Scope of Night Work

**1.** [P1] **Night work defined** — Night work applies to any associate working between 23:00 and 06:00, where more than half of their working time falls within these hours.

**2.** [P2] **Night work restriction — duration** — Night shifts must not exceed 7.5 hours. If it is necessary to exceed this limit, written approval must be obtained from the associate.

**3.** [P3] **Night work restriction — age** — Associates under 18 years of age must not be assigned to night work.

---

#### Section B — Fatigue Controls

**4.** [P4] **Pre-employment vetting** — Night worker suitability must be assessed during the pre-employment process.

**5.** [P5] **Supervisor checks** — Supervisors must conduct regular checks on night workers to monitor fatigue and wellbeing.

**6.** [P6] **Breaks and welfare** — Night workers must have access to adequate breaks and welfare facilities during their shift.

---

#### Section C — Violence and Security Controls

**7.** [P7] **Security systems** — Property security systems, including controlled access, must be operational during night hours.

**8.** [P8] **Video surveillance** — Video surveillance must be active and monitored during night hours.

**9.** [P9] **Conflict resolution training** — All associates working at night must receive adequate training in conflict resolution.

**10.** [P10] **Alarm and emergency training** — Associates must be trained on the use of alarms and security alerts, and on how to contact emergency services.

---

#### Section D — Lone Worker Controls

**11.** [P11] **Avoid prolonged lone working** — Associates must not, where practicable, work alone for extended periods.

**12.** [P12] **Regular call-in** — Lone workers and night workers without an on-site supervisor must call the security office hourly and provide their current location.

**13.** [P13] **Personal alarm** — Lone workers must carry a personal alarm.

**14.** [P14] **Communication device** — Lone workers must carry a mobile telephone or radio at all times.

**15.** [P15] **Management monitoring** — Management must conduct regular checks on lone workers during their shift.

**16.** [P16] **Sign in and out** — All night and lone workers must sign in at the start of their work and sign out on completion.

---

#### Section E — Hazardous Night Operations

**17.** [P17] **Hazardous operations defined** — The following night operations are considered hazardous and require additional controls: cleaning using dangerous chemicals or equipment, working in confined areas, working at height, ventilation duct cleaning, and clock rounds.

**18.** [P18] **Permit to Work** — A Permit to Work must be issued before commencing any hazardous night operation.

**19.** [P19] **Two-person rule** — Hazardous night operations must be carried out by a minimum of two persons.

**20.** [P20] **Communication provision** — Regular checks on communication provision must be maintained throughout hazardous night operations.

**21.** [P21] **Task modification** — Where a particularly hazardous night operation is identified, consider modifying the task in the interest of safety before proceeding.

---

#### Section F — Scheduling and Administration

**22.** [P22] **Consecutive night work limit** — Night work schedules must not exceed 2 consecutive weeks.

**23.** [P23] **Weekly schedule submission** — Department Managers must send the weekly shift schedules of night workers, and any changes to those schedules, to the [Security Director] by email before each week commences.

**24.** [P24] **Night Workers Status Checklist** — The [Security Director's office / designated administrator] creates a Night Workers Status Checklist based on the submitted shift schedule. This checklist is used to track and verify the safety and status of all night workers.

**25.** [P25] **Department Head assessment** — Each night worker role must be thoroughly assessed by the Department Head to identify hazards and ensure appropriate controls are in place.

**MEVZUAT note:** This SOP supports compliance with Law No. 6331 on Occupational Health and Safety (İş Sağlığı ve Güvenliği Kanunu), specifically Art. 4 (İşverenin genel yükümlülükleri — General Obligations), Art. 5 (Risklerden korunma ilkeleri — Risk Prevention Principles), Art. 17 (Çalışanların eğitimi — Worker Training), and Art. 19 (Çalışanların yükümlülükleri — Worker Obligations). Night and lone working arrangements are governed by general employer duty of care under Art. 4; no standalone sub-regulation exists — apply risk assessment per Art. 10.
