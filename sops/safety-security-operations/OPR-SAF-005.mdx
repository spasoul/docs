### Purpose

Ensure all work on or near roofs is conducted safely with appropriate authorization, equipment, and supervision.

---

### Scope

All departments. All associates, contractors, and supervisors accessing or working on any roof area.

---

### Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Engineering Department Head] / [Occupational Safety Manager] | Issue Permit to Work; ensure safe access/egress; maintain physical safety features |
| [Duty Manager] / Supervisor | Confirm pre-notification before roof access; authorize work |
| Department Heads | Ensure associates have completed Working at Height training |
| All associates and contractors | Follow this SOP; notify supervisor before access; work in pairs where practicable |

---

### Procedure

#### Section A — Authorization and Access Control

**1.** [P1] **Authorized access only** — Display prominent warning notices at all roof access points: *"Access to authorised persons only — Permit to Work required — Door to be kept locked."* Keep roof access doors locked at all times when not in active use.

**2.** [P2] **Permit to Work** — All roof work requires a Permit to Work. Contractors must also complete a method statement and risk assessment before commencement.

**3.** [P3] **Training requirement** — All persons accessing roofs must have completed Working at Height training and must be aware of their competency in relation to the tasks to be performed.

**4.** [P4] **Pre-notification** — Before accessing a roof, every person must inform their supervisor or [Duty Manager] of the location and expected duration of the work.

**5.** [P5] **Work in pairs** — Roof work must be carried out in pairs wherever practicable.

---

#### Section B — Physical Safety Requirements

**6.** [P6] **Access and egress** — Provide safe, properly erected and maintained access and egress to all roof areas.

**7.** [P7] **Guard rails and protection** — Provide adequate guard rails, toe boards, anchor points, safety lines/harnesses, crawl boards, ladders, or scaffolding as appropriate. Ensure all physical safety features and equipment are adequately inspected, secured, and capable of bearing the required load.

**8.** [P8] **Fragile materials** — Ensure access on or over fragile materials is guarded, suitably covered, and clearly marked with warning signs.

**9.** [P9] **Long-term vs short-term work** — For long-term roof work: use guard rails and scaffold. For short-term work: safety harness, wires, and anchor points are appropriate.

**10.** [P10] **Roofline walking** — Do not walk along the line of roof bolts above the purlins, or along the roof edge of a fragile roof or roof lights.

**11.** [P11] **Sloping and fragile roofs** — Use purpose-built ladders and crawl boards. Do not use homemade or improvised ladders or boards.

---

#### Section C — PPE and Tools

**12.** [P12] **PPE** — Wear hard hats and suitable clothing and footwear for the prevailing weather conditions.

**13.** [P13] **Tools** — Carry tools in a tool belt when accessing or working on the roof. Do not hold tools in hand while climbing. Do not drop materials or equipment from a roof; lower them in a controlled manner in suitable containers, or use an enclosed debris chute if access is via ladder or scaffold.

**14.** [P14] **Safety harness certification** — Safety harness equipment must be certified as safe at least annually.

**15.** [P15] **Anchor and cable load testing** — Anchorage points and cables must be load tested in accordance with manufacturer's instructions.

---

#### Section D — Environmental and Electrical Conditions

**16.** [P16] **Weather check** — Assess weather conditions before commencing work. Where practicable, work in daylight, dry, fine, low-wind conditions only. Suspend work during adverse weather that may affect safety.

**17.** [P17] **Power tools** — Battery-powered tools are preferred for roof work. Cables and extension leads should only be used if absolutely necessary.

**18.** [P18] **Lighting and emergency routes** — Maintain suitable and sufficient lighting, and clearly signed emergency exit routes on all roof areas.

**19.** [P19] **Ladders and scaffolding** — Where ladders or scaffolding are used for access, follow the relevant safe-use procedures (SOP-OPR-SAF-004 for ladders).
