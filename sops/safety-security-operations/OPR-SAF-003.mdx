## Purpose

Ensure all work at height is carried out safely, with appropriate risk assessment, equipment, and controls.

---

## Scope

All departments. All associates performing or supervising work at height including maintenance, cleaning, decorating, rigging, or any activity where a fall could cause injury.

---

## Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Engineering Department Head] / [Occupational Safety Manager] | Complete documented safety controls and risk assessments; authorize work at height |
| Department Heads | Ensure associates are competent before assigning height work |
| All associates | Follow this SOP; report concerns; refuse unsafe tasks without retaliation |

---

## Procedure

#### Section A — Authorization and Risk Assessment

**1.** [P1] **Risk assessment required** — A risk assessment must be completed before any working-at-height task commences. [Engineering Department] and [Occupational Safety Manager] complete and document safety controls.

**2.** [P2] **Examples of height tasks** — Tasks covered include but are not limited to:
   - Chandelier raising/lowering
   - Platform and audience seating setup
   - General maintenance and decorating
   - Lighting rigging and setup
   - Window cleaning
   - Access to shelving and high-level cleaning

**3.** [P3] **Hazard identification** — Principal hazards include: falls (most common cause of fatal or serious injury), falling objects, and falls from collapsing structures.

**4.** [P4] **Competency check — the person** — Before assigning height work, verify:
   - Age and fitness to work at height
   - No fear of heights — no associate may be forced to work at height
   - Training is completed and documented
   - Work equipment provided is fit for purpose
   If competency, confidence, or equipment is in doubt, contract the task to a competent external company or individual.

**5.** [P5] **Permit to Work** — All precautionary measures must be referenced in a Permit to Work before height work commences. For specialist equipment, clear safety information, training, and registration of the task are required.

---

#### Section B — Equipment Selection

**6.** [P6] **Appropriate equipment** — Select equipment suited to the task and environment:
   - Zip-up scaffold / fixed scaffold / lifting platform
   - Telescopic Work Platform
   - Scissor lifts
   - Ladders (short-duration light work only — see SOP-OPR-SAF-004)
   - Towers
   Associates must not use equipment they are unsure how to operate. Consult the manufacturer for guidance.

**7.** [P7] **Environmental factors** — Account for:
   - Indoor height restrictions or obstacles
   - Proximity to roads or power cables
   - Weather, temperature, and lighting conditions
   - Stability and evenness of ground
   - Need for additional supports

---

#### Section C — Protection from Falls

**8.** [P8] **Edge protection** — In accordance with local legislation: at heights over 1.2 m, 110 mm handrails must be in place. 150 mm toe boards and intermediate handrails are required.

**9.** [P9] **Safe working area** — Do not leave equipment lying around the work area. Maintain a safe working distance from the edge. Where this is not possible, use a safety harness.

**10.** [P10] **PPE — fall arrest** — Personal protective equipment (harness, rope lines) must carry safety loading certification where applicable. PPE is the last line of protection, not a substitute for safe working practices.

---

#### Section D — Protection from Falling Objects

**11.** [P11] **Exclusion zones** — Establish safety exclusion areas around the work activity. Restrict pedestrian access for the public and associates not involved in the task.

**12.** [P12] **Signage and barriers** — Display appropriate signage and rope off affected areas.

**13.** [P13] **Object security** — Do not climb carrying large or awkward objects. Secure all objects to the structure (e.g., lash scaffolding boards). All associates and any passers-by below must wear hard hats. This applies equally to temporary stage or seating arrangements.

---

#### Section E — Structures and Equipment Integrity

**14.** [P14] **Structure design** — Any structure built to support people, or where collapse would endanger people, must be designed and built by competent persons. A competent person inspects the structure after completion and before use, and attaches an inspection note.

**15.** [P15] **Equipment inspection** — Where collapse or equipment failure at height is a serious risk, management must ensure equipment has been subject to routine insurance inspection and is inspected by a competent person prior to use.

---

#### Section F — Eyebolts

**16.** [P16] **Eyebolt inspection** — Where eyebolts are provided to support window cleaners or maintenance contractors, they must be maintained in safe and reliable condition. All eyebolts must be inspected before use by the user.

**17.** [P17] **Load testing** — All eyebolts and anchorage points must be load tested by a professional contractor in accordance with manufacturer's instructions and applicable legislative requirements. Evidence of testing must be kept on site by [Engineering Department] for inspection.
