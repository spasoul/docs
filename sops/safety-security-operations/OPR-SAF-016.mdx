## Purpose

Prevent associates from coming into contact with pathogens through correct infection control practices in the workplace.

---

## Scope

All departments. All associates working on [the property].

---

## Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] / [Food and Occupational Safety Manager] | Oversee infection control procedures; investigate all occupational exposure incidents |
| Department Heads | Reinforce infection control practices in their areas; ensure cleaning procedures are followed |
| All associates | Follow all personal hygiene, food preparation, and workplace cleanliness procedures at all times |

---

## Procedure

#### Section A — Core Principle

**1.** [P1] **Assume everyone is potentially infectious** — The basis of good infection control is to treat every person as potentially infectious. Proper procedures must be followed at all times. Infection may be spread by a person during the incubation period — before symptoms appear — without the infected person being aware.

**2.** [P2] **Transmission routes** — Infectious agents can be spread by: breathing in airborne germs from coughs or sneezes; touching contaminated objects, food, or surfaces; skin-to-skin contact or sharing personal items; contact with body fluids (saliva, urine, faeces, blood) via cuts, abrasions, or mucus membranes of the mouth and eyes.

---

#### Section B — Personal Hygiene

**3.** [P3] **Handwashing** — Thoroughly wash hands with water and soap for at least 15 seconds after visiting the toilet, before preparing food, and after touching clients or equipment. Dry hands with disposable paper towels.

**4.** [P4] **Cover cuts** — Cover any cuts or abrasions with a waterproof dressing. Intact and healthy skin is a major barrier to pathogens.

**5.** [P5] **Gloves** — Wear gloves when handling body fluids or equipment containing body fluids, when touching another person's broken skin or mucus membranes, or when performing any invasive procedure. Wash hands between clients. Use fresh gloves for each client where necessary.

**6.** [P6] **Personal items** — Do not share towels, clothing, razors, toothbrushes, shavers, or other personal items.

---

#### Section C — Food Preparation Hygiene

**7.** [P7] **Wash hands** — Wash hands before and after handling food.

**8.** [P8] **Do not touch face** — Avoid touching hair, nose, or mouth during food preparation.

**9.** [P9] **Temperature control** — Keep hot food hot and cold food cold.

**10.** [P10] **Separate handling** — Use separate storage, utensils, and preparation surfaces for cooked and uncooked foods.

**11.** [P11] **Clean utensils and surfaces** — Wash all utensils and preparation surfaces thoroughly according to Cleaning Instructions after use.

---

#### Section D — Workplace Cleanliness

**12.** [P12] **Regular surface cleaning** — Regularly wash floors, bathrooms, and surfaces (such as tables and bench tops) according to Cleaning Instructions.

**13.** [P13] **Walls and ceilings** — Periodically wash walls and ceilings.

**14.** [P14] **Mops, brushes, and cloths** — Thoroughly wash and dry mops, brushes, and cloths after every use. Drying is especially important: many pathogens rely on moisture to thrive.

**15.** [P15] **Body fluid spills** — Use disinfectants to clean up blood and other body fluid spills. Always wear gloves, clean the surface before applying disinfectant, and follow the manufacturer's instructions exactly.

**16.** [P16] **Spot cleaning** — Spot clean whenever necessary.

---

#### Section E — Contaminated Sharps

**17.** [P17] **Never recap needles** — Never attempt to re-cap or bend used needles.

**18.** [P18] **Handle by the barrel** — Handle contaminated sharps by the barrel only.

**19.** [P19] **Sharps disposal container** — Place contaminated sharps in an appropriate puncture-proof container — yellow, labelled "Danger — Contaminated Sharps", and marked with a black biohazard symbol.

---

#### Section F — Occupational Exposure

**20.** [P20] **Flush** — If you come into contact with blood or body fluids, immediately flush the affected area with running water.

**21.** [P21] **Wash** — Wash the area with plenty of warm water and soap.

**22.** [P22] **Seek medical advice** — Seek medical advice immediately after any occupational exposure to blood or body fluids.

**23.** [P23] **Report and investigation** — [The Food and Occupational Safety Manager] must investigate all incidents involving contact with blood or body fluids and take action to prevent recurrence.

---

#### Section G — Additional Controls

**24.** [P24] **Emergency Situation Bag** — Keep gloves and a CPR mask in the Emergency Situation Bag at all times.

**25.** [P25] **Eye protection** — Wear goggles or other effective eye protection whenever there is a risk of blood or body fluid splashing into the eyes.

**26.** [P26] **Inform clients** — Inform clients with potentially infectious health conditions about ways to prevent transmission while in close quarters.
