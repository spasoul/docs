### Purpose

Regulate duties, authority, responsibilities, rights, and obligations of employer and employees to ensure occupational health and safety at the workplace and to improve existing health and safety conditions, in accordance with Law No. 6331 on Occupational Health and Safety.

---

### Scope

All departments. All employees, contractors, and temporary workers.

---

### Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [General Manager] / [Employer Representative] | Chair the Occupational Safety Committee; final authority |
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Day-to-day safety oversight, inspections, risk analyses, training coordination |
| [Occupational Physician] | Health surveillance, medical clearances, co-sign Annual Plan |
| Department Heads | Deliver role-specific safety training; issue and record PPE; sign Job Safety Analyses |
| All employees | Complete required training; report incidents; use PPE as issued |

---

### Procedure

#### Section A — Occupational Safety Committee

**1.** [P1] **Committee composition** — Constitute an Occupational Safety Committee with at minimum the following members:
   - [Employer] or [Employer Representative] — Committee Leader
   - [Legal Occupational Safety Specialist]
   - [Occupational Physician]
   - [Human Resources Representative]
   - [Security Representative]
   - [Engineering Department Representative]
   - [Employee Representative]

**2.** [P2] **Meeting schedule** — The Committee meets quarterly. Notify all members of meeting agenda, date, time, and location at least 48 hours in advance. Emergency meetings may be called without notice in the event of a serious accident or situation requiring immediate action.

**3.** [P3] **Decisions** — Decisions are made by majority vote. On tie, the Leader decides. Maintain an Official Report for every meeting; all Committee members sign it. Distribute decisions to members; communicate relevant findings to all employees.

---

#### Section B — Training

**4.** [P4] **Minimum training** — Every employee receives occupational safety training of at least 4 hours delivered by the [Occupational Safety Manager] and 4 hours by the [Occupational Physician] within every three-year period. Training topics include:
   - Committee duties and authorities
   - National legislation and OHS standards
   - Common accident causes and hazardous incident patterns
   - Industrial hygiene fundamentals
   - Effective communication in safety contexts
   - Emergency situations and procedures
   - Occupational diseases
   - Workplace-specific risks

**5.** [P5] **Trigger-based retraining** — Provide safety training upon:
   - New hire onboarding
   - Role transfer or job change
   - Change of equipment or introduction of new technology
   - Return to work after 6+ months' absence (refresher required before resuming duties)
   - Following an occupational accident or disease (additional training on causes and prevention)

**6.** [P6] **Hazardous and very hazardous roles** — Employees working in roles classified as hazardous or very hazardous must hold documented vocational training certification before commencing work. This applies to permanent employees and workers from outside undertakings.

---

#### Section C — Employee Representative

**7.** [P7] **Representative count** — Elect employee representatives according to workforce size:
   - 2–50 employees → 1 representative
   - 51–100 employees → 2 representatives
   - 101–500 employees → 3 representatives

**8.** [P8] **Election process** — Announce candidacy collection 7 days before the election date. At least 50% of workforce must participate for the election to be valid. Select by majority vote; on tie, Leader decides based on qualifications (education, tenure, experience). Terms are 5 years. If a representative vacates the role, the next-highest vote-getter assumes the position.

**9.** [P9] **Documentation** — Keep an Official Report of the election signed by the Employer Representative and the presiding employee. File with the employee list bearing signatures.

---

#### Section D — Incident Reporting

**10.** [P10] **Internal incident report** — The injured employee's supervisor must complete a Supervisor Accident Investigation Report (SAIR) with the employee within 48 hours of the reported injury or illness (or as soon as reasonably possible).
   - Section 1 may be completed by any employee authorized to document work-related injuries.
   - Section 2 is completed by the supervisor to identify cause(s) and determine preventive actions.
   - Employees will not be disciplined for delayed reporting of an injury or illness.
   - [The property] prohibits retaliation against any employee who reports a work-related injury or illness.

**11.** [P11] **Monthly safety inspections** — [Occupational Safety Manager] conducts monthly property inspections. All findings and recommended corrective actions are recorded in the Findings and Recommendations log. The log must be co-signed by [Employer] and [Occupational Physician].

---

#### Section E — Emergency and Crisis Plan

**12.** [P12] **Plan ownership** — Emergency and Crisis Plan is prepared jointly by [Security Director] and [Occupational Safety Manager]. Every page is signed by the [Occupational Safety Manager].

**13.** [P13] **Emergency team ratios** — Constitute and maintain the following minimum emergency teams:
   - First Aid Team: minimum 1 member per 20 employees
   - Fire Response Team: minimum 1 member per 50 employees
   - Search Team: minimum 1 member per 20 employees
   - Rescue Team: minimum 1 member per 50 employees
   - Each team has one designated Leader.

**14.** [P14] **Emergency drill** — Conduct a full emergency drill at least once per year in compliance with national legal requirements. Retain the drill report with full detail and all improvement actions documented.

---

#### Section F — Risk Analysis and Job Safety Analysis

**15.** [P15] **Job Safety Analysis (JSA)** — Department Trainers or Heads conduct JSA for their areas with the support of the [Occupational Safety Manager]. Every employee is trained on their JSA by the Department Trainer or Head and signs the JSA Training Form. Copies go to HR, Security, and the [Occupational Safety Manager].

**16.** [P16] **Risk analysis** — [Occupational Safety Manager] prepares the property-wide Risk Analysis. Every page is signed by all Occupational Safety Committee members and all Department Heads.

---

#### Section G — Health Surveillance

**17.** [P17] **Health checks** — [Occupational Physician] conducts health surveillance including assessment upon return to work following repeated absences due to occupational accidents, occupational diseases, or health conditions (on request).

---

#### Section H — Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

**18.** [P18] **PPE issuance** — Department Head issues PPE using a PPE Delivery Form. The receiving employee signs the form. Signed forms are retained in the Employee File held in HR.

---

#### Section I — Annual Evaluation and Annual Training Plan

**19.** [P19] **Annual plan** — [Occupational Safety Manager] prepares and presents an Annual Evaluation and Annual Training Plan. The plan must be signed by: [Employer Representative], [Legal Occupational Safety Specialist], [Occupational Physician], and [HR Director].

---

#### Section J — Permit-Required Works

**20.** [P20] **Hot work** — Any flaming or sparking activity capable of causing fire or explosion (e.g., welding, cutting) requires a Hot Work Permit before commencement. Verify permit is issued and conditions met before allowing work to start.

**21.** [P21] **Confined space** — Operations in enclosed spaces (tanks, boilers, reactors, or areas with limited ventilation and restricted access) require a Confined Space Permit. Do not enter without permit and appropriate PPE.
