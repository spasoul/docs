### Purpose

Ensure all associates are protected from chemical accidents by applying correct storage, handling, PPE, and disposal practices for all hazardous substances on [the property].

---

### Scope

All departments. All associates who handle, store, or work in proximity to chemicals or hazardous substances.

---

### Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] / [Food and Occupational Safety Manager] | Oversee COSHH compliance; ensure assessments are kept and training is delivered |
| Department Heads | Maintain COSHH assessments and SDS for their area; ensure associates are trained; control access to chemical stores |
| All associates | Follow all chemical handling, storage, and PPE procedures; report spillages immediately |

---

### Procedure

#### Section A — Understanding Chemical Hazards

**1.** [P1] **What is a hazardous substance** — [The property] uses substances and chemicals that may be hazardous to health if used without proper precautions. Any substance with the potential to harm a person is a hazardous substance. Accidents with some hazardous substances can cause permanent damage or death.

**2.** [P2] **Chemical hazard examples** — Chemical hazards include: direct skin contact causing burns or skin rashes; splashes or spills damaging the eyes; inhalation of volatile chemicals (e.g. solvents); accumulation of high concentrations of vapour or gas, particularly in enclosed spaces.

---

#### Section B — Storage

**3.** [P3] **Storage location** — Store chemicals in a locked, well-ventilated area. Store below waist height wherever possible to reduce the risk of spillage onto head and eye areas.

**4.** [P4] **Pallets and bunding** — Chemicals must be stored on a pallet and bunded appropriately.

**5.** [P5] **Signage** — Chemical stores must be clearly indicated with the relevant hazard signage.

**6.** [P6] **PPE at store** — Appropriate PPE must be available wherever chemicals are stored and used. PPE must be stored outside the chemical store and must be worn before entering.

**7.** [P7] **Safety Data Sheets on display** — Safety Data Sheets (SDS) must be clearly displayed and visible where chemicals are stored and decanted.

**8.** [P8] **Restricted access** — Access to chemicals must be restricted to key associates who use them in their daily activities.

**9.** [P9] **Never mix chemicals** — Different chemicals must never be mixed together. Empty containers must never be reused to store different or decanted chemicals.

**10.** [P10] **Labelling** — All chemical containers must be clearly labelled with the identity of the chemical. Do not use chemicals from unlabelled containers or unmarked cylinders.

**11.** [P11] **Minimum quantities** — Every effort must be made to keep quantities stored to a minimum, in accordance with daily needs and risk.

**12.** [P12] **Do not drag flammable containers** — Never drag containers labelled "Flammable".

---

#### Section C — Handling

**13.** [P13] **Use for specified purpose only** — Chemicals must only be used for their specified job as directed by the product SDS. Use only products supplied by [the Company] that are covered by Safety Data Sheets.

**14.** [P14] **No eating, drinking, or smoking** — Never eat, drink, or smoke when handling chemicals. Always wash hands directly after use and before putting hands near your mouth.

**15.** [P15] **Spraying technique** — When spraying tile or window cleaner, hold the spray bottle at arm's length and direct the spray away from your body.

**16.** [P16] **Glove hygiene** — Each time you use gloves, wash them before removing them using cold tap water with a normal handwashing motion. Always wash your hands after removing the gloves.

**17.** [P17] **No food or drink containers for chemicals** — Food and drink containers or vessels must never be used to store chemicals.

---

#### Section D — Spillages and Disposal

**18.** [P18] **Spillage response** — Spillages must be reported immediately. Contain the spill and refer to the relevant SDS for the correct method of clearance.

**19.** [P19] **Disposal** — Chemicals must be safely disposed of in accordance with the SDS. Empty chemical containers must be delivered to [the Dangerous Waste Material Responsible].

**20.** [P20] **Hospital transfer** — If a person is taken to hospital due to exposure to a hazardous substance, the relevant SDS must be given to the person accompanying them.

---

#### Section E — Assessment and Training

**21.** [P21] **COSHH assessments** — The SDS is a tool for completing chemical assessments; it is not a substitute for the assessment itself. Substances must be identified by their commercial name and active ingredients. A copy of all COSHH assessments and SDS sheets relevant to the department must be kept by the Head of Department for easy associate access.

**22.** [P22] **Substance list** — A list of substances and chemicals currently in use and their storage locations is available from your Head of Department.

**23.** [P23] **Supplier training** — Training offered by suppliers must be arranged and undertaken in accordance with the requirements of the Statutory Training Matrix.

**24.** [P24] **6-monthly training** — Associates who handle or use chemicals on a regular basis must receive chemical safety training on a 6-monthly basis.

---

#### Section F — Eyewash Stations

**25.** [P25] **Eyewash provision** — An eyewash station or sink must be located in the direct vicinity of all chemical stores and decanting areas.

**26.** [P26] **Monthly inspection** — Eyewash stations are checked monthly using the Eyewash Checklist. Technical area eyewash units are checked by [the Engineering team]; eyewash stations in other areas are checked by [the Workplace Nurse].
