## Purpose

Ensure safe, authorized, and responsible operation of buggies (electric vehicles) across [the property].

---

## Scope

All departments using buggies. All drivers, passengers, and supervisors responsible for vehicle authorization.

---

## Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Department Manager] | Authorize and issue buggies to qualified drivers; ensure disclaimer form is signed |
| [Security Director] / [Occupational Safety Manager] | Authority to immediately prohibit any individual from operating or riding a buggy |
| [Technical Department] | Maintain vehicles; manage maintenance records |
| [Guest Services Team] | Daily cleaning of buggies |
| Driver | Pre-use checks; safe operation; reporting defects; charging compliance |

---

## Procedure

#### Section A — Authorization

**1.** [P1] **Driver eligibility** — Buggies may only be operated by employees who hold a valid driver's license and have passed both a written test and a hands-on evaluation. Use by any other person — including guests — is prohibited.

**2.** [P2] **Issuance** — The department manager issues the buggy to the driver by formal handover (zimmet). The driver signs a disclaimer form before taking possession.

**3.** [P3] **Prohibition authority** — [Security Director], [Occupational Safety Manager], or their appointed nominee has authority to immediately prohibit any individual from operating or riding a buggy.

---

#### Section B — Pre-Use Checks

**4.** [P4] **Pre-use inspection** — Before each use, check:
   - Battery charge level (if below 20%, connect to charger; do not operate)
   - Tyre air pressure
   - General vehicle condition (body, controls, lighting)
   - Forward/reverse switch and gear position — set to 'N' (neutral) before starting

---

#### Section C — Operating Rules

**5.** [P5] **Pedestrians** — Afford pedestrians the right-of-way at all times. Operate with utmost courtesy, care, and consideration for pedestrians and other users.

**6.** [P6] **Speed and caution** — Maintain a safe speed at all times. Do not overtake other vehicles. Exercise extreme caution on steep, loose, or poor terrain, in wet weather, and when approaching crossings, sharp turns, or areas open to the public or other vehicles. Reduce speed in wet weather.

**7.** [P7] **Downhill and inclines** — Maintain a safe speed when descending hills or any incline. Use the brake to reduce speed. Be aware that sudden stops or direction changes may cause loss of control.

**8.** [P8] **Turns and roundabouts** — At roundabouts, stay as far right as possible and check for oncoming vehicles. Stop at blind intersections before proceeding.

**9.** [P9] **Parking and traffic flow** — Operate and park buggies so as not to impede pedestrian or vehicular flow on roadways, ramps, or pavements. Do not use roadways unless expressly permitted. When parking: engage the parking brake, set ignition to 'off', and leave gear in neutral.

**10.** [P10] **Property boundary** — Do not operate the buggy outside [the property] boundaries.

**11.** [P11] **Lightning** — Do not operate a buggy where there is a risk of lightning. If lightning occurs during operation, cease use immediately.

**12.** [P12] **Impairment** — Persons under the influence of drugs or alcohol must not operate a buggy.

**13.** [P13] **Vehicle limitations** — The sun top does not protect against roll-over or flying objects. The windscreen does not protect against flying objects including tree branches. Refer to the "Safety and Operational" instructions posted on the buggy windscreen, which form part of these safety standards.

**14.** [P14] **Turn restrictions** — Observe posted turn restriction rules on property (refer to [property directional signage]).

---

#### Section D — Movement Rules

**15.** [P15] **Starting** — Press the accelerator pedal slowly to move. On a slope, keep one foot on the brake while starting.

**16.** [P16] **Pedal use** — Use one foot for both accelerator and brake pedals.

**17.** [P17] **No gear change in motion** — Do not change the forward/reverse switch (gear) while the vehicle is moving.

**18.** [P18] **No sudden manoeuvres** — Do not make sudden manoeuvres at start, during driving, or when stopping.

**19.** [P19] **Reversing** — Before reversing, check that the area behind the vehicle is clear before moving.

---

#### Section E — Passengers and Load

**20.** [P20] **Seating** — Ensure all passengers are seated before moving. Passengers must remain seated and keep their entire body within the vehicle at all times while in motion. There are no seat belts; all occupants must hold the provided handles.

**21.** [P21] **Boarding** — Do not attempt to board a buggy after it has begun moving.

**22.** [P22] **Capacity** — Maximum 2 persons per seat. Do not exceed the buggy's total passenger or weight capacity.

**23.** [P23] **Cargo** — Do not carry objects outside the vehicle's cargo area. Secure all transported materials so they cannot fall during transit.

**24.** [P24] **Children** — Children must not board a buggy without a guardian. Do not leave children alone in the vehicle. Ensure children's safety throughout the journey. On long vehicles, children must not be seated in the rearmost position (safety risk).

---

#### Section F — Maintenance

**25.** [P25] **Driver responsibility** — The driver is responsible for the vehicle's care. If any abnormal noise, malfunction, or defect is detected, inform the [Technical Department] immediately and remove the vehicle from service.

**26.** [P26] **Maintenance mode** — During maintenance, verify the vehicle's Tow-Run or Maintenance-Operate button is in the correct position before commencing.

**27.** [P27] **Maintenance records** — [Technical Department] is responsible for maintaining vehicle maintenance records.

---

#### Section G — Charging

**28.** [P28] **Charge monitoring** — Monitor the battery charge indicator and warning lights on the panel. If charge falls below 20%, connect the charger and remove the vehicle from service.

**29.** [P29] **Charging rules** —
   - Use only the manufacturer-approved charger.
   - Do not use extension or supplementary cables.
   - Do not charge more than one vehicle on the same outlet.
   - Do not disconnect the vehicle before the charge is complete.
   - Do not move the vehicle while charging.
   - Verify the charging cable is intact and undamaged before connecting.

---

#### Section H — Cleaning

**30.** [P30] **Daily cleaning** — [Bell Team] cleans buggies daily in the garage area before returning them to the main entrance. Do not wash buggies with water; clean using a mop and towels only.
