### Purpose

Ensure all associates conduct office work in a safe manner and are protected from common office-related hazards.

---

### Scope

All departments. All associates performing work in office environments.

---

### Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Training on office safety hazards; oversight of workstation compliance |
| Department Heads | Ensure office areas comply with safety standards; reinforce procedures with their teams |
| All associates | Apply all office safety practices at all times |

---

### Procedure

**1.** [P1] **Keep floors and walkways clear** — Floors and walkways must be kept clear of boxes, files, and other stationery at all times.

**2.** [P2] **Access to high shelving** — Access to shelving and other high items must only be undertaken using the footstool or steps provided for that purpose. Do not use chairs or improvised platforms.

**3.** [P3] **Trailing cables** — All trailing electrical cables must be adequately covered to prevent trip hazards.

**4.** [P4] **Electrical equipment** — Do not interfere with electrical equipment or remove panels, particularly on photocopiers. Report faults to your Head of Department.

**5.** [P5] **Safe door** — Take care with the use of the safe. Keep the safe door closed when not in use to minimise the trip hazard it presents.

**6.** [P6] **Chemicals and substances** — Take care with office chemicals and substances, including toner, correction fluid (Tipp-Ex), and thinners. Follow label instructions and ensure adequate ventilation.

**7.** [P7] **Manual handling** — Keep manual handling risks to a minimum. When moving heavy loads — such as boxes of paper — seek assistance rather than attempting to lift alone.

**8.** [P8] **Filing cabinet loading** — Do not overfill filing cabinets. Open only one drawer at a time to prevent the cabinet from overbalancing.

**9.** [P9] **Close drawers when finished** — Close drawers when finished with a task. Do not leave a drawer open and unattended, as colleagues may walk into it and be injured.

**10.** [P10] **Paper handling** — Handle filed materials carefully and without rushing to prevent paper-cut lacerations.

**11.** [P11] **Filing cabinet drawer sequence** — If working in a lower file drawer, do not allow another person to open the drawer above you. This prevents the cabinet from tipping over and prevents head injury.

**12.** [P12] **Cabinet doors** — Close cabinet doors after accessing them to avoid personal injury from open doors.

**13.** [P13] **Equipment instructions and JSA** — Read the Equipment Using Instructions or Job Safety Analysis (JSA) forms before using any office equipment, including box knives and paper cutters.
