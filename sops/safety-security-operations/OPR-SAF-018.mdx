## Purpose

Ensure associates are protected from work-related upper limb disorders and musculoskeletal injuries caused by prolonged improper sitting, standing, or incorrect posture.

---

## Scope

All departments. All associates performing seated desk work or prolonged standing work.

---

## Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Training on ergonomic posture; workstation safety oversight |
| Department Heads | Ensure workstations support correct posture; encourage regular breaks |
| All associates | Apply correct posture at all times; take breaks; seek advice if experiencing discomfort |

---

## Procedure

#### Section A — Seated Work

**1.** [P1] **Change positions** — Change positions periodically. Organise tasks to allow variety, with frequent breaks, to promote circulation and reduce muscle fatigue.

**2.** [P2] **Seat height** — Adjust seat height so that feet are flat on the ground or on a solid surface such as a footrest, to support the lower back and promote an upright posture.

**3.** [P3] **Lumbar support** — If the chair lacks adequate lumbar support, use a cushion to improve lower back support.

**4.** [P4] **Arm rests** — Adjust arm rests to allow the shoulders to remain relaxed — not hunched or raised when resting on them.

**5.** [P5] **Backrest angle** — Recline the backrest to an angle of 100–110 degrees periodically to support the weight of the torso.

**6.** [P6] **Seat pan** — Use a chair with a seat pan that does not press behind the knees, even when tilted, to maintain healthy circulation.

**7.** [P7] **Seated posture** — When seated, keep the head and neck upright, facing forward. Keep shoulders and arms relaxed, close to the body, and perpendicular to the floor. Keep forearms, wrists, and hands in a straight line. Avoid bending wrists while typing.

**8.** [P8] **Regular breaks** — Take regular breaks when working in a seated position for extended periods.

**9.** [P9] **Medical conditions** — If you have a condition such as a herniated disc or back injury, seek medical advice before performing ergonomic exercises. If you experience pain, discomfort, numbness, tingling, clumsiness, or loss of strength or flexibility, stop and contact a health professional.

---

#### Section B — Standing Work

**10.** [P10] **Supportive footwear** — Wear supportive, cushion-soled footwear when performing standing work.

**11.** [P11] **Shift weight** — Periodically shift weight from one foot to the other to prevent undue stress on one foot.

**12.** [P12] **Knees slightly bent** — Keep knees slightly bent to prevent circulation problems caused by locked knees.

**13.** [P13] **Stand straight** — Stand straight and tall, with ears over shoulders over hips. Tuck the hips under to help straighten the spine and prevent excessive spinal wear.

**14.** [P14] **Foot rest** — Use a small sturdy box (approximately 6 inches / 15 cm tall) to rest one foot at a time, alternating feet, to reduce foot fatigue during prolonged standing.
