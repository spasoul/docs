## Purpose

Ensure all window cleaning operations are conducted safely, with appropriate contractor vetting, access controls, and electrical isolation.

---

## Scope

All departments. All associates and contractors involved in window cleaning operations.

---

## Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Engineering Department Head] / [Occupational Safety Manager] | Assess access methods; issue Permit to Work; exchange safety policies with contractors |
| [Management] | Vet and select contractors on competency; supervise operations |
| All associates | Be aware of active cleaning operations; follow electrical isolation protocols |

---

## Procedure

#### Section A — Assessment and Contractor Management

**1.** [P1] **Access assessment** — Where providing safe access involves significant difficulties, assess whether the benefit of having windows cleaned justifies the risk. Do not proceed if safe access cannot be achieved.

**2.** [P2] **Contractor selection** — Select contractors on the basis of competency, not cost alone. A Permit to Work must be obtained before work commences.

**3.** [P3] **Pre-work coordination** — Management must meet with contractors before operations begin to agree on safe systems of work for the property. This must include an exchange of safety policies, risk assessments, and emergency procedures.

**4.** [P4] **Contractor sign-in** — All contractors must sign in at the designated point where a Permit to Work is issued by a competent person before commencing.

**5.** [P5] **Staff awareness** — All associates must be made aware of any window cleaning operation in progress in their area.

**6.** [P6] **Unsafe practices** — All unsafe practices must be prohibited immediately. Any contractor who fails to follow procedures will have their contract cancelled.

---

#### Section B — Weather and Work Suspension

**7.** [P7] **Adverse weather** — Suspend work at height during any period of adverse weather that may affect safety.

---

#### Section C — Electrical Isolation

**8.** [P8] **Electrical isolation** — All electrical systems accessible to cleaners — including lights and fans — must be switched off while cleaning is in progress.

---

#### Section D — Cleaning from Inside

**9.** [P9] **No fall risk** — Ensure there is no danger of a fall through an open window before commencing inside cleaning.

**10.** [P10] **Pivoted windows** — Horizontally and vertically pivoted windows must have adequate locking or automatic devices to fix them in the reversed cleaning position.

**11.** [P11] **Maximum reach limits** — The maximum safe reach through an open window is: 560 mm sideways, 510 mm upwards, 610 mm downwards.

**12.** [P12] **Above ground floor** — Side-hung, pivoted, or sliding windows above the ground floor must be limited to 100 mm opening during cleaning.

**13.** [P13] **Childproof catches** — Safety bolts/catches and opening/closing mechanisms must be childproof.

---

#### Section E — Eyebolts

**14.** [P14] **Eyebolt inspection** — Where eyebolts are provided to support window cleaners or maintenance contractors, they must be maintained in a safe and reliable condition. All eyebolts must be inspected by the user before each use.

**15.** [P15] **Load testing** — All eyebolts and anchorage points must be load tested by a professional contractor in accordance with manufacturer's instructions and applicable legislative requirements. Evidence of testing must be kept on site by [Engineering Department] for inspection.
