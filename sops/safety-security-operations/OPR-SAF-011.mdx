### Purpose

Ensure all associates follow correct procedure for lifting heavy items in order to prevent injury.

---

### Scope

All departments. All associates performing manual handling or lifting tasks.

---

### Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Oversee manual handling safety; training |
| Department Heads | Ensure associates are trained before performing lifting tasks |
| All associates | Follow correct lifting technique at all times; request assistance when needed |

---

### Procedure

**1.** [P1] **Risk awareness** — Back strain is a common injury caused by lifting objects that are too heavy or too large for one person, by improper use of the legs, or by twisting the back while lifting. Contusions to fingers or feet can be caused by placing a heavy object incorrectly.

**2.** [P2] **Assess before lifting** — Before lifting, determine whether the object is light and small enough to be safely lifted by one person.

**3.** [P3] **Request assistance** — Always ask for assistance if the object is too heavy or large to carry safely by one person.

**4.** [P4] **Clear path** — Before moving an object, ensure you have a clear path to the destination.

**5.** [P5] **Foot position** — Plant your feet firmly on the ground, shoulder-width apart, before lifting.

**6.** [P6] **Correct lift technique** — Bend at the knees until your thighs are parallel to the floor. Keep your back straight as you lift. Do not use your back to initiate the lift.

**7.** [P7] **Keep body close** — When performing a lift, keep your body close to the object being lifted.

**8.** [P8] **No twisting** — If you must transfer the object in a different direction from the original lift, do not twist your body. Always move your feet to face the direction in which you want to place the object.

**9.** [P9] **Placing on a shelf** — If placing the object on a shelf or rack, use a steady step ladder. Maintain balance while climbing or standing on the ladder. Do not hold or move the object in your hand while ascending or descending the ladder.

**10.** [P10] **Setting down** — To place an object on a designated shelf, rack, pallet, or cart: bend at the knees until thighs are parallel to the floor, keep the back straight, lower the object, and release the hand grip to set it down.

**11.** [P11] **Use handling equipment** — Make use of any material handling carts or lifting devices available when handling large and/or heavy objects.
