## Purpose

Ensure all associates are protected from fire and are equipped with the knowledge to respond correctly when a fire is discovered.

## Scope

All departments. All associates and all persons on [the property].

## Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Fire safety training; oversight of extinguisher maintenance and fire safety compliance |
| Department Heads | Reinforce fire safety procedures in their areas; ensure fire doors remain closed; report issues immediately |
| All associates | Act in accordance with their training; report any conditions that may lead to a fire to their Head of Department |

## Procedure

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Understanding Fire">
    **1.** [P1] **The fire triangle** — Fire requires three elements to start and continue: heat, fuel, and oxygen. This is known as the fire triangle. If one element is removed, the fire will die out.

    **2.** [P2] **Fire injuries** — Fires cause serious physical and psychological injuries and can kill. Burns to large areas of the body cause dehydration, which can be fatal. Breathing hot air from a fire can damage the lungs and eventually make breathing impossible.

    <Warning>
    **3.** [P3] **Smoke hazards** — Smoke is thick, black, and lethally toxic. Smoke irritates the eyes, causing panic, confusion, and loss of vision. A person in a smoke-filled room may lose consciousness and die from oxygen deprivation. Keep all fire doors closed at all times to control smoke spread.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="When Fire Is Discovered">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Raise the alarm">
        [P4] Immediately raise the alarm by breaking the nearest fire alarm call point or dialling on the nearest telephone.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Fight the fire if safe">
        [P5] Fight the fire with the relevant extinguisher only if it is safe and you are trained to do so. Do not fight the fire if you lack adequate or appropriate equipment, if you might inhale toxic smoke, or if the fire is too large.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Close down and evacuate">
        [P6] If in no immediate danger, close down your department. Leave via the nearest fire exit, taking any guests or associates with you, and proceed to the assembly point.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Warning>
    **Evacuation rules** [P7]:
    - Do not stop to collect personal belongings
    - Do not run or panic
    - Do not use lifts
    - Do not re-enter the building until told to do so by the fire marshal
    </Warning>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Extinguisher Classes">
    **8.** [P8] **Extinguisher classes** — Make sure the fire extinguisher is rated for the type of fire before use:

    | Class | Fire Type | Example |
    |---|---|---|
    | **A** | Regular combustibles | Paper, wood |
    | **B** | Flammable liquids | Grease, oil |
    | **C** | Electrical fires | Equipment, wiring |
    | **D** | Combustible metals | Magnesium |

    <Warning>
    [P9] **Kitchen oil fires and electrical fires** — Fire blankets are provided for oil fires in the kitchen. Never use water or foam fire extinguishers on electrical or oil fires.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="PASS Method">
    Use the **PASS** method when operating a fire extinguisher:

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Pull the pin">
        [P11] Pull the pin to allow discharge. Do not use an extinguisher without a pin, or one that is undercharged or overcharged. Ensure the needle is in the green area. Position yourself with the wind at your back.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Aim at the base">
        [P12] Aim at the base of the fire, not at the flames. Aiming at the flames allows the agent to pass through without effect. You must hit the fuel.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Squeeze the handle">
        [P13] Squeeze the top handle to release the pressurised agent. Be aware the agent fills enclosed spaces quickly and can irritate the respiratory system. Always maintain a clear exit route.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Sweep side to side">
        [P14] Sweep the nozzle from side to side across the base of the fire until extinguished.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Tip>
    The goal of a fire extinguisher is to provide you with a means of reaching safety, not to fight large fires.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
