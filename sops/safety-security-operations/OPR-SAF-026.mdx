### Purpose

Ensure all associates are protected from fire and are equipped with the knowledge to respond correctly when a fire is discovered.

---

### Scope

All departments. All associates and all persons on [the property].

---

### Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Fire safety training; oversight of extinguisher maintenance and fire safety compliance |
| Department Heads | Reinforce fire safety procedures in their areas; ensure fire doors remain closed; report issues immediately |
| All associates | Act in accordance with their training; report any conditions that may lead to a fire to their Head of Department |

---

### Procedure

#### Section A — Understanding Fire

**1.** [P1] **The fire triangle** — Fire requires three elements to start and continue: heat, fuel, and oxygen. This is known as the fire triangle. If one element is removed, the fire will die out.

**2.** [P2] **Fire injuries** — Fires cause serious physical and psychological injuries and can kill. Burns to large areas of the body cause dehydration, which can be fatal. Breathing hot air from a fire can damage the lungs and eventually make breathing impossible.

**3.** [P3] **Smoke hazards** — Smoke is thick, black, and lethally toxic. Smoke irritates the eyes, causing panic, confusion, and loss of vision. A person in a smoke-filled room may lose consciousness and die from oxygen deprivation. Keep all fire doors closed at all times to control smoke spread.

---

#### Section B — Action When Fire Is Discovered

**4.** [P4] **Raise the alarm** — Immediately raise the alarm by breaking the nearest fire alarm call point or dialling on the nearest telephone.

**5.** [P5] **Fight the fire if safe to do so** — Fight the fire with the relevant extinguisher only if it is safe and you are trained to do so. Do not fight the fire if you lack adequate or appropriate equipment, if you might inhale toxic smoke, or if the fire is too large.

**6.** [P6] **Close down and evacuate** — If in no immediate danger, close down your department. Leave via the nearest fire exit, taking any guests or associates with you, and proceed to the assembly point.

**7.** [P7] **Evacuation rules** — Do not stop to collect personal belongings. Do not run or panic. Do not use lifts. Do not re-enter the building until told to do so by the fire marshal.

---

#### Section C — Fire Extinguisher Classes

**8.** [P8] **Extinguisher classes** — Make sure the fire extinguisher is rated for the type of fire before use. The fire classes are: Class A = regular combustibles (paper, wood, etc.); Class B = flammable liquid fires (grease, oil, etc.); Class C = electrical fires; Class D = combustible metals (magnesium, etc.).

**9.** [P9] **Kitchen oil fires and electrical fires** — Fire blankets are provided for oil fires in the kitchen. Never use water or foam fire extinguishers on electrical or oil fires.

---

#### Section D — Using a Fire Extinguisher (PASS)

**10.** [P10] **PASS method** — Remember the acronym PASS: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep.

**11.** [P11] **Pull the pin** — Pull the pin. This allows you to discharge the extinguisher. Do not use a fire extinguisher that does not have a pin in place, that is undercharged, or that is overcharged — these are safety hazards and must never be used. Ensure the needle is in the green area before use. Position yourself with the wind at your back; if you take the wind against you, the fire will come toward you.

**12.** [P12] **Aim at the base** — Aim at the base of the fire, not at the flames. Aiming at the flames allows the extinguishing agent to pass through without effect. You must hit the fuel to put out the fire.

**13.** [P13] **Squeeze the handle** — Squeeze the top handle or lever to release the pressurised extinguishing agent. Be aware that the agent fills enclosed spaces quickly and can irritate the respiratory system. Always maintain a clear exit route. The goal of a fire extinguisher is to provide you with a means of reaching safety, not to fight large fires.

**14.** [P14] **Sweep from side to side** — Sweep the extinguisher nozzle from side to side across the base of the fire until it is extinguished.
