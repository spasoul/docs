### Purpose

Provide timely first aid support to associates who become ill or are injured at work, in compliance with applicable legislation.

---

### Scope

All departments. All associates and all persons on [the property].

---

### Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Oversee first aid provision; ensure appointed persons are designated per department |
| [Hotel Nurse] (in season) / [Work Site Doctor] (out of season) | Check and replenish first aid boxes; conduct infirmary equipment calibration and control; maintain First Aid Cabinets Control Form |
| Department Heads | Ensure at least one appointed person is available per department per shift |
| Security / MOD | Maintain AED training; retrieve and deploy AED when required |
| All associates | Know the location of the nearest first aid box; know the appointed person for their department |

---

### Procedure

#### Section A — Legislation and First Aid Provision

**1.** [P1] **Legislative requirement** — Applicable local legislation requires the property to provide adequate and appropriate equipment, facilities, and trained personnel to enable first aid to be given to associates who become ill or are injured at work.

**2.** [P2] **Appointed person** — Each department must have a designated appointed person responsible for first aid arrangements and emergency response. At least one appointed person must be available per department per shift.

**3.** [P3] **Cross-department coverage** — Where an appointed person from another department provides immediate assistance outside their own department, this arrangement must be formally defined and clearly communicated.

**4.** [P4] **Large or remote departments** — In large departments with comprehensive facilities, or properties in remote locations with longer emergency service response times, a fully qualified First Aider must be on duty.

---

#### Section B — First Aid Boxes

**5.** [P5] **Location and visibility** — First aid boxes must be sited conspicuously and be visible to all. First aid box locations are recorded in the First Aid Box List.

**6.** [P6] **Box contents** — First aid box contents are specified in the First Aid Material List in accordance with applicable local legislation. Minimum content includes 20 individually wrapped sterile adhesive dressings (assorted sizes) and 6 medium-sized sterile unmedicated wound dressings.

**7.** [P7] **Eyewash station** — An eyewash station must be provided in any area where chemicals are used.

**8.** [P8] **Checks and replenishment** — First aid boxes are checked regularly by [the hotel nurse / work site doctor]. The First Aid Cabinets Control Form is completed at each check. The eyewash checklist is completed by the hotel nurse once a month.

---

#### Section C — AED (Automatic External Defibrillator — Life-POINT Pro)

**9.** [P9] **Training** — Security personnel and Managers on Duty (MODs) must complete First Aid and AED training to be qualified to provide immediate assistance.

**10.** [P10] **Siting** — AED units must be sited conspicuously and be visible to all. The AED is located in [the front office] during the in-season period and in [the infirmary] during the out-of-season period. Security will provide the device when required.

**11.** [P11] **Trained use only** — The AED must only be used by persons who have received medical education and AED training.

**12.** [P12] **Unresponsive patients only** — The AED must only be used on unresponsive, unconscious persons.

**13.** [P13] **Dry, clean chest** — The patient's chest must be dry and clean before applying AED pads.

**14.** [P14] **Do not touch during analysis** — Do not touch the patient while the AED is analysing heart rhythm. Contact during analysis may disrupt the diagnostic process.

**15.** [P15] **Age and weight restrictions** — Do not use AED on patients aged 0–1 year (defibrillation is not recommended). Use paediatric pads for patients aged 1–8 years or under 25 kg. Use adult pads for patients aged over 8 years or over 25 kg.

**16.** [P16] **Calibration and maintenance** — The AED must be calibrated at 1-year intervals. Periodic maintenance is required at 2-year intervals. Equipment controls are followed weekly using the Calibration AED Control Form by [the Work Site Doctor].
