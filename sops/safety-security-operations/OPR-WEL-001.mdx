## Purpose

To handle the situation in a discreet manner; to avoid causing embarrassment or discomfort to the guest.

---

## Scope

Applies whenever a team member suspects a guest is under the influence of drugs or alcohol prior to or during a treatment or service. Drugs include all classes, even legal drugs prescribed by a medical doctor.

---

## Responsibility

All associates delivering guest-facing treatments or services. [Spa Manager] / [Department Head] for incident reporting.

---

## Policy Overview

From a therapeutic and medical perspective, there are many reasons why a treatment should be withheld from a guest under the influence of drugs or alcohol. Note here that drugs include all classes, even legal drugs prescribed by a medical doctor; spa treatments can cause alcohol to be more quickly absorbed into the bloodstream causing nausea, discomfort, dizziness and other symptoms some of which can be unpleasant both for the guest and for the therapist; it is also our duty of care towards our guests, not to endanger their life in any way.

---

## Procedure

If you suspect the guest is under the influence of drugs or alcohol:

1. [P1] Check the consultation card they had previously filled out to see if they are on any medication.

2. [P2] Also, check whether they have any illnesses. Some illnesses, especially disorders like Multiple Sclerosis can make the person seem intoxicated when they are not. Please be discreet. If you are in doubt, then speak to the manager.

3. [P3] Do not judge the guest or make fun of them.

4. [P4] If they do have a condition, ask them if it is normally safe for them to have the type of treatment booked.

5. [P5] If no, ask if they are on medication (they may have forgotten to write it in) or if they have consumed any intoxicating substances before their visit.

6. [P6] If the person says yes, and they appear to be intoxicated, explain to the guest that it is not advisable for them to receive treatments at this time and that the [spa / department] recommends rescheduling the treatment at a later date. [Note that the cancellation charge does not apply in this case.] If at any time you are not comfortable handling the guest, then call the manager.

7. [P7] If you successfully handle the guest, you must still inform the [Spa Manager] / [Department Head] and a report must be completed in case the guest files a complaint.

8. [P8] If at any time the guest is not responding to you or is out of control, leave the room and call the manager immediately — your safety is our first priority. In this case, you must also call security.


<Accordion title="Checklist" icon="clipboard-check">
1. [C1] Consultation card reviewed for medication and health conditions before approaching the guest.
2. [C2] Discretion maintained throughout; no judgment expressed to or in front of the guest.
3. [C3] If treatment withheld: rescheduling offered; no cancellation charge applied; manager notified.
4. [C4] Incident report completed and submitted to [Spa Manager] / [Department Head] after every case, including successfully resolved ones.
5. [C5] If guest unresponsive or out of control: room vacated immediately; manager and security called.
