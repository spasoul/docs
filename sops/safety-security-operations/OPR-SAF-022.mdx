### Purpose

Ensure all associates apply common sense and deliberate thinking before beginning, during, and after every task to prevent accidents and injuries.

---

### Scope

All departments. All associates at all times.

---

### Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Training on hazard awareness and pre-task thinking |
| Department Heads | Reinforce stop-and-think culture in their teams; ensure barriers and signage are used |
| All associates | Stop, think, and ask questions before beginning any task |

---

### Procedure

**1.** [P1] **Stop and think before starting** — Before beginning any task, stop and think about what you are about to do. This helps you understand what PPE and other materials you will need to accomplish the job safely.

**2.** [P2] **Consider all factors beforehand** — Think about what factors could affect you and others while you are doing the job. Place barriers around the work site if necessary to prevent injury from carelessness.

**3.** [P3] **Stay aware while working** — While working, be aware of your surroundings so that any variables not considered during planning can be dealt with promptly. This includes wind if working outside and increased foot traffic around the work site.

**4.** [P4] **Do not let distractions affect safety** — Arguments or other events during breaks can cause loss of focus. Do not allow personal distractions to reduce your attention to safety on the work site.

**5.** [P5] **Reassess after breaks** — If taking a break mid-task, reassess the first steps and current state before continuing. Try not to let anything that happened during the break affect the safety of the work site.

**6.** [P6] **Review the area after completing the job** — After completing the task, think about how safe the area is after cleanup. Consider keeping barriers in place until the area is fully safe — for example, if concrete needs to set, or if a surface is still hot from steam cleaning.

**7.** [P7] **Ask if unsure** — The most important thing is to stop, think, and ask questions about anything you do not understand. If you think there might be a problem but cannot identify it, ask someone else to evaluate the situation. Two heads are often better than one.
