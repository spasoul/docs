## Purpose

To ensure the health and safety of a pregnant guest and her unborn child.

---

## Scope

Applies at the point of booking and at reception check-in when a guest declares or is identified as pregnant. Covers treatment restrictions by trimester and safe treatment alternatives.

---

## Responsibility

Receptionist for booking and check-in restrictions. Therapist for in-treatment contraindication compliance and oil substitution. [Spa Manager] for escalation and staff guidance.

---

## Policy Overview

Explain and recommend the right treatments for pregnant guests.

---

## Procedure

### Section A — General Approach

1. [P1] Inform the guest that as we need to ensure the safety of both her and her unborn child, the type of spa treatments she can book are restricted.

2. [P2] The guest is likely to be understanding and respectful of our ethical standards.

3. [P3] Be aware that the following restrictions may seem excessive but we must err on the side of caution to ensure guest and their unborn child's safety.

### Section B — Treatments NOT to Be Sold Under Any Circumstance

4. [P4] The following spa treatments are **NOT** to be sold to a pregnant guest under **any** circumstance:

   a. [SOP-OPR-WEL-002.P.4a] Baths — they can stimulate early childbirth.
   b. [SOP-OPR-WEL-002.P.4b] Body Wraps.
   c. [SOP-OPR-WEL-002.P.4c] Any type of foot treatment, including foot exfoliations and foot massage.
   d. [SOP-OPR-WEL-002.P.4d] Any treatment which applies or generates heat onto the body.

### Section C — Treatments Safe at Any Stage of Pregnancy

5. [P5] The following spa treatment categories are safe for a pregnant guest:

   a. [SOP-OPR-WEL-002.P.5a] Any type of facial — but facials which include extra massage of the feet must substitute a hand or scalp massage to replace the foot work.
   b. [SOP-OPR-WEL-002.P.5b] Body Polishes and Body Scrubs — avoid the feet area.
   c. [SOP-OPR-WEL-002.P.5c] Any type of hand treatment.
   d. [SOP-OPR-WEL-002.P.5d] Any type of scalp treatment.

### Section D — Treatments Safe After the First Trimester

6. [P6] The following specific spa treatments are safe for a pregnant guest **after the first trimester**:

   a. [SOP-OPR-WEL-002.P.6a] All facials.
   b. [SOP-OPR-WEL-002.P.6b] Pregnant Massage — but using BASE OIL, not Aromatherapy Oil. A good all-round substitute product is Olive Oil or Grape Seed Oil.

---

## Checklist

1. [C1] Pregnancy status confirmed at booking and re-checked at check-in; gestation trimester noted on consultation card.
2. [C2] No contraindicated treatments booked or delivered (baths, body wraps, foot treatments, heat treatments).
3. [C3] Foot work substituted with hand or scalp massage in any facial including a foot massage element.
4. [C4] Pregnant Massage delivered with BASE OIL only (not Aromatherapy Oil); no Aromatherapy Oil applied at any time.
5. [C5] If trimester is unclear or guest is uncertain, treat as first trimester (most conservative restrictions apply).

## MEVZUAT Note

> Pregnancy is sensitive personal data under **KVKK Law No. 6698**, specifically Art. 6 (Özel nitelikli kişisel veriler — Sensitive Personal Data: pregnancy status and gestation stage are health data requiring explicit written consent or processing by persons under secrecy obligation for preventive medicine), Art. 10 (Aydınlatma yükümlülüğü — Transparency: inform guest of data controller identity, purpose, recipients, and rights before collecting pregnancy information), and Art. 8 (Kişisel verilerin aktarılması — Transfer: if pregnancy data is shared with third-party medical providers, lawful transfer conditions must be met). Consultation card data must be handled with restricted access and appropriate retention controls. Compliance with Turkish Ministry of Health maternal health protection rules applies. This SOP also supports **Law No. 6331** Art. 4 (İşverenin genel yükümlülükleri — General Obligations: duty of care for guest safety) and Art. 17 (Çalışanların eğitimi — Worker Training: staff trained on pregnancy-related contraindications and safe service modifications).
