### Purpose

Ensure all associates understand and act upon safety signs and symbols, which communicate hazards, mandatory actions, prohibitions, and emergency information throughout [the property].

---

### Scope

All departments. All associates and all persons on [the property].

---

### Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Ensure safety signs are correctly installed, maintained, and understood by all associates |
| Department Heads | Ensure their areas are correctly signed; report damaged or missing signs |
| All associates | Read, understand, and act in accordance with all safety signs at all times |

---

### Procedure

#### Section A — Purpose of Safety Signs

**1.** [P1] **What safety signs do** — Safety signs and symbols are important safety communication tools. They indicate various hazards present in a work area, warn associates to watch for those hazards, and provide required information and safety instructions.

---

#### Section B — Sign Colour and Meaning

**2.** [P2] **Red signs** — Red is used for prohibition signs (dangerous behaviour that must not occur), hazard alarms (stop, close, emergency stop), and fire-fighting equipment (identification and location of fire equipment).

**3.** [P3] **Yellow signs** — Yellow is used for warning signs. Yellow signs mean: be careful; take precautions.

**4.** [P4] **Blue signs** — Blue is used for mandatory (obligation) signs. Blue signs require certain behaviour or a specific action — most commonly, the use of personal protective equipment. Blue is considered a safety colour only when used in circular shapes.

**5.** [P5] **Green signs** — Green is used for emergency escape signs and first aid signs, indicating doors, exits, roads, equipment, and facilities. Green is also used for "no danger" (return to normal) indications.

**6.** [P6] **Black and white signs** — Black and white are used for barriers and signs placed in dangerous areas.

**7.** [P7] **Orange signs** — Orange signs may be used as a substitute for yellow signs in outdoor areas. Orange is particularly effective in poor natural light conditions.

---

#### Section C — Compliance

**8.** [P8] **Obey all safety signs** — All associates must read and act in accordance with all safety signs. Ignoring safety signs contributes directly to workplace accidents and injuries.

**9.** [P9] **Report damaged or missing signs** — Report any damaged, obscured, or missing safety signs to your Head of Department or the [Occupational Safety Manager] immediately.
