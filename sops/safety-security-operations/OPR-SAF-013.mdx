## Purpose

Ensure all associates are properly trained on moving trolleys and wheeled vehicles safely, to protect guests, colleagues, and themselves, and to prevent damage to surroundings.

---

## Scope

All departments. All associates moving trolleys, wheeled carts, housekeeping bins, or any other wheeled vehicle on [the property].

---

## Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Oversee wheeled vehicle movement safety; training |
| Department Heads | Ensure associates are trained before operating wheeled vehicles; reinforce procedures in high-traffic areas |
| All associates | Inspect vehicles before use; follow correct movement procedures; request assistance when needed |

---

## Procedure

#### Section A — Pre-Movement Inspection

**1.** [P1] **Inspect before use** — Before moving any trolley or wheeled vehicle, ensure it is in good working condition: not bent, chipped, or obstructed, so it can be operated smoothly and without interruption.

**2.** [P2] **Closing mechanism** — Ensure the closing mechanism, if fitted, is working properly.

**3.** [P3] **Wheels** — Ensure wheels are working properly. Damaged, bent, or non-functioning wheels may cause the cart to topple or become uncontrollable, or may require excessive force to move.

**4.** [P4] **Cleanliness** — Ensure the trolley is clean and presentable before moving it through front-of-house areas.

---

#### Section B — Loading

**5.** [P5] **Load within capacity** — Ensure the load is not excessive, so the vehicle can be moved and controlled easily.

**6.** [P6] **Items inside** — Ensure all items are inside the trolley and not visible on the outside. If using an open trolley, ensure items are neatly arranged and presentable.

**7.** [P7] **Close and cover** — If the trolley has a closing mechanism, ensure it is closed. If the trolley has a cover, ensure it is on.

---

#### Section C — Movement Rules

**8.** [P8] **Walking pace only** — Never move a wheeled cart or vehicle faster than a normal to slow walking pace. Objects in motion are difficult to stop; the faster they move, the harder they are to control.

**9.** [P9] **Push, do not pull** — Wheeled carts and vehicles must always be pushed from behind — never pulled from the front or sides. Pulling creates greater physical strain and risks crushing injuries if the operator falls.

**10.** [P10] **Not overloaded** — Do not overload the trolley. An overloaded vehicle is harder to control and increases the risk of tipping.

**11.** [P11] **Clear visibility** — Always ensure you can see the route of travel ahead. If necessary, ask another person to walk alongside to improve visibility.

**12.** [P12] **Stop for approaching persons** — Stop if you notice a person coming towards you and allow them to pass before resuming movement.

**13.** [P13] **Corners, doorways, and elevators** — Exercise heightened caution when turning corners, passing through doorways, and entering or exiting elevators. Prevent damage to doors, walls, frames, and the trolley itself.

**14.** [P14] **Wall corners** — Be especially careful at wall corners.

**15.** [P15] **Ask for assistance** — Ask for assistance if the trolley is too heavy to manoeuvre safely.

---

#### Section D — Ramps and Slopes

**16.** [P16] **Two-person minimum on ramps** — A minimum of two people must be used when moving wheeled vehicles on ramps. One person must be positioned at the front of the vehicle to act as a brake and prevent it from accelerating.

**17.** [P17] **Choose the right vehicle** — Select the appropriate wheeled vehicle type for ramp use, to prevent items from falling during transit.

**18.** [P18] **Evaluate alternative routes** — Before using a wheeled vehicle on a ramp or slope, evaluate whether an alternative transport route is available (e.g. buggy or goods lift).

**19.** [P19] **Prohibited route — high-slope road** — It is forbidden to carry any material using a wheelbarrow on [the designated high-slope road] due to the gradient. [Property-specific exceptions to this rule, if any, are defined separately for affected departments.]
