### Purpose

Ensure all associates know how to use stairs and ramps safely, and follow correct walking practices throughout the property.

---

### Scope

All departments. All associates walking anywhere on [the property].

---

### Responsibility

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| [Occupational Safety Manager] | Training on safe walking; ensure hazard signage available throughout property |
| Department Heads | Reinforce procedures in high-traffic and high-risk areas |
| All associates | Follow safe walking rules at all times; address and sign wet/hazardous surfaces |

---

### Procedure

#### Section A — General Walking Rules

**1.** [P1] **Watch where you are going** — Do not let yourself become distracted. Always watch where you are going. Ensure work areas and travel routes are well lit.

**2.** [P2] **Hazard awareness** — Obstacles, uneven ground, wet or slippery surfaces can cause slips, trips, and falls. Remain alert to these hazards at all times.

**3.** [P3] **Irrigation hazard** — Stairs, roads, and ramps may be wet due to irrigation or cleaning. Always be careful, especially during and after watering periods.

**4.** [P4] **Always walk, never run** — Never run or take more than one step at a time. Running or taking multiple steps at once may cause serious injury, including back and spinal injuries.

**5.** [P5] **Walk on the right side** — Always walk on the right side of corridors and stairways. Observe and listen for others traveling in the opposite direction around corners.

**6.** [P6] **No phone use while walking** — Do not talk on a cell phone while walking, climbing stairs, or using escalators.

**7.** [P7] **Footwear** — Wear slip-resistant safety shoes. Anti-slip tape for standard shoes is available from [Laundry / designated department].

**8.** [P8] **Look for signage** — Always observe and heed caution and wet floor signs.

---

#### Section B — Passages and Common Areas

**9.** [P9] **No storage in passages or stairs** — Passages and stairs must not be used as storage areas. All delivered goods must be removed to the proper store without delay.

**10.** [P10] **No smoking** — Passages and stairs are no-smoking areas.

---

#### Section C — Stairs and Ramps

**11.** [P11] **Hold the handrail** — Always hold the handrail when walking up or down a staircase or ramp. Failure to use the handrail is a leading cause of stair and ramp injuries.

**12.** [P12] **Wet stairs and ramps** — Stairs and ramps become more dangerous when wet. Place a wet floor sign at both the top and the bottom of any wet stair or ramp. Exercise extreme caution — serious bodily injury may occur on wet stairs or ramps.

**13.** [P13] **Carrying objects on stairs** — Carrying objects while using stairs or ramps increases the risk of slips and falls. Limit yourself to objects that do not impair your vision and allow you to hold the handrail.

**14.** [P14] **Both hands occupied — use the elevator** — If both hands are occupied with a carried load, elevators must be used instead of stairs.

---

#### Section D — Property-Specific Hazards

**15.** [P15] **High-slope ramps and slippery surfaces** — [The property] has high-slope ramps and certain staircase surfaces that are particularly slippery. Exercise heightened caution in these areas.

**16.** [P16] **Designated buggy road** — [The designated buggy road] is not a walking path. Do not use it for pedestrian access.

**17.** [P17] **Shared buggy and pedestrian pathways** — In areas where buggy and pedestrian access share the same path, both buggy users and pedestrians must proceed carefully and consciously.
