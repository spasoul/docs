## Purpose

To safely deliver normobaric (ambient-pressure) supplemental oxygen sessions using high-flow nasal cannula or non-rebreather mask to support post-exercise recovery, cognitive clarity, and cellular oxygenation without the pressurised environment of hyperbaric therapy.

---

## Scope

Covers normobaric oxygen delivery at FiO₂ levels between 30% and 95% via nasal cannula, Venturi mask, or non-rebreather mask. Does not cover hyperbaric oxygen therapy (see SOP-SVC-LNG-002), mechanical ventilation, or oxygen therapy for acute medical conditions.

---

## Responsibility

[Specialist] — delivers session, monitors SpO₂ and flow rate. [Clinical] nurse required on-site if FiO₂ exceeds 40% or session duration exceeds [X minutes]. Physician prescribes FiO₂ target and duration as part of guest programme.

---

## Absolute Contraindications

- Untreated pneumothorax
- Severe COPD with known CO₂ retention (oxygen-induced hypercapnia risk)
- Active bleomycin chemotherapy (oxygen toxicity potentiation)
- Paraquat poisoning history

## Relative Contraindications (physician clearance required)

- Mild-to-moderate COPD (titrated flow with SpO₂ monitoring)
- History of oxygen-induced seizures
- Pregnancy (FiO₂ limits per obstetric guidance)
- Concurrent use of sedatives or respiratory depressants

## MEVZUAT Note

> **Law No. 1219** (Turkish Medical Practice Law): Articles 1 and 3 require physician prescription for oxygen therapy with FiO₂ >40%; clinical staff must be licensed. **Tıbbi Cihaz Yönetmeliği** (Turkish Medical Devices Regulation): Article 5 and 10 require oxygen delivery equipment (concentrators, regulators) to meet CE/MDR certification; Art. 83-86 (Piyasa gözetimi ve denetimi — Market surveillance and adverse event reporting) apply and Turkish Ministry of Health ÜTS (Ürün Takip Sistemi) registration; maintenance records required. **KVKK Law No. 6698** (Personal Data Protection Law): Article 6 governs session records and SpO₂ data — sensitive health data protections apply. **Law No. 6331** (Occupational Health and Safety Law): Articles 4, 10, 11, and 17 (Çalışanların eğitimi — Worker Training) mandate fire safety protocols for oxygen-enriched environments and emergency response procedures.
