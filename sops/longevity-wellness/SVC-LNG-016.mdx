## Purpose

To safely deliver alternating hot and cold exposure sessions — combining infrared sauna, steam, hot plunge, and cold plunge/ice bath — to stimulate vascular cycling, reduce inflammation, enhance recovery, and train autonomic stress resilience as part of the longevity programme.

---

## Scope

Covers structured hot-cold alternation protocols using existing facility thermal assets (infrared sauna, steam room, hot plunge pool, cold plunge pool, ice bath). Does not cover standalone cryotherapy (see SOP-SVC-LNG-001) or standalone infrared sauna (see SOP-SVC-LNG-007) when delivered as isolated sessions.

---

## Responsibility

[Specialist] — supervises protocol sequence, monitors guest response during transitions, and manages thermal shock risk. Physician clearance required for cardiovascular risk guests.


<Warning>
**Absolute Contraindications** — Do not proceed under any circumstances:
- Uncontrolled hypertension or hypotension
- Recent myocardial infarction or unstable angina (within [X months])
- Active deep vein thrombosis or pulmonary embolism
- Raynaud's phenomenon (severe — cold exposure contraindicated)
- Open wounds, active skin infections, or fever
</Warning>


<Note>
**Relative Contraindications** — Physician clearance required:
- Controlled cardiovascular disease (physician to define temperature limits and exposure duration)
- Pregnancy (any trimester — extreme thermal exposure contraindicated)
- Peripheral neuropathy (impaired thermal sensation)
- Epilepsy (thermal shock may trigger seizures in susceptible individuals)
- Mild Raynaud's (modified cold exposure with shorter duration)
</Note>
