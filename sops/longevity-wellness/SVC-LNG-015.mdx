## Purpose

To safely deliver dry salt aerosol therapy (halotherapy) in a controlled salt chamber environment to support respiratory health, mucociliary clearance, skin condition improvement, and relaxation as part of the longevity programme.

---

## Scope

Covers halogenerator-based dry salt aerosol sessions in a dedicated salt room/chamber. Does not cover wet salt therapy (saline nebulisation), speleotherapy (natural salt cave), or salt therapy for acute respiratory disease management.

---

## Responsibility

[Specialist] — operates halogenerator, manages session environment, and monitors guest response. No physician presence required for standard sessions; physician referral if respiratory distress or allergic reaction occurs.


<Warning>
**Absolute Contraindications** — Do not proceed under any circumstances:
- Active tuberculosis or other infectious respiratory disease
- Severe hypotension (salt aerosol may further lower blood pressure)
- Active haemoptysis (coughing blood)
- Acute respiratory failure
- Salt sensitivity allergy (rare but documented)
</Warning>


<Note>
**Relative Contraindications** — Physician clearance required:
- Asthma (may trigger bronchospasm in some individuals; rescue inhaler must be accessible)
- COPD (mild-to-moderate; may benefit but monitoring required)
- Hyperthyroidism (iodine content in some salt preparations)
- Open skin wounds or severe dermatitis (salt exposure may irritate)
- Pregnancy (precautionary; limited evidence)
</Note>
