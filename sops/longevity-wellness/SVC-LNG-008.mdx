## Purpose

To safely deliver intermittent pneumatic compression (IPC) therapy sessions to enhance lymphatic drainage, improve circulation, accelerate muscle recovery, and reduce swelling, using calibrated compression devices.

---

## Scope

Covers full-leg, arm, and recovery boot IPC devices. Covers sequential and uniform compression modes. Does not cover medical-grade compression garments prescribed for clinical DVT management (separate medical protocol).

---

## Responsibility

[Specialist] or [Coach] — fits garments, sets device parameters, monitors session. Guest may complete session with periodic check-ins.

---

## Absolute Contraindications

- Active deep vein thrombosis (DVT) or pulmonary embolism — compression may dislodge clot
- Active infection or cellulitis in treatment area
- Open wounds or skin breakdown in treatment area
- Severe peripheral artery disease (arterial insufficiency — compression will reduce blood flow)
- Congestive heart failure (fluid redistribution risk — physician clearance required)
- Known malignancy in treatment area (lymphatic stimulation risk)

## Relative Contraindications (physician clearance required)

- History of DVT (stable, >6 months — physician assessment of clot resolution required)
- Peripheral neuropathy (impaired sensation — cannot reliably report discomfort)
- Mild peripheral artery disease
- Recent lower limb surgery or fracture
- Pregnancy (lower limb compression — physician decision)

## MEVZUAT Note

> **Tıbbi Cihaz Yönetmeliği** (Turkish Medical Devices Regulation): Article 5 and 10 require compression devices to meet CE/MDR certification; Art. 83-86 (Piyasa gözetimi ve denetimi — Market surveillance and adverse event reporting) apply and Turkish Ministry of Health ÜTS (Ürün Takip Sistemi) registration. **Law No. 1219** (Turkish Medical Practice Law): Article 1 requires physician referral pathway for guest access to compression therapy. **KVKK Law No. 6698** (Personal Data Protection Law): Article 6 governs session records and adverse event documentation. **Law No. 6331** (Occupational Health and Safety Law): Articles 4, 10, and 17 (Çalışanların eğitimi — Worker Training) mandate facility safety and operator ergonomics protocols.
