## Purpose

To safely deliver float therapy sessions in an Epsom-salt (magnesium sulphate) saturated tank for deep relaxation, stress reduction, pain relief, and sensory rest, while maintaining water hygiene and guest safety.

---

## Scope

Covers closed float pod and open float pool configurations. Covers water quality management, guest preparation, session delivery, and post-session procedures.

---

## Responsibility

[Specialist] — certified float operator responsible for water quality, guest briefing, and session monitoring. Guest floats independently inside pod; operator available immediately outside throughout.

---

## Absolute Contraindications

- Open wounds, cuts, or abrasions (Epsom salt causes severe pain; infection risk)
- Active skin infections, contagious skin conditions, or tinea
- Uncontrolled epilepsy (risk of seizure in enclosed water environment)
- Severe claustrophobia (enclosed pod — open float option may apply with physician approval)
- Incontinence
- Intoxication (drugs or alcohol)
- Kidney disease — high magnesium absorption risk (physician clearance)
- Recent hair colouring or chemical treatment (contamination risk to solution)

## Relative Contraindications (physician clearance required)

- Controlled epilepsy (open float configuration with enhanced monitoring)
- Severe depression or psychosis (acute sensory deprivation risk — physician decision)
- Low blood pressure (hypotension — supine position and heat may cause dizziness on exit)
- Pregnancy (second/third trimester — physician approval; first trimester contraindicated)
