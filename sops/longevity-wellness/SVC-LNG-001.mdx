## Purpose

To safely deliver whole-body or localised cryotherapy sessions at therapeutic temperatures, maximising recovery, inflammation reduction, and mood benefits while preventing cold injury.

## Scope

Covers preparation, guest screening, session delivery, and post-session monitoring for cryotherapy chamber or localised cryo device. Applies to all guest cryotherapy sessions.

## Responsibility

[Cryotherapy Specialist] — certified operator present for entire session. Unattended sessions are prohibited.

## Contraindications

<Warning>
**Absolute Contraindications** — Do not proceed under any circumstances:
- Raynaud's disease or cold urticaria
- Severe cardiovascular disease or arrhythmia
- Uncontrolled hypertension
- Active deep vein thrombosis
- Open wounds, severe skin conditions, or neuropathy in exposed areas
- Pregnancy
- Claustrophobia (whole-body chamber)
- Cryoglobulinaemia
</Warning>

<Note>
**Relative Contraindications** — Physician clearance required:
- Controlled hypertension
- History of stroke or heart attack (stable, >12 months)
- Peripheral artery disease (mild)
- Metal implants in the treatment area
</Note>

## Procedure

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pre-Session Equipment Check">
    1. [P1] Inspect cryotherapy chamber: verify temperature display functional, door seals intact, nitrogen supply levels adequate, emergency stop accessible.
    2. [P2] Pre-cool chamber to operating temperature [−110°C to −140°C] per manufacturer specifications; confirm temperature achieved before guest entry.
    3. [P3] Check emergency stop button and intercom system functional; verify operator can maintain visual contact throughout.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Guest Screening & Preparation">
    4. [P4] Verify contraindication clearance in guest Programme Design Form (SOP-GDN-CUR-004); re-screen verbally at each session for any new conditions.
    5. [P5] Confirm guest is dry (no damp skin or hair); remove all metal jewellery, wet clothing, and footwear.
    6. [P6] Issue protective items: gloves, socks, and face mask as required; brief guest on session experience and emergency exit procedure.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Session Delivery">
    7. [P7] Confirm session duration: first session [X minutes]; subsequent sessions [Y minutes] per protocol; do not exceed [Z minutes maximum].
    8. [P8] Maintain continuous verbal contact every [30 seconds]; instruct guest to report any pain, numbness, excessive cold, or discomfort immediately.

    <Warning>
    [P9] **Terminate session immediately** if: guest requests, numbness reported, skin colour changes noted, vital signs deviate, or equipment alarm sounds.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Post-Session Monitoring">
    10. [P10] Assist guest to exit chamber; observe skin for redness, frostbite signs, or cold urticaria response for minimum [X minutes].
    11. [P11] Allow guest to warm naturally in designated recovery area; do not apply direct heat immediately.
    12. [P12] Document session in guest treatment log: date, duration, temperature achieved, guest response, any adverse events.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Accordion title="Regulatory Compliance (MEVZUAT)" icon="scale-balanced">
  **Tıbbi Cihaz Yönetmeliği** (Turkish Medical Devices Regulation): Article 5 and 10 require cryotherapy chambers to meet CE/MDR certification; Art. 83-86 (Piyasa gözetimi ve denetimi — Market surveillance and adverse event reporting) apply and be registered with Turkish Ministry of Health; equipment must undergo periodic safety inspections.

  **Law No. 1219** (Turkish Medical Practice Law): Article 1 requires physician referral pathway for guest access to cryotherapy.

  **KVKK Law No. 6698** (Personal Data Protection Law): Article 6 governs session records and adverse event documentation.

  **Law No. 6331** (Occupational Health and Safety Law): Articles 4, 10, 15 (Sağlık gözetimi — Health Surveillance), and 17 (Çalışanların eğitimi — Worker Training) mandate staff safety training for cryogenic equipment operation and facility safety protocols.
</Accordion>
