## Purpose

To safely deliver whole-body or localised cryotherapy sessions at therapeutic temperatures, maximising recovery, inflammation reduction, and mood benefits while preventing cold injury.

---

## Scope

Covers preparation, guest screening, session delivery, and post-session monitoring for cryotherapy chamber or localised cryo device. Applies to all guest cryotherapy sessions.

---

## Responsibility

[Cryotherapy Specialist] — certified operator present for entire session. Unattended sessions are prohibited.

---

## Absolute Contraindications

- Raynaud's disease or cold urticaria
- Severe cardiovascular disease or arrhythmia
- Uncontrolled hypertension
- Active deep vein thrombosis
- Open wounds, severe skin conditions, or neuropathy in exposed areas
- Pregnancy
- Claustrophobia (whole-body chamber)
- Cryoglobulinaemia

## Relative Contraindications (physician clearance required)

- Controlled hypertension
- History of stroke or heart attack (stable, >12 months)
- Peripheral artery disease (mild)
- Metal implants in the treatment area

## MEVZUAT Note

> **Tıbbi Cihaz Yönetmeliği** (Turkish Medical Devices Regulation): Article 5 and 10 require cryotherapy chambers to meet CE/MDR certification; Art. 83-86 (Piyasa gözetimi ve denetimi — Market surveillance and adverse event reporting) apply and be registered with Turkish Ministry of Health; equipment must undergo periodic safety inspections. **Law No. 1219** (Turkish Medical Practice Law): Article 1 requires physician referral pathway for guest access to cryotherapy. **KVKK Law No. 6698** (Personal Data Protection Law): Article 6 governs session records and adverse event documentation. **Law No. 6331** (Occupational Health and Safety Law): Articles 4, 10, 15 (Sağlık gözetimi — Health Surveillance), and 17 (Çalışanların eğitimi — Worker Training) mandate staff safety training for cryogenic equipment operation and facility safety protocols.
