## Purpose

To safely deliver structured respiratory and breathwork sessions — including diaphragmatic breathing, box breathing, cyclic hyperventilation (Wim Hof–style), and coherence breathing — to support autonomic regulation, stress resilience, CO₂ tolerance, and parasympathetic recovery.

---

## Scope

Covers guided breathwork sessions (individual and small-group), breath-hold protocols, and respiratory muscle training. Does not cover mechanical ventilation, spirometry diagnostics, or treatment of diagnosed respiratory disease (separate clinical protocol required).

---

## Responsibility

[Specialist] — certified breathwork facilitator; leads sessions, monitors guest response, and manages hyperventilation risk. Physician referral required if respiratory, cardiovascular, or neurological condition identified during screening.

---

## Absolute Contraindications

- Uncontrolled epilepsy or seizure disorder
- Recent pneumothorax or active lung pathology
- Severe cardiovascular disease (uncontrolled arrhythmia, recent MI, aortic aneurysm)
- Active psychosis or dissociative disorder
- Late-stage pregnancy (third trimester — aggressive breath-hold protocols only)

## Relative Contraindications (physician clearance required)

- Asthma (moderate-to-severe; rescue inhaler must be accessible)
- History of panic disorder or PTSD (hyperventilation may trigger episodes)
- Hypertension on medication (blood pressure response monitoring required)
- First/second trimester pregnancy (gentle diaphragmatic only; no breath-hold or cyclic hyperventilation)
- Recent abdominal or thoracic surgery

## MEVZUAT Note

> **Law No. 1219** (Turkish Medical Practice Law): Article 1 requires physician referral pathway for breathwork interventions targeting respiratory conditions. **KVKK Law No. 6698** (Personal Data Protection Law): Article 6 governs session records and health data — breathwork uses no regulated medical devices. **Law No. 6331** (Occupational Health and Safety Law): Articles 4, 10, and 17 (Çalışanların eğitimi — Worker Training) mandate facility safety for session environments and emergency response protocols (vasovagal/syncope risk). Documented informed consent required for hyperventilation-based protocols.
