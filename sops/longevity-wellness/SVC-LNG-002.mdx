## Purpose

To safely deliver pressurised oxygen therapy sessions to enhance tissue oxygenation, accelerate recovery, reduce inflammation, and support cognitive and cellular repair.

---

## Scope

Covers hard-shell or soft-shell hyperbaric chamber operation for wellness HBOT. Clinical HBOT (wound healing, decompression sickness) requires separate medical protocols beyond this SOP's scope.

---

## Responsibility

[Physician] — prescribes protocol, monitors for contraindications, on-call during session. [Specialist] or [Clinical] — operates chamber and monitors guest.

---

## Absolute Contraindications

- Untreated pneumothorax
- Current upper respiratory infection or sinusitis (pressure equalisation risk)
- Uncontrolled epilepsy
- Certain chemotherapy agents (bleomycin, doxorubicin, cisplatin — oxygen toxicity risk)
- Claustrophobia (unmanaged)
- Pregnancy (first trimester — caution; physician decision)

## Relative Contraindications (physician clearance required)

- Ear or sinus surgery within 3 months
- Controlled pulmonary disease (COPD, asthma)
- Pacemaker or implanted defibrillator (device compatibility check required)
- History of seizures (stable)

## MEVZUAT Note

> **Law No. 1219** (Turkish Medical Practice Law): Articles 1 and 3 require physician prescription of HBOT sessions and physician oversight during treatment. **Tıbbi Cihaz Yönetmeliği** (Turkish Medical Devices Regulation): Article 5 and 10 require hyperbaric chambers to meet CE/MDR certification; Art. 83-86 (Piyasa gözetimi ve denetimi — Market surveillance and adverse event reporting) apply and Turkish Ministry of Health ÜTS (Ürün Takip Sistemi) registration; periodic equipment safety inspections mandatory. **KVKK Law No. 6698** (Personal Data Protection Law): Article 6 governs session records and adverse event documentation. **Law No. 6331** (Occupational Health and Safety Law): Articles 4, 10, 15 (Sağlık gözetimi — Health Surveillance), 11, and 17 (Çalışanların eğitimi — Worker Training) mandate facility safety, fire safety protocols for oxygen-enriched environments, and emergency response procedures.
