## Purpose

To safely deliver whole-body electrical muscle stimulation (EMS) training sessions to enhance muscle activation, strength, and body composition outcomes, under certified supervision with calibrated intensity and appropriate guest conditioning.

---

## Scope

Covers whole-body EMS suit training and localised EMS pad applications for wellness and performance purposes. Does not cover therapeutic TENS or clinical NMES for medical rehabilitation (separate protocol).

---

## Responsibility

[Specialist] or [Coach] — certified EMS trainer present and actively coaching throughout entire session. Unattended EMS sessions are prohibited.

---

## Absolute Contraindications

- Electronic implants: pacemaker, ICD, cochlear implant, insulin pump, or any active electronic implant
- Pregnancy
- Active malignancy
- Epilepsy
- Open wounds, skin infections, or skin breakdown in electrode contact areas
- Acute musculoskeletal injury (fracture, muscle tear, acute inflammation)
- Metal implants in EMS field area (may cause localised heating — physician clearance required)

## Relative Contraindications (physician clearance required)

- Cardiovascular disease (EMS increases cardiac demand equivalent to moderate exercise)
- Renal impairment (rhabdomyolysis risk with high-intensity sessions — creatine kinase monitoring may be required)
- Haemophilia or bleeding disorders
- Severe osteoporosis
- Recent surgery (within [X months])

4. [P4] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Confirm guest has eaten at least [X hours] before session (not immediately pre-session; not fully fasted for high-intensity).
5. [P5] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Assist guest into EMS undergarment and suit; dampen electrodes as required per manufacturer protocol; confirm full contact across all electrode areas.
6. [P6] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Brief guest: EMS sensation (vibration, contraction), trainer controls intensity at all times, guest to call out any discomfort or pain immediately.

### Section C — Session Delivery

7. [P7] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Begin session at low intensity; increase gradually per guest response and trainer protocol; first session maximum [X%] of device range.
8. [P8] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Guide guest through prescribed movement programme during stimulation; maintain active verbal communication throughout.
9. [P9] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Session duration: maximum [X minutes] — EMS is significantly more intense than equivalent conventional exercise; do not exceed.
10. [P10] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Terminate immediately if: pain (not standard contraction sensation), arrhythmia symptoms, fainting, skin burning, or guest request.

11. [P11] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Remove suit; inspect electrode contact areas for skin irritation or burns.
12. [P12] **[PLACEHOLDER]** — Advise guest: significant DOMS (delayed onset muscle soreness) is expected, especially after first sessions; hydrate well; avoid strenuous exercise for [X hours]; seek medical advice if dark urine occurs (rhabdomyolysis indicator).

## MEVZUAT Note

> **Tıbbi Cihaz Yönetmeliği** (Turkish Medical Devices Regulation): Article 5 and 10 require EMS devices to meet CE/MDR certification; Art. 83-86 (Piyasa gözetimi ve denetimi — Market surveillance and adverse event reporting) apply and Turkish Ministry of Health ÜTS (Ürün Takip Sistemi) registration; operator certification required. **Law No. 1219** (Turkish Medical Practice Law): Article 1 requires physician referral pathway for guest access to EMS. **KVKK Law No. 6698** (Personal Data Protection Law): Article 6 governs session records and adverse event documentation. **Law No. 6331** (Occupational Health and Safety Law): Articles 4, 10, 15 (Sağlık gözetimi — Health Surveillance), and 17 (Çalışanların eğitimi — Worker Training) mandate facility safety and trainer/guest safety protocols; trainer must be trained to recognise rhabdomyolysis risk and refer immediately.
