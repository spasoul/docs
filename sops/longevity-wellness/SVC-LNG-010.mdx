## Purpose

To safely administer ozone therapy — via rectal insufflation, ear insufflation, ozone sauna, or minor autohemotherapy — under strict physician supervision, to support immune modulation, antioxidant activity, and cellular oxygenation.

---

## Scope

Covers: rectal insufflation, ear insufflation, ozone cabinet/sauna (topical), and minor autohemotherapy (ozone blood treatment). Intravenous ozone (major autohemotherapy) is treated as a clinical procedure requiring additional physician protocols beyond this SOP's scope.

---

## Responsibility

[Physician] — prescribes modality, concentration, and volume; available on-site or immediately on-call during all sessions. [Clinical] — administers procedure under physician protocol.

---

## Absolute Contraindications

- Glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase (G6PD) deficiency — ozone causes haemolytic anaemia
- Pregnancy
- Active hyperthyroidism (ozone stimulates thyroid)
- Uncontrolled bleeding disorders or anticoagulation (autohemotherapy)
- Active haemorrhage
- Ozone allergy or severe sensitivity
- Inhalation of ozone gas — absolutely prohibited (pulmonary toxicity)
- Same-day HBOT without explicit physician approval (concurrent oxidative load)

## Relative Contraindications (physician clearance required)

- Controlled cardiovascular disease
- Autoimmune conditions (immune stimulation risk)
- Recent surgery (within [X weeks])
- History of pulmonary conditions (ensure no airway exposure to ozone gas)

## MEVZUAT Note

> **Law No. 1219** (Turkish Medical Practice Law): Articles 1 and 3 require physician prescription of ozone therapy and continuous physician supervision during treatment. **Tıbbi Cihaz Yönetmeliği** (Turkish Medical Devices Regulation): Article 5 and 10 require ozone generators to meet CE/MDR certification; Art. 83-86 (Piyasa gözetimi ve denetimi — Market surveillance and adverse event reporting) apply and Turkish Ministry of Health ÜTS (Ürün Takip Sistemi) registration. **KVKK Law No. 6698** (Personal Data Protection Law): Article 6 governs clinical treatment records and adverse event documentation. **Law No. 6331** (Occupational Health and Safety Law): Articles 4, 10, 11, and 17 (Çalışanların eğitimi — Worker Training) mandate facility safety, ozone gas handling protocols (ambient exposure limits), and emergency response procedures.
