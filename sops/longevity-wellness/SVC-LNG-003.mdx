## Purpose

To safely administer intravenous therapy — including nutrient infusions, hydration protocols, and NAD+ infusions — under physician prescription and clinical supervision, maximising efficacy while preventing adverse reactions.

---

## Scope

Covers all IV infusion protocols: hydration, vitamin/mineral infusions, glutathione, and NAD+ protocols. Excludes blood products and chemotherapy (separate medical protocols required).

---

## Responsibility

[Physician] — prescribes formulation, dose, and rate; present or on immediate call during administration. [Clinical] — qualified nurse or paramedic who inserts IV line and monitors infusion.

---

## Absolute Contraindications

- Known allergy or previous adverse reaction to any infusion component
- Active infection or cellulitis at intended IV insertion site
- Severe cardiac failure or fluid overload (fluid infusions)
- Renal failure (NAD+ and high-dose vitamin infusions — nephrology clearance required)
- Uncontrolled hypertension
- Pregnancy (formulation-dependent — physician decision required)

## Relative Contraindications (physician clearance required)

- Controlled cardiac or renal disease
- Diabetes mellitus (glucose monitoring during infusion required)
- Anticoagulant or antiplatelet therapy
- History of phlebitis, thrombosis, or difficult venous access
- Mild renal impairment (dose adjustment required)
- NAD+ in patients on psychoactive medications (interaction screening required)

## MEVZUAT Note

> **Law No. 1219** (Turkish Medical Practice Law): Articles 1 and 3 require physician prescription of IV therapy and continuous physician supervision during administration; clinical staff (nurse/paramedic) must be licensed. **Tıbbi Cihaz Yönetmeliği** (Turkish Medical Devices Regulation): Article 5 and 10 require IV infusion equipment (cannulae, pumps, lines) to meet CE/MDR certification; Art. 83-86 (Piyasa gözetimi ve denetimi — Market surveillance and adverse event reporting) apply. **KVKK Law No. 6698** (Personal Data Protection Law): Article 6 governs all clinical treatment records and adverse event documentation. **Law No. 6331** (Occupational Health and Safety Law): Articles 4, 10, 11, and 17 (Çalışanların eğitimi — Worker Training) mandate facility safety, clinical staff safety protocols, and emergency response procedures (anaphylaxis protocols).
