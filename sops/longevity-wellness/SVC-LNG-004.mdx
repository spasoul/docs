## Purpose

To safely deliver red and near-infrared light therapy sessions to support cellular energy production, skin health, inflammation reduction, and recovery, using calibrated PBM devices at evidence-based parameters.

---

## Scope

Covers whole-body or targeted photobiomodulation panel sessions. Covers red light (630–700 nm) and near-infrared (800–1100 nm) wavelengths. Laser PBM devices require separate clinical protocol.

---

## Responsibility

[Specialist] — certified PBM operator present for session setup and monitoring. Guest may be unattended during session if no contraindications and safety protocols confirmed; operator remains within call distance.

---

## Absolute Contraindications

- Direct irradiation of eyes (risk of retinal damage)
- Active malignancy in treatment area (light may stimulate tumour tissue)
- Photosensitising medications (porphyrins, tetracyclines, fluoroquinolones, retinoids — photosensitivity risk)
- Active photosensitive skin conditions (lupus, porphyria)
- Pregnancy — irradiation of the abdomen

## Relative Contraindications (physician clearance required)

- History of skin cancer (treatment area exclusion; physician decision)
- Epilepsy (flicker-sensitive — check device flicker rate)
- Thyroid conditions (avoid direct irradiation of thyroid area)
- Implanted light-activated medical devices

## MEVZUAT Note

> **Tıbbi Cihaz Yönetmeliği** (Turkish Medical Devices Regulation): Article 5 and 10 require PBM devices claiming medical benefits to meet CE/MDR certification; Art. 83-86 (Piyasa gözetimi ve denetimi — Market surveillance and adverse event reporting) apply and Turkish Ministry of Health ÜTS (Ürün Takip Sistemi) registration; laser devices require additional Class classification compliance. **Law No. 1219** (Turkish Medical Practice Law): Article 1 requires physician referral pathway for guest access to PBM. **KVKK Law No. 6698** (Personal Data Protection Law): Article 6 governs session records and adverse event documentation. **Law No. 6331** (Occupational Health and Safety Law): Articles 4, 10, and 17 (Çalışanların eğitimi — Worker Training) mandate facility safety and operator safety protocols for optical radiation equipment.
