### Purpose

Establish minimum facility and service standards for the outdoor pool and beach area, define the daily setup and maintenance routine, and specify how pool attendants deliver guest arrival service and coordinate poolside food and beverage delivery with the F&B Department.

### Scope

1. [S1] Applies to Pool Attendants, Beach Attendants, and the Pool Supervisor responsible for the outdoor pool deck and beach area.
2. [S2] Covers pool water condition, pool deck, lounger zones, beach area, poolside amenities, showers, and restrooms in or adjacent to the pool/beach area.
3. [S3] Food and beverage delivery (waiter service, complimentary sorbets, water, reef-safe sunscreen) is the responsibility of the **F&B Department**. Pool Attendants coordinate the service but do not execute F&B tasks independently.
4. [S4] Pool water testing, chemical dosing, and technical pool maintenance are the responsibility of Engineering/Maintenance in accordance with [PROPERTY POOL MAINTENANCE PROTOCOL]. This SOP governs cleanliness and operational readiness checks only.

### Responsibility

1. [R1] **Pool / Beach Attendant** — completes daily setup, maintains cleanliness throughout shift, delivers guest arrival service, coordinates F&B service requests.
2. [R2] **Pool Supervisor / Fitness Centre Manager** — oversees opening checklist compliance, escalates facility faults, liaises with F&B Manager on poolside service readiness.
3. [R3] **F&B Department** — executes all food and beverage service at the pool and beach, including waiter service, complimentary water, sorbets, and sunscreen, as flagged at steps P.13–P.18.
4. [R4] **Engineering / Maintenance** — responsible for pool water quality, chemical balance, pump systems, and all technical infrastructure.

### Procedure

**Opening & Facility Check**

1. [P1] Verify that the pool is clean, at the appropriate temperature, and well maintained (tiling intact, underwater lighting functional, pool edges clean). If any water quality, temperature, or structural issue is observed, notify Engineering before opening the pool to guests. [LQA 19.19]
2. [P2] Confirm that **depth signs** are present, visible, and legible at all points of the pool. Do not open the pool if any depth sign is missing or damaged — replace or report to Engineering immediately. [LQA 19.20]
3. [P3] Inspect the entire pool surround and beach area — remove all debris, litter, and foreign items. The area around the pool must be clean and completely free of debris at opening. [LQA 19.21; Forbes Pool/Beach 5.2]
4. [P4] Inspect all loungers, side tables, and umbrellas: [LQA 19.22; Forbes Pool/Beach 5.2]
   a. [SOP-SVC-MVT-002.P.4a] Ample quantity must be available and evenly arranged to ensure adequate space and privacy for guests.
   b. [SOP-SVC-MVT-002.P.4b] All items must be clean and in good repair. Remove any damaged or soiled item from service before opening.
   c. [SOP-SVC-MVT-002.P.4c] All furniture must be of exceptional quality, consistent with the property's luxury standard.
5. [P5] Verify that the separate shower(s) designated for pool/beach use are clean, functional, and well stocked with soap or shower gel. Report any fault to Engineering immediately. [LQA 19.24]
6. [P6] Check that poolside restrooms (if located in or adjacent to the pool/beach area) are clean, fully stocked, and well maintained. [Forbes Pool/Beach 5.2]

**Amenity Setup**

7. [P7] Stock the towel station with ample clean towels before the pool opens. Towels must be automatically available to guests — do not wait for guests to ask. [LQA 19.23; Forbes Pool/Beach 5.2]
8. [P8] Ensure adequate sun protection measures are in place throughout the pool/beach area, including:
   a. [SOP-SVC-MVT-002.P.8a] Shade options (umbrellas, parasols, or covered zones) available and positioned for guest use.
   b. [SOP-SVC-MVT-002.P.8b] Sun protection products available at the designated amenity station in coordination with F&B. [Forbes Pool/Beach 5.2; LQA 19.26 — see P.15]

**Guest Arrival & Lounger Setup**

9. [P9] Upon guest arrival at the pool or beach, escort the guest to a suitable lounger. Set-up assistance must be provided or offered proactively — do not wait for the guest to request it. [Forbes Pool/Beach 5.2]
10. [P10] Set up the lounger for the guest: arrange towels, confirm umbrella angle for shade, and ensure side table is in place. [LQA 19.25; Forbes Pool/Beach 5.2]
11. [P11] Offer complimentary water to the guest upon set-up. [LQA 19.25]
    > **F&B coordination:** Complimentary water is delivered by the F&B Department. At this step, the Pool Attendant either presents water already pre-positioned at the station or immediately signals the F&B poolside server to deliver. Do not delay the offer pending F&B availability — confirm the coordination protocol with the F&B Manager at start of shift.

**Complimentary Poolside Services**

12. [P12] The following complimentary services are offered to guests at the outdoor pool in coordination with F&B: [LQA 19.26]
    a. [SOP-SVC-MVT-002.P.12a] Cleaning of sunglasses — offered proactively during guest check-in at the lounger or on request.
    b. [SOP-SVC-MVT-002.P.12b] Complimentary sorbets or refreshments — offered during service rounds. *(F&B executes delivery.)*
    c. [SOP-SVC-MVT-002.P.12c] Reef-safe sunscreen — available at the poolside amenity station or offered by F&B/Pool Attendant.
    > **F&B coordination:** Sorbets, food, and beverage items are the responsibility of the F&B Department to prepare, source, and deliver. Pool Attendants are responsible for offering services to guests and immediately notifying the F&B poolside server. The specific complimentary service offering (e.g., sorbet flavors, sunscreen brand) is determined by the F&B Manager and may vary seasonally.

**Waiter Service**

13. [P13] Waiter service must be available and clearly communicated to guests at the outdoor pool and/or beach. [LQA 19.27]
    > **F&B coordination:** Waiter service is fully executed by the F&B Department. Pool Attendants direct guests to the [POOLSIDE F&B SERVICE METHOD] (e.g., signal flag, dedicated server station, QR menu) and may relay orders to the F&B team if needed. All food and beverage orders, preparation, and delivery are F&B responsibility.
14. [P14] Drinks must be delivered promptly — **within 10 minutes of order**. [Forbes Pool/Beach 5.2]
    > **F&B coordination:** The 10-minute delivery standard applies to the F&B Department. Pool Attendants monitor and escalate to the F&B poolside supervisor if a guest's order has not been delivered within the timeframe.
15. [P15] A poolside menu must be available and offered to guests, featuring a variety of food and beverage options including nutritionally focused choices and accommodation for dietary restrictions. [Forbes Pool/Beach 5.2]
    > **F&B coordination:** Menu design, content, pricing, and physical condition are the responsibility of the F&B Department. Pool Attendants ensure menus are present at loungers or readily available — report any missing, damaged, or outdated menus to the F&B poolside supervisor immediately.

**During Operating Hours — Maintenance**

16. [P16] Maintain pool deck and beach in a well-organised, professional appearance throughout all operating hours. Vacated loungers must be reset (towels collected, cushions/pads arranged, side tables cleared) **promptly** — do not allow a used, uncleared lounger to remain visible to incoming guests. [Forbes Pool/Beach 5.2; LQA 19.21]
17. [P17] Collect soiled towels discreetly throughout the shift. Store used towels out of guest sightlines. Replenish clean towel stock continuously so the station is never empty. [Forbes Pool/Beach 5.2; LQA 19.23]
18. [P18] Conduct a full pool deck and beach sweep at regular intervals — remove litter, collect used serviceware, and reset furniture as needed.
19. [P19] Apply the Forbes cross-cutting service-tone standards at all guest interactions: maintain alert posture, make eye contact, smile, use the guest's name when known, anticipate needs, offer a polite closing. → See SOP-SVC-MVT-001 P.10–P.11.

### Checklist

1. [C1] Pool clean, at correct temperature, well maintained; Engineering notified of any fault.
2. [C2] All depth signs present, visible, and legible.
3. [C3] Pool surround and beach free of all debris.
4. [C4] All loungers, side tables, and umbrellas clean, in good repair, and arranged for adequate guest space and privacy.
5. [C5] Pool/beach shower(s) clean, functional, and stocked.
6. [C6] Poolside restrooms (if present) clean, stocked, and well maintained.
7. [C7] Towel station stocked; towels automatically available to guests.
8. [C8] Shade options in place; sun protection products available.
9. [C9] F&B poolside server briefed on complimentary services (water, sorbets, sunscreen) and waiter service start time.
10. [C10] Poolside menus present, clean, and current.
11. [C11] Vacated loungers reset promptly; soiled towels collected discreetly.

### Brand Notes

- **No legacy SOP baseline.** Authored from scratch against LQA 19.19–19.27 and Forbes Pool/Beach 5.2. No Template-SOPs.md equivalent exists for this stream.
- **F&B Department coordination (LQA 19.25–19.27):** Per Zeki's decision (2026-04-12), standards 19.25, 19.26, and 19.27 are included as standard steps without "resort only" gating. F&B Department is the executing party for water, sorbets, sunscreen delivery, waiter service, and poolside menus. Pool Attendants are responsible for triggering and monitoring the service. Cross-department coordination protocol must be established at property rollout.
- **[POOLSIDE F&B SERVICE METHOD]** — property specifies how guests signal for service (e.g., flag system, QR code, dedicated server).
- **[PROPERTY POOL MAINTENANCE PROTOCOL]** — Engineering's water quality and chemical management protocol, referenced at S.4.
- **Forbes 10-minute drink delivery standard (P.14):** Forbes Pool/Beach 5.2 standard. Applied as a measurable KPI for F&B Department — Pool Attendants monitor and escalate breaches.
- **LQA 19.26 complimentary services:** "cleaning sunglasses, sorbets, reef-safe sunscreen, etc." — the "etc." gives flexibility. Property may add to this list at rollout. Reef-safe sunscreen replaces any standard sunscreen per LQA's sustainability intent.
- **Forbes "distinctive and promotes a sense of place" standard:** ambient/design standard for the pool setting — not an operational step but should be noted by property design team at rollout.
