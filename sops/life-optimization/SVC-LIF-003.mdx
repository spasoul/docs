## Purpose

To deliver personalised movement and exercise guidance — covering cardiovascular fitness, strength training, mobility/flexibility, and daily activity patterns — to support musculoskeletal health, metabolic function, fall prevention, and longevity outcomes beyond structured fitness sessions.

---

## Scope

Covers movement assessment, personalised exercise programme design (complementing FIT department group classes), daily activity coaching, and take-home movement plans. Does not cover rehabilitation of acute injury or post-surgical rehabilitation (physiotherapy referral required), nor replace FIT department structured classes (coordinates with them).

---

## Responsibility

[Coach] — certified exercise physiologist or fitness professional; conducts movement assessment, designs programmes, and delivers coaching. Physician referral required if musculoskeletal injury, cardiovascular limitation, or fall risk identified.


<Warning>
**Absolute Contraindications** — Do not proceed under any circumstances:
- Acute musculoskeletal injury (fracture, acute tendon/ligament tear — physiotherapy required first)
- Unstable cardiovascular condition (uncontrolled arrhythmia, acute heart failure, recent cardiac event within [X weeks])
- Acute systemic illness or fever
</Warning>


<Note>
**Relative Contraindications** — Physician clearance required:
- Osteoporosis or osteopenia (impact and load restrictions)
- Joint replacement (within [X months] — range-of-motion limitations)
- Controlled cardiovascular disease (heart rate and exertion limits defined by physician)
- Pregnancy (trimester-specific exercise modifications)
- Chronic pain conditions (fibromyalgia, chronic low back pain — exercise type and intensity adaptation)
</Note>
