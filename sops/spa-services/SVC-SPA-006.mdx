### Purpose

Ensure the health and safety of a pregnant guest and her unborn child by defining which treatments are prohibited, which are safe, and which become available after the first trimester — with clear guidance for both reception (booking) and therapist (delivery) roles.

### Scope

1. [S1] Applies to all spa receptionists (at booking) and therapists (at delivery).
2. [S2] Covers the complete pregnancy treatment matrix: prohibited treatments, safe-anytime treatments, and first-trimester-restricted treatments.
3. [S3] Oil/product substitutions for pregnant guests are specified in this SOP and override any default oil selection in SOP-SVC-SPA-001.

### Responsibility

1. [R1] **Spa Receptionist** — identifies pregnancy at booking or check-in, explains restrictions, and books only permitted treatments.
2. [R2] **Therapist** — verifies pregnancy status before treatment, applies product substitutions, and adapts technique per this SOP.
3. [R3] **Spa Director** — approves any exception requests and handles guest complaints related to pregnancy restrictions.

### Procedure

**Initial Communication**

1. [P1] Inform the guest that treatments are restricted during pregnancy. The guest will most likely already understand the restrictions.
2. [P2] The restrictions may seem excessive, but it is always best to err on the side of caution.

**NEVER-SELL List (Prohibited Throughout Pregnancy)**

3. [P3] The following treatments must never be sold or performed on a pregnant guest:
   (a) Baths — can stimulate early childbirth.
   (b) Body Wraps.
   (c) Any type of foot treatment, including foot exfoliations and foot massage.
   (d) Any treatment which applies or generates heat onto the body.

**Safe Treatments (All Trimesters)**

4. [P4] The following are safe throughout pregnancy:
   (a) Any type of facial — but facials that normally include extra foot massage must substitute hand or scalp massage in place of the foot work.
   (b) Body Polishes and Body Scrubs — avoiding the feet area.
   (c) Any type of hand treatment.
   (d) Any type of scalp treatment.

**Safe AFTER First Trimester**

5. [P5] The following become available after the first trimester:
   (a) All facials (without the foot-massage substitution restriction).
   (b) Pregnant Massage — using BASE OIL only, not Aromatherapy Oil. A good all-round substitute product is Olive Oil or Grape Seed Oil.

**Therapist Verification**

6. [P6] Before beginning any treatment, the therapist must verify the guest's pregnancy status and trimester from the consultation card. Cross-check against the permitted treatment lists above.
7. [P7] If the guest's gestation is unclear or falls near the trimester boundary, err on the side of the more restrictive category.

### Checklist

1. [C1] Pregnancy identified at booking or check-in.
2. [C2] Restrictions explained to guest.
3. [C3] Only permitted treatments booked.
4. [C4] NEVER-SELL list enforced (no baths, wraps, foot treatments, heat treatments).
5. [C5] Foot-massage substitution applied to facials (hand or scalp massage).
6. [C6] Base oil used for post-first-trimester massage (no aromatherapy oil).
7. [C7] Trimester verified from consultation card before treatment.

None required.

### Brand notes

- "Olive Oil or Grape Seed Oil" is the source recommendation for base oil substitution. Properties may specify their preferred carrier oil brand in a brand overlay, provided it is fragrance-free and non-aromatherapy.
- Properties offering prenatal-specific treatment menus should align those menus with the permitted categories in this SOP.
