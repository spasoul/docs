### Purpose

Protect guest privacy and dignity throughout every treatment by defining mandatory draping techniques, anatomical coverage rules, and privacy protocols that may never be lowered — even at the guest's request.

### Scope

1. [S1] Applies to all therapists performing any body treatment, massage, or wet treatment.
2. [S2] Covers draping standards, anatomical sub-procedures, turning technique, mid-treatment privacy transitions, and the no-naked-guest rule.
3. [S3] Guest disrobing privacy (therapist leaving the room, knocking protocol) is initiated in SOP-GDN-CUR-002; this SOP governs all draping once the guest is on the treatment table.

### Responsibility

1. [R1] **Therapist** — executes all draping procedures for every treatment without exception.
2. [R2] **Spa Supervisor** — conducts periodic draping-compliance observations.
3. [R3] **Spa Director** — handles any guest complaint or incident related to draping violations.

### Procedure

**Foundational Rules**

1. [P1] Only the body part being worked on is to be uncovered, while breasts and genital regions will be covered at all times.
2. [P2] The therapist must audibly confirm the treatment before performing it (e.g., "Today you will be having a full body Swedish massage") and briefly describe the procedures.
3. [P3] Even if a guest requests some alternate form of draping, draping procedures must follow the techniques and standards outlined in this SOP.
4. [P4] Never lower these draping standards at the request of the guest.
5. [P5] The treatment service will not be provided to naked guests / without towels.

**General Draping Standards**

6. [P6] A top sheet must always be used (or an extra-large body towel for body treatments / hot stone).
7. [P7] Women's breasts will never be uncovered or massaged.
8. [P8] Re-drape worked areas immediately after completing work on that section.
9. [P9] All draping movements must be seamless, efficient, with no invasive movements.

**Wet Treatment Attire**

10. [P10] For wet treatments, advise the guest regarding swimsuit or disposable garment. For one-piece swimsuits with shoulder straps, ask the female guest if she is comfortable slipping her arms out while holding the front, to allow back work.

**Bolster & Eye Comfort**

11. [P11] For massage, place a bolster or rolled towel under the guest's ankles while prone and under the knees while supine, or adjust the treatment bench if applicable. [LQA 21.25]
12. [P12] Offer an eye pad or face towel when the guest turns onto their back (not applicable for facials). [LQA 21.26]

**Undraping the Back**

13. [P13] Fold the sheet at the sacrum, tuck in at the hips, and anchor with the hand. For gluteal access, use a diagonal fold. Never expose genitals or midline inferior to the sacrum.

**Undraping Lower Limbs (Prone)**

14. [P14] Tuck the sheet under the leg, roll/gather, and tuck midway between the back of the knee and the gluteal fold.

**Turning the Guest**

15. [P15] The therapist stands at one side, reaches across to create tent space, looks toward the feet, and lowers the sheet. The therapist's back must be to any mirrors in the room. In couples' massage, position to prevent cross-view between guests.

**Draping the Chest / Uncovering the Abdomen**

16. [P16] Maintain sufficient breast cover at all times. Ask for permission before performing abdomen work on ANY guest. Uncover the abdomen only after the chest is properly draped. Fold to the hip bone; never expose inferior to the hip bone.
17. [P17] For pectoralis work on a female client, only the superior edge may be exposed with sufficient draping. Working the entire pectoralis major muscle is not appropriate with female guests in the spa setting.

**Anterior Lower Limbs**

18. [P18] Use a figure-4 tuck for ROM stretches with genital protection; apply "diaper" draping technique as needed.

**Upper Limb Stretching**

19. [P19] Ensure breast tissue remains covered at all times during upper limb stretching.

**Mid-Treatment Privacy Transitions**

20. [P20] If the guest needs to undress mid-treatment (e.g., scrub → shower → massage transition), hold a towel in front of the therapist to shield the guest.
21. [P21] Show changing facilities (including jewelry box) within the treatment room if the guest has not yet changed. Display the "room occupied" sign.
22. [P22] Knock before entering. The therapist will ensure that the room occupied sign is displayed.

**Privacy During Disrobing**

23. [P23] Privacy must be given during disrobing and throughout the treatment. The guest must be appropriately covered with towels or sheets at all times. [LQA 21.22, 21.27]

### Checklist

1. [C1] Treatment confirmed audibly before starting.
2. [C2] Top sheet or extra-large towel in place.
3. [C3] Breasts and genital regions covered at all times.
4. [C4] Worked areas re-draped immediately.
5. [C5] Bolster/rolled towel placed (ankles prone, knees supine).
6. [C6] Eye pad/face towel offered when turned supine.
7. [C7] Permission obtained before abdomen work.
8. [C8] Mirror rule and couples cross-view prevention applied during turns.
9. [C9] Room occupied sign displayed during privacy transitions.
10. [C10] No treatment provided to naked/un-toweled guest.

### Brand notes

- The pectoralis restriction ("not appropriate … in the spa setting") was originally brand-specific. It is retained as a universal standard due to its conservative safety posture. Properties offering sports-massage programs may petition the Spa Director for a documented exception with additional consent protocols.
- "Diaper" draping is the industry term; properties may use alternative terminology in guest-facing materials but must train therapists on this technique by name.
