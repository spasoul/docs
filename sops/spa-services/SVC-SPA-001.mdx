### Purpose

Ensure every guest receives a consistent, personalized treatment opening — from therapist readiness and hand hygiene through oil/scent selection, environment confirmation, and the mandated pressure-check sequence — so the treatment begins on time, at the right pressure, in a comfortable environment.

### Scope

1. [S1] Applies to all therapists performing any spa treatment.
2. [S2] Covers the sequence from the moment the therapist is ready to begin through the first five minutes of active treatment.
3. [S3] Guest collection, orientation, and draping are covered in SOP-GDN-CUR-002 and SOP-SVC-SPA-002 respectively. This SOP begins after the guest is positioned and draped.

### Responsibility

1. [R1] **Therapist** — executes the full introduction and pressure-check sequence for every treatment.
2. [R2] **Spa Supervisor** — monitors compliance through periodic observation and guest feedback.
3. [R3] **Spa Receptionist** — communicates immediately if the therapist is not available at the booking time. [LQA 21.16, 21.17; cross-ref SOP-GDN-CUR-001 P.24]

### Procedure

**Therapist Readiness**

1. [P1] The therapist must be available as per the booking time. [LQA 21.16]
2. [P2] If the therapist is not available at the booking time, an apology must be offered to the guest and the guest must be assured that the treatment will not be cut short. [LQA 21.17]
3. [P3] Wash hands visibly before and after each treatment. The guest must have visual or verbal indication of hand washing. [LQA 21.23]

**Consultation Card Review**

4. [P4] Refer to the consultation card for the guest's name and any additional information that can be used to personalize the experience.
5. [P5] Refer to the consultation card for any potential concerns and treatment specifics — existing injuries, desired pressure, allergies to certain types of oils, medications.
6. [P6] For massage, ask if the guest wants to relax or work out any sore or tired areas.

**Oil / Scent Selection**

7. [P7] For signature treatments, advise the guest of the oil/scent to be used. For non-signature massage treatments, give the guest a choice of oils/scent. [LQA 21.21]

**Environment Confirmation**

8. [P8] Ask if the music and volume is suitable. Soothing/relaxing music must be played at an appropriate volume, or natural environmental sounds where applicable. [LQA 21.30]
9. [P9] Ask if the air temperature is suitable. [LQA 21.24]

**Treatment Start & Duration**

10. [P10] The treatment must begin and end on time and last for the full duration. [LQA 21.31]
11. [P11] The treatment must be free of any interruptions and outside noise. [LQA 21.29]

**Pressure Check (Massage Treatments)**

12. [P12] Within five (5) minutes of starting the treatment: (a) ask if the pressure is suitable; (b) if desired, adjust the pressure or massage style according to the guest's wishes; (c) once adjusted, verify (seconds after) that the correct tempo / pressure has been achieved.
13. [P13] Confirm that the pressure is comfortable for the guest on a second occasion during the treatment. [LQA 21.28]

### Checklist

1. [C1] Therapist available at booking time.
2. [C2] Hands washed visibly before treatment.
3. [C3] Consultation card reviewed (injuries, allergies, medications, pressure preference).
4. [C4] Oil/scent advised (signature) or choice offered (non-signature).
5. [C5] Music and temperature confirmed with guest.
6. [C6] Treatment started on time and ran full duration.
7. [C7] No interruptions during treatment.
8. [C8] Pressure checked within 5 minutes of treatment start.
9. [C9] Pressure confirmed a second time during treatment.
10. [C10] Hands washed after treatment.

None required.
