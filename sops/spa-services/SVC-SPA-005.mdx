## Purpose

Handle guests who are or appear to be under the influence of alcohol, drugs, or medications in a discreet, dignified manner — prioritizing guest safety, therapist safety, and duty of care — while avoiding embarrassment and ensuring proper documentation regardless of outcome.

## Scope

1. [S1] Applies to all therapists, spa receptionists, and spa managers.
2. [S2] Covers identification, assessment, rescheduling, escalation, and documentation of intoxication incidents.
3. [S3] The cancellation charge waiver for intoxication cases overrides the standard fee tiers in SOP-GDN-CCG-003.

## Responsibility

1. [R1] **Therapist** — identifies signs of intoxication, conducts the initial discreet assessment, and recommends rescheduling.
2. [R2] **Spa Manager** — handles escalated situations, manages the guest interaction if the therapist is uncomfortable, and ensures the incident report is filed.
3. [R3] **Security** — is called immediately if the guest becomes unresponsive or out of control.

## Procedure

**Medical & Medication Check**

1. [P1] Check the consultation card for any medications the guest is taking.
2. [P2] Check for any illnesses. Some illnesses, especially disorders like Multiple Sclerosis, can make the person seem intoxicated when they are not. Please be discreet. If you are in doubt, speak to the manager.
3. [P3] Do not judge the guest or make fun of them.

**Assessment**

4. [P4] If a medical condition exists, ask the guest if the treatment is normally safe for them given that condition.
5. [P5] If no medical explanation is present, ask the guest if they have consumed any medication or intoxicating substances. Note: there are therapeutic/medical reasons to withhold treatment — spa treatments accelerate alcohol absorption causing nausea, discomfort, and dizziness. This applies even to legal drugs prescribed by a medical doctor.

**Rescheduling**

6. [P6] If the guest is intoxicated, recommend rescheduling the treatment. The cancellation charge does not apply in this case. If uncomfortable handling the situation, call the manager.

**Mandatory Reporting**

7. [P7] If you successfully handle the guest, you must still inform the Spa Manager and a report must be completed in case the guest files a complaint.

**Safety Escalation**

8. [P8] If at any time the guest is not responding to you or is out of control, leave the room and call the manager immediately — your safety is our first priority. In this case, you must also call security.

<Accordion title="Checklist" icon="clipboard-check">
1. [C1] Consultation card checked for medications and conditions.
2. [C2] Discreet assessment conducted (medical vs. intoxication).
3. [C3] Guest treated with dignity — no judgment or ridicule.
4. [C4] Rescheduling offered with cancellation fee waived.
5. [C5] Spa Manager informed regardless of outcome.
6. [C6] Written incident report filed.
7. [C7] Security called if guest unresponsive or out of control.
</Accordion>
