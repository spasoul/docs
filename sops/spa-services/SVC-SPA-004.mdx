## Purpose

Provide VIP guests with a heightened, anticipatory spa experience — from pre-arrival preparation through personal welcome and treatment delivery — while ensuring non-VIP guests are never neglected or made to feel secondary.

## Scope

1. [S1] Applies to the Spa Manager/Supervisor, assigned therapists, and all spa staff present during a VIP visit.
2. [S2] Covers the sequence from VIP pre-notification through treatment completion.
3. [S3] Standard arrival, treatment, and farewell SOPs (ARR-001, ARR-002, TRT-001, TRT-003) continue to apply; this SOP adds the VIP-specific overlay.

## Responsibility

1. [R1] **Spa Manager / Supervisor** — pre-notifies therapists, personally welcomes the VIP, inspects the treatment room.
2. [R2] **Assigned Therapist** — prepares the room in advance, stands by for the VIP, delivers the treatment to the highest standard.
3. [R3] **All Spa Staff** — maintain equal service quality for all other guests during VIP visits.

## Procedure

**Pre-Notification**

1. [P1] The Spa Manager pre-notifies therapists of the incoming VIP guest, including name, room number, arrival time, treatment booked, and any special requirements.

**Room Preparation**

2. [P2] The treatments for a VIP guest should be prepared fifteen (15) minutes in advance before the start time.
3. [P3] The therapist who is on duty should stand by the [VIP WAITING AREA] with a cold towel and welcome tea five (5) minutes before the guest's arrival time.
4. [P4] The Spa Manager / Supervisor checks the treatment room personally before the VIP enters.

**Welcome**

5. [P5] The Spa Manager / Supervisor welcomes the VIP personally.
6. [P6] Use the guest's name throughout. Offer a cold towel and welcome tea. Welcome the guest in the required area (lobby / guest room) when necessary.
7. [P7] Confirm VIP information: name, room, arrival time, treatment, special requirements. Verify equipment readiness and sanitation.

**During Treatment**

8. [P8] Pay close attention to the VIP's needs and concerns. Maintain the highest standards throughout the treatment.
9. [P9] VIP guests are never asked for autographs when receiving a treatment.

**Non-VIP Guest Protection**

10. [P10] Non-VIP guests should also be taken care of when a VIP guest is around. Non-VIP guests should never feel like they are not receiving a service or are being ignored.

<Accordion title="Checklist" icon="clipboard-check">
1. [C1] Therapist pre-notified of VIP details.
2. [C2] Treatment room prepared 15 minutes in advance.
3. [C3] Therapist standing by with cold towel and tea 5 minutes before arrival.
4. [C4] Manager/Supervisor personally inspected room and welcomed VIP.
5. [C5] No autograph requests made.
6. [C6] Non-VIP guest service levels maintained.
</Accordion>

- KVKK note: VIP identity is sensitive personal data. Limit disclosure of VIP status to staff with a direct operational need.
