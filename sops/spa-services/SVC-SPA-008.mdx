## Purpose

Establish a clear, firm policy that in-room massage or treatment services are not offered — ensuring quality, safety, and hygiene standards can only be guaranteed within the spa environment.

## Scope

1. [S1] Applies to all spa reception staff, therapists, and spa managers.
2. [S2] Covers the refusal protocol and the guest-redirect script when an in-room treatment is requested.

## Responsibility

1. [R1] **Spa Receptionist** — declines the in-room request, explains the rationale, and offers the spa treatment room alternative.
2. [R2] **Spa Manager** — handles escalated requests or guest complaints related to this policy.

## Procedure

**Policy Statement**

1. [P1] We kindly maintain our quality of service and standards by not offering in-room massage or treatment services.

**Guest Request Handling**

2. [P2] If a guest requests an in-room treatment, explain that quality and safety standards cannot be guaranteed outside the spa environment.
3. [P3] Offer the spa treatment room as the alternative. Assist with booking and provide escort details per SOP-GDN-CUR-001.
4. [P4] If the guest insists, escalate to the Spa Manager. The Spa Manager reiterates the policy and offers additional alternatives (e.g., adjusted timing, preferred therapist, complimentary amenity).

**No Exceptions**

5. [P5] This policy applies to all guests, including VIP guests. No exceptions without written approval from the Spa Director and the General Manager.

<Accordion title="Checklist" icon="clipboard-check">
1. [C1] In-room request declined with explanation.
2. [C2] Spa treatment room offered as alternative.
3. [C3] Escalated to Spa Manager if guest insists.
4. [C4] No in-room treatment performed without dual written approval (Spa Director + GM).
</Accordion>


<Accordion title="Brand Notes" icon="building">
- This is a hard ban reaffirmed in the most recent review cycle (Sep 2024). Properties considering an in-suite treatment program must obtain corporate-level approval and develop a dedicated SOP with enhanced safety, hygiene, and worker-safety protocols before any exception is granted.
- Worker safety rationale: therapists in unsupervised guest rooms lack the safety infrastructure (panic buttons, colleague proximity, controlled environment) available in the spa.
</Accordion>
