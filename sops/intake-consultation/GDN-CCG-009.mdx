## Purpose

Maintain professionalism, productivity, and the spa's tranquil environment by restricting the use of personal electronic devices during working hours. Electronic devices transmit electromagnetic radiation which causes stress on a cellular level — this directly opposes the benefits of spa treatments which aim to de-stress and rebalance health and energy.

## Scope

1. [S1] Applies to all spa employees (reception, therapists, coordinators, attendants) during working hours within the spa area.
2. [S2] Covers personal electronic devices; property-issued devices used for operational purposes are excluded.
3. [S3] Guest device policies (e.g., photography restrictions in treatment areas) are covered by the property's general guest-device policy and are outside this SOP.

## Responsibility

1. [R1] **All Spa Employees** — comply with this policy during working hours.
2. [R2] **Spa Reception Manager** — enforces the policy and addresses violations.
3. [R3] **Spa Director** — approves any exceptions and handles repeated violations.

## Procedure

**Prohibited Devices**

1. [P1] The use of personal electronic devices — including mobile phones, personal tablets, personal laptops (except for training purposes), and portable music systems — is not permitted during working hours in the spa area.
2. [P2] Personal devices must be stored in staff lockers or designated storage areas during shifts. Devices must be set to silent mode.

**Manager Exception**

3. [P3] Only spa managers may use a mobile phone in the spa area, and only for operational purposes (e.g., coordinating with other departments, emergency communication).

**Break-Time Use**

4. [P4] Personal devices may be used during designated break times in staff break areas only — not in any guest-facing or treatment area.

**Violations**

5. [P5] First violation: verbal reminder and coaching by the Spa Reception Manager.
6. [P6] Repeated violations: documented per the property's disciplinary procedures and escalated to the Spa Director.

1. [C1] No personal devices visible or audible in guest-facing areas during operating hours.
2. [C2] Devices stored in staff lockers during shifts.
3. [C3] Manager device use limited to operational purposes.
</Accordion>
