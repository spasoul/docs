### Purpose

Define the minimum facility standards, daily maintenance routine, and amenity provision for all tennis and squash courts, ensuring courts are clean, safe, fully equipped, and ready for guest use at all times.

### Scope

1. [S1] Applies to Fitness Attendants and the Fitness Centre Manager responsible for court area maintenance.
2. [S2] Covers all tennis courts and squash courts, including nets, surfaces, lighting, and courtside amenities.
3. [S3] Court booking management and racket/equipment loan are covered in [PROPERTY COURT BOOKING & EQUIPMENT PROTOCOL] and are not within scope of this SOP.
4. [S4] Structural repair of court surfaces, net posts, or flood lighting is the responsibility of Engineering. This SOP governs cleanliness and operational readiness checks only.

### Responsibility

1. [R1] **Fitness Attendant** — completes daily court inspection and setup, maintains cleanliness throughout shift, replenishes courtside amenities.
2. [R2] **Fitness Centre Manager** — escalates any structural fault or safety concern to Engineering, ensures court opening standards are met before play begins.
3. [R3] **Engineering / Maintenance** — responsible for structural court maintenance, net post integrity, and flood lighting repair.

### Procedure

**Daily Court Inspection**

1. [P1] Inspect each tennis court before opening to guests: [LQA 19.28; LQA 19.29]
   a. [SOP-OPR-FAC-002.P.1a] Court surface must be clean and free of all debris (leaves, dust, standing water, foreign objects). Sweep or blow clear before play is permitted.
   b. [SOP-OPR-FAC-002.P.1b] Court surface must be well maintained — check for cracks, damaged areas, or uneven patches. Report any surface defect to Engineering immediately; restrict use of affected court until repaired.
   c. [SOP-OPR-FAC-002.P.1c] Court lines must be clearly defined and visible. If any line is faded or obscured, flag for repainting and notify the Fitness Centre Manager.
2. [P2] Inspect flood lighting on each court — all lights must be operational and provide adequate illumination for play. Report any failed fixture to Engineering with priority flag. [LQA 19.28]
3. [P3] Inspect the tennis court net(s): [LQA 19.30]
   a. [SOP-OPR-FAC-002.P.3a] Net must be at the correct regulation height and properly tensioned.
   b. [SOP-OPR-FAC-002.P.3b] Net, posts, and straps must be in good repair — no tears, fraying, or visible damage. Replace or report for replacement immediately if any defect is found.
4. [P4] Repeat steps P.1–P.3 for squash courts where applicable (surface, lighting, walls, glass panels if present).

**Courtside Amenity Setup**

5. [P5] Before courts open to guests, stock the courtside amenity station with: [LQA 19.31]
   a. [SOP-OPR-FAC-002.P.5a] **Drinking water** — non-plastic cups or non-plastic bottled water. Sufficient quantity for expected court usage.
   b. [SOP-OPR-FAC-002.P.5b] **Clean towels** — folded and presented at the courtside station.
6. [P6] Replenish drinking water and towels between court sessions or whenever stock is depleted. Never leave the amenity station empty during operating hours.

**During Operating Hours**

7. [P7] Inspect courts between sessions — collect used towels, remove any litter or debris, reset the amenity station.
8. [P8] If a structural issue or safety hazard is discovered during operations (e.g., net failure, cracked surface, failed flood light), immediately restrict access to the affected court and notify Engineering. Advise any waiting guests of the temporary closure and offer alternative arrangements where possible.
9. [P9] Log all court defects, fault reports, and Engineering notifications in the [MAINTENANCE LOG].

### Checklist

1. [C1] Court surfaces clean, free of debris, and well maintained; lines clearly defined.
2. [C2] Flood lighting fully operational on all courts.
3. [C3] Tennis net(s) at correct height, properly tensioned, and in good repair.
4. [C4] Courtside amenity station stocked: drinking water (non-plastic) and clean towels.
5. [C5] Any defect found logged and Engineering notified; affected court restricted from use if safety issue.

### Brand Notes

- **No legacy SOP baseline.** Authored from scratch against LQA 19.28–19.31. No Template-SOPs.md equivalent exists for this stream.
- **LQA 19.28 flood lighting:** standard states courts must be "flood lit" — interpreted as courts must have flood lighting available and operational, not that lights must be on at all times. Property operating hours and ambient light conditions govern when lighting is activated.
- **LQA 19.31 "drinking water and towels available":** applied at courtside station (P.5). Non-plastic vessels per property sustainability standard (consistent with LQA 19.14 gym water standard).
- **[PROPERTY COURT BOOKING & EQUIPMENT PROTOCOL]** — property-specific; covers racket loan, court reservations, ball provision.
- **[MAINTENANCE LOG]** — property specifies platform/format. Consistent with SOP-OPR-FAC-001 and SOP-SVC-MVT-001.
- **Squash courts:** LQA 19.28–19.31 references "tennis/squash courts" jointly. Step P.4 extends the inspection framework to squash. Any squash-specific standards (wall markings, glass panel integrity) should be added at rollout as property-specific annexes.
