### Purpose

Define the minimum safety, cleanliness, and operational standards for all heat experiences — sauna, steam room, and jacuzzi — ensuring these facilities meet hygiene requirements, display mandatory safety information, and are maintained at appropriate temperatures throughout operating hours.

### Scope

1. [S1] Applies to Fitness Attendants and the Fitness Centre Manager responsible for wet and heat area maintenance.
2. [S2] Covers sauna, steam room(s), and jacuzzi, including safety signage, temperature instruments, towel provision, and general cleanliness.
3. [S3] Technical maintenance of heating elements, steam generators, jacuzzi pump systems, and water chemistry are the responsibility of Engineering. This SOP governs operational readiness, cleanliness, and safety checks only.
4. [S4] MEVZUAT (Turkish law) compliance requirements for heat experiences — including maximum exposure times, ventilation standards, and medical contraindication notices — will be integrated at Phase 6 (Compliance matrix close-out). Where MEVZUAT requirements conflict with this SOP, MEVZUAT takes precedence.

### Responsibility

1. [R1] **Fitness Attendant** — completes daily inspection, verifies safety signage and instruments, maintains cleanliness, replenishes towel provision.
2. [R2] **Fitness Centre Manager** — escalates temperature, equipment, or safety faults to Engineering; ensures facilities are not opened until all standards are met.
3. [R3] **Engineering / Maintenance** — responsible for heating element calibration, steam generator maintenance, jacuzzi water chemistry, and ventilation systems.

### Procedure

**Pre-Opening Safety & Facility Check**

1. [P1] Before any heat experience opens to guests, verify that **safety instructions are clearly displayed outside each facility** — positioned at the entrance where guests can read them before entering. [LQA 19.32]
   > Safety signage must include (at minimum): maximum recommended exposure time, contraindications (pregnancy, heart conditions, etc.), and emergency contact / alert procedure. MEVZUAT-mandated content will be integrated at Phase 6. Until then, apply the property's existing safety signage.
2. [P2] If any safety sign is missing, damaged, or illegible, do **not** open the affected facility. Replace or repair immediately and notify the Fitness Centre Manager. [LQA 19.32]
3. [P3] Inspect each heat experience for cleanliness and maintenance: [LQA 19.33]
   a. [SOP-OPR-FAC-003.P.3a] Benches, walls, floors, and glass panels (if present) must be clean and completely hygienic.
   b. [SOP-OPR-FAC-003.P.3b] Facility must be well maintained — check for damaged benches, cracked tiles, faulty lighting, and any hygiene concerns (mould, staining). Report all defects to Engineering with priority flag.
4. [P4] Verify that each heat experience is at its appropriate operating temperature before opening: [LQA 19.33]
   a. [SOP-OPR-FAC-003.P.4a] **Sauna** — confirm temperature is within the property's approved range using the in-sauna thermometer (required per P.5).
   b. [SOP-OPR-FAC-003.P.4b] **Steam room** — confirm steam is generating and temperature is within range.
   c. [SOP-OPR-FAC-003.P.4c] **Jacuzzi** — confirm water temperature is correct and jets are operational.
   d. [SOP-OPR-FAC-003.P.4d] If temperature is outside the acceptable range or equipment is malfunctioning, do not open the facility — notify Engineering and the Fitness Centre Manager immediately.
5. [P5] Verify that the **sauna** has both: [LQA 19.34]
   a. [SOP-OPR-FAC-003.P.5a] A **thermometer** — present, visible, and functioning.
   b. [SOP-OPR-FAC-003.P.5b] A **timer** — present, visible, and functioning (for guest self-monitoring of exposure time).
   c. If either instrument is absent or faulty, do not open the sauna. Replace or report immediately.

**Towel Provision**

6. [P6] Confirm that **towel hooks and a towel depository** are conveniently located next to each heat experience before opening. [LQA 19.35]
   a. [SOP-OPR-FAC-003.P.6a] Towel hooks must be clean, secure, and sufficient in number for expected guest volume.
   b. [SOP-OPR-FAC-003.P.6b] Towel depository (bin, basket, or designated receptacle for used towels) must be in place, clean, and emptied from the previous session.
7. [P7] Ensure an adequate supply of clean towels is available at or near the heat experience entrance for guests to collect on entry.

**During Operating Hours**

8. [P8] Check each heat experience at regular intervals throughout the shift — confirm cleanliness, temperature, and that safety signage remains in place and legible.
9. [P9] Empty and replace the towel depository whenever it reaches capacity. Replenish clean towels continuously.
10. [P10] If a guest reports discomfort, equipment malfunction, or a safety concern related to any heat experience, respond immediately: assist the guest, close the facility if safety requires it, and notify the Fitness Centre Manager.
11. [P11] Log all temperature deviations, equipment faults, and facility closures in the [MAINTENANCE LOG].

**Closing Procedure**

12. [P12] At closing, confirm all heat experiences are shut down per Engineering's technical protocol.
13. [P13] Complete a full cleanliness check of each facility — remove all used towels, wipe down benches and surfaces, and report any damage or hygiene concern discovered.

### Checklist

1. [C1] Safety instructions clearly displayed and legible outside each heat experience.
2. [C2] Each facility clean, well maintained, and at the correct operating temperature.
3. [C3] Sauna thermometer and timer both present and functioning.
4. [C4] Towel hooks and towel depository in place and clean next to each heat experience.
5. [C5] Clean towels available at/near each heat experience entrance.
6. [C6] No facility opened with missing safety sign, faulty temperature instrument, or out-of-range temperature.

### Brand Notes

- **No legacy SOP baseline.** Authored from scratch against LQA 19.32–19.35. No Template-SOPs.md equivalent exists for this stream.
- **MEVZUAT note:** This SOP supports compliance with **Law No. 6331** on Occupational Health and Safety (İş Sağlığı ve Güvenliği Kanunu), specifically Art. 4 (İşverenin genel yükümlülükleri — General Obligations), Art. 5 (Risklerden korunma ilkeleri — Risk Prevention Principles), Art. 10 (Risk değerlendirmesi — Risk Assessment: heat exposure risk assessment required), Art. 17 (Çalışanların eğitimi — Worker Training: staff trained on heat-related emergency response), and Art. 19 (Çalışanların yükümlülükleri — Worker Obligations). Turkish regulatory requirements for heat experiences (maximum exposure times, ventilation standards, mandatory medical contraindication notices, jacuzzi water quality testing intervals) are documented in discovery-notes.md §8A seeds. The property's existing safety signage and Engineering protocols apply pending equipment-specific calibration at rollout.
- **LQA 19.33 "appropriately heated":** temperature ranges are property-specific and must be confirmed by Engineering at rollout. Typical hospitality benchmarks: sauna 80–100°C; steam room 40–50°C; jacuzzi 38–40°C. These are guidance ranges only — not mandated by LQA.
- **LQA 19.35 "conveniently located":** towel hooks and depository must be at the entrance/threshold of the heat experience, not inside or far from the entrance. Position must be intuitive without staff direction.
- **[MAINTENANCE LOG]** — consistent with SOP-OPR-FAC-001 and other FIT SOPs.
