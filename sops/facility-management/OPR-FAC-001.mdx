### Purpose

Establish minimum facility conditions for the fitness centre, define the daily opening checklist, and set maintenance standards for all equipment, surfaces, and amenities, ensuring every guest arrives to a gym that is clean, fully equipped, and ready for use.

### Scope

1. [S1] Applies to the Fitness Centre Manager, Head Gym Attendant, and all Fitness Attendants responsible for opening and maintaining the gym.
2. [S2] Covers the main gym floor, cardio area, free-weights zone, and stretching/mat area.
3. [S3] Excludes pool, courts, wet/heat areas, and changing rooms — covered in SOP-SVC-MVT-002, SOP-OPR-FAC-002, SOP-OPR-FAC-003, and SOP-OPR-FAC-004 respectively.

### Responsibility

1. [R1] **Fitness Centre Manager** — reviews opening checklist daily, escalates equipment faults, updates maintenance log, approves Out of Order tagging.
2. [R2] **Head Gym Attendant** — completes opening checklist, performs amenity setup, reports defects before opening.
3. [R3] **All Fitness Attendants** — maintain facility conditions throughout shift, restock amenity stations as needed, log faults immediately.

### Procedure

**Opening Procedure**

1. [P1] Arrive at [PROPERTY GYM OPENING PREP TIME] before the gym opens to guests. Confirm that 24-hour access signage is displayed and legible at the gym entrance. [LQA 19.1]
2. [P2] Check ambient temperature — set to a comfortable level appropriate for exercise. Note and report any HVAC fault to Engineering immediately; do not open the gym if temperature is extreme. [LQA 19.3]
3. [P3] Check for any odors throughout the gym floor. If any are present, ventilate and apply approved odor-neutral solution. The gym must be odor-free before opening. [LQA 19.3]
4. [P4] Inspect all walls, ceilings, and floors — must be clean and well maintained. Report any defects (damage, staining, peeling) to Engineering with priority flag. [LQA 19.5]
5. [P5] Clean all windows and mirrors with streak-free solution until completely smear/smudge free. [LQA 19.6]
6. [P6] Verify all lighting is fully functional throughout the gym floor. Sufficient lighting must be provided at all times. Replace any failed bulbs or report for replacement before opening. [LQA 19.7]

**Equipment Readiness**

7. [P7] Confirm the minimum equipment complement is present, clean, sanitised, and in good working order: [LQA 19.8]
   a. [SOP-OPR-FAC-001.P.7a] Minimum **5 cardiovascular machines** (e.g., treadmill, elliptical, rowing machine, stationary bike, stair-climber).
   b. [SOP-OPR-FAC-001.P.7b] Minimum **5 resistance machines** (may be incorporated into a universal machine).
   c. [SOP-OPR-FAC-001.P.7c] **Two full sets of hand-held free weights** — verify full range present and correctly racked.
   d. [SOP-OPR-FAC-001.P.7d] Any equipment that is faulty, damaged, or unsafe must be tagged **Out of Order** immediately. Notify Engineering and the Fitness Centre Manager before opening.
8. [P8] Verify gym layout: zones for cardio machines, free weights, and stretching/mat areas must be clearly defined, with ample space for guests to exercise and navigate freely without obstruction. [LQA 19.9; Forbes Fitness 5.1]
9. [P9] Test each individual television monitor at cardiovascular machines — must be clean, functioning, and operable by the guest at the machine. Report any faulty monitors to Engineering. [LQA 19.11]
10. [P10] Confirm the wall clock is clearly visible from the gym floor and displays the correct time. Sync to the property time reference if any discrepancy is found. [LQA 19.16]

**Amenity Setup**

11. [P11] Place disinfectant wipes or a disinfectant cleaning agent with absorbent towels at each designated cleaning station throughout the gym floor. Stations must be clearly visible and accessible. [LQA 19.13; Forbes Fitness 5.1]
12. [P12] Stock the sweat towel station with clean, folded towels of appropriate size for gym use. [LQA 19.14; Forbes Fitness 5.1]
13. [P13] Verify the touchless water dispenser is filled and operational. Non-plastic cups or non-plastic bottled water must be provided. No single-use plastic cups. [LQA 19.14]
14. [P14] Prepare chilled towels and present them in [PROPERTY-DESIGNATED PRESENTATION VESSEL] at the designated station. All amenities must be presented on or in distinctive, high-quality serviceware. Replenish throughout the day as stock depletes. [LQA 19.15; Forbes Fitness 5.1]
15. [P15] Arrange the fruit bowl or healthy amenity display (e.g., cereal bars, etc.) — ensure items are fresh, plentiful, and completely free of plastic packaging. Remove and replace any item past peak freshness. [LQA 19.17; Forbes Fitness 5.1]
16. [P16] Verify Wi-Fi connectivity throughout the gym floor on a staff device. Connection must be strong and continuous, with no requirement to log in more than once. Report any connectivity issues to IT before opening. [LQA 19.18]

**During Operating Hours — Maintenance**

17. [P17] Inspect and restock all amenity stations at intervals throughout the shift. → See SOP-SVC-MVT-001 for attendant circulation frequency requirements.
18. [P18] Remove used or soiled towels discreetly during rounds. Replenish towel stock immediately so the station is never empty. [Forbes Fitness 5.1]
19. [P19] Wipe down all equipment surfaces following guest use. Ensure disinfectant cleaning stations remain stocked so guests may also self-clean. [Forbes Fitness 5.1; LQA 19.13]
20. [P20] Log any equipment faults, Out of Order items, or facility defects in the [MAINTENANCE LOG] immediately upon discovery. Include equipment ID, fault description, time reported, and Engineering ticket reference.

**Closing Procedure**

21. [P21] Complete the closing checklist — all equipment secured, perishable amenities (fruit, chilled towels) removed or stored per hygiene protocol, surfaces cleaned.
22. [P22] Confirm Out of Order tags are in place on any faulty equipment and Engineering follow-up is logged.
23. [P23] If 24-hour access is available, verify gym remains accessible and well-lit per [PROPERTY 24-HOUR ACCESS PROTOCOL]. If not, secure the gym per [PROPERTY CLOSING PROTOCOL].

### Checklist

1. [C1] 24-hour access signage visible and legible at gym entrance.
2. [C2] Ambient temperature comfortable for exercise; gym is odor-free.
3. [C3] Walls, ceilings, and floors clean and well maintained; defects reported.
4. [C4] Windows and mirrors completely smear/smudge free.
5. [C5] All lighting fully functional throughout gym floor.
6. [C6] Minimum equipment complement present (5 cardio, 5 resistance, 2 free-weight sets) — all clean and in good working order; faulty units tagged Out of Order.
7. [C7] Gym zones clearly defined with ample exercise space.
8. [C8] All cardio TV monitors clean and fully operational.
9. [C9] Wall clock visible and displaying correct time.
10. [C10] Disinfectant wipes/cleaning stations stocked throughout gym floor.
11. [C11] Sweat towel station stocked with clean, folded towels.
12. [C12] Touchless water dispenser filled; non-plastic cups or bottled water available.
13. [C13] Chilled towels prepared and presented on distinctive, high-quality serviceware.
14. [C14] Fruit bowl / healthy amenity display fresh, plentiful, and plastic-free.
15. [C15] Wi-Fi tested — strong, continuous, single-login connection confirmed.
16. [C16] Maintenance log updated; Engineering notified of all active faults.

### Brand Notes

- **No legacy SOP baseline.** Authored from scratch against LQA 19.1–19.18 and Forbes Fitness 5.1. No Template-SOPs.md equivalent exists for this stream.
- **[PROPERTY GYM OPENING PREP TIME]** — replace with property-specific prep arrival time (e.g., 30 minutes before opening).
- **[PROPERTY-DESIGNATED PRESENTATION VESSEL]** — property specifies the serviceware for chilled towel presentation. Must meet "distinctive high quality" threshold (Forbes 5.1).
- **[PROPERTY 24-HOUR ACCESS PROTOCOL]** / **[PROPERTY CLOSING PROTOCOL]** — property-specific access and security procedures.
- **[MAINTENANCE LOG]** — property specifies maintenance log tool/platform.
- **Fruit bowl vs. healthy amenity display:** LQA 19.17 permits either. Property choice; both options must be plastic-free.
- **Forbes 5.1 — "All amenities are thoughtfully presented on or in distinctive high quality serviceware":** applied at P.14 (chilled towels) and by implication to all amenity stations. Property must confirm serviceware meets this standard at rollout.
- **LQA 19.11 individual TV monitors:** standard assumes monitors present. If property does not have individual monitors, flag as non-conformance against 19.11 — cannot be waived by this SOP.
