### Purpose

Establish minimum facility, presentation, and amenity standards for all changing rooms and locker areas serving the fitness centre, ensuring guests arrive to a space that is clean, well maintained, fully stocked, and presented to a luxury standard at all times.

### Scope

1. [S1] Applies to Fitness Attendants and the Fitness Centre Manager responsible for changing room maintenance and amenity provision.
2. [S2] Covers all changing rooms and locker areas associated with the fitness centre, including showers, sinks, toilets, lockers, vanity areas, and all guest amenities therein.
3. [S3] Structural repair of plumbing, locker mechanisms, fixtures, and electrical systems is the responsibility of Engineering. This SOP governs operational readiness, cleanliness, and amenity provision only.
4. [S4] Changing rooms serving the spa are governed separately under the applicable Spa SOPs and are not within scope of this document.

### Responsibility

1. [R1] **Fitness Attendant** — completes daily opening inspection, maintains cleanliness and amenity stock throughout shift, reports all defects and faults immediately.
2. [R2] **Fitness Centre Manager** — reviews opening checklist compliance, escalates facility or equipment faults to Engineering, ensures changing rooms meet standards before guests are admitted.
3. [R3] **Engineering / Maintenance** — responsible for plumbing, locker mechanism repair, lighting replacement, HVAC, and all structural maintenance within the changing rooms.

### Procedure

**Opening Inspection**

1. [P1] Inspect the décor and overall presentation of the changing room before opening to guests. The changing room must present a fresh appearance of a high standard — modern, high-end finish throughout. Any area of visible wear, damage, or substandard finish must be reported to Engineering and the Fitness Centre Manager with a priority flag. [LQA 19.36]
2. [P2] Inspect all walls, ceilings, and floors — must be clean and well maintained throughout the changing room and locker area. Report any defects (damage, staining, grout deterioration, peeling surfaces) to Engineering immediately. [LQA 19.37]
3. [P3] Verify that all lighting is fully illuminated throughout the changing room, including vanity areas, shower cubicles, locker zones, and common areas. Replace any failed bulb or report immediately for replacement before opening. [LQA 19.38]
4. [P4] Inspect all showers, sinks, and toilets: [LQA 19.39]
   a. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.4a] All fixtures must be clean and free of staining, limescale, and residue.
   b. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.4b] All fixtures must be in good working order — functioning drains, correct water pressure, operable taps/controls. Report any fault to Engineering immediately.
   c. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.4c] Do not open affected shower cubicle(s) or sanitary area if any fixture is faulty — restrict from use until repaired.
5. [P5] Verify that each shower cubicle has a **shampoo, conditioner, and soap dispenser** present, visible, filled, and functioning. [LQA 19.40]
   > Dispensers must be large-format and refillable/recyclable per P.12. Confirm they are filled to an adequate level — not depleted or near-empty.
6. [P6] Inspect all lockers: [LQA 19.41]
   a. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.6a] Lockers must be attractive in appearance — clean inside and out, free of marks, stickers, or visible damage.
   b. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.6b] Lockers must be well maintained — locking mechanisms, hinges, and doors must be fully functional. Report any fault to Engineering immediately.
7. [P7] Inspect the **shaving / make-up mirror station**: [LQA 19.43]
   a. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.7a] Mirror must be well lit — all vanity lighting fully operational.
   b. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.7b] Mirror must be easily accessible — no obstructions, correct mounting height.
   c. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.7c] Mirror surface must be clean and smear-free.
8. [P8] Confirm **weighing scales** are present and in the designated location. [LQA 19.44]
   a. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.8a] Scales must be clean and fully functional. Test operation before opening.
   b. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.8b] If scales are faulty, report to Engineering immediately. Do not leave defective or visibly dirty scales in the guest area.
9. [P9] Verify that a **hairdryer** is available at the designated vanity station: [LQA 19.45]
   a. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.9a] Hairdryer must meet the minimum quality specification: **1875 wattage minimum; Ionic, lamination, or Tourmaline fusion technology**.
   b. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.9b] Hairdryer must be in good working order — test function before opening. Clean the unit and cord.
   c. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.9c] A hairdryer that is faulty, does not meet the minimum specification, or presents a safety concern must be removed from service and reported to the Fitness Centre Manager for replacement.

**Amenity Setup**

10. [P10] Place **clean, good-quality towels** at the designated towel station within or adjacent to the changing room. [LQA 19.42]
    > Towels must be folded consistently and presented neatly. Replenish throughout the shift so the station is never empty.
11. [P11] Confirm that **bathrobes and footwear (slippers)** are available at the designated station or in the designated storage area within the changing room, ready for guest use. [LQA 19.42]
    > Bathrobes must be clean, pressed, and presented neatly. Footwear must be hygienic and individually wrapped or presented per the property's presentation standard.
12. [P12] Stock the vanity amenity station with a **minimum amenity range** including: body lotion, deodorant, hair care items, razors, and shaving cream. [LQA 19.46]
    a. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.12a] All dispensed amenities (shampoo, conditioner, bath gel, body lotion) must be provided in **large-format bottles that are refillable or recyclable**. Miniature single-use format bottles are not permitted. [LQA 19.47]
    b. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.12b] All bathroom amenities must be **completely free of plastic wrapping**. Remove any plastic packaging before placing amenities in the guest area. [LQA 19.48]
    c. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.12c] All items must be fully stocked before opening. Check fill levels of all dispensers; refill any that are below half capacity.

**During Operating Hours**

13. [P13] Inspect and service the changing rooms at regular intervals throughout the shift. Each inspection must cover:
    a. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.13a] Cleanliness of all surfaces, floors, mirrors, and fixtures — clean and wipe down as required.
    b. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.13b] Shower cubicles — remove used items, wipe down walls and bench, check dispenser levels.
    c. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.13c] Towel, bathrobe, and footwear stations — collect used/soiled items discreetly; replenish as required.
    d. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.13d] Amenity station — replenish any item that is depleted or near-depleted.
    e. [SOP-OPR-FAC-004.P.13e] Locker area — remove any items left behind by guests; wipe down locker exteriors.
14. [P14] Collect all soiled towels and bathrobes discreetly during rounds — never leave used linen visible to incoming guests.
15. [P15] If any equipment, fixture, or amenity fault is discovered during operating hours, log it in the [MAINTENANCE LOG] immediately and notify Engineering. Restrict access to the affected area or item if a safety concern exists.

**Closing Procedure**

16. [P16] At closing, complete a full inspection of all changing room areas — remove all soiled towels, bathrobes, and footwear; wipe down all surfaces, mirrors, and fixtures; check dispenser levels and refill as needed for the next day.
17. [P17] Confirm all lockers are empty and closed. Report any items left by guests to [LOST AND FOUND PROTOCOL].
18. [P18] Log any defects, faults, or maintenance items discovered during the closing inspection in the [MAINTENANCE LOG]. Confirm Engineering follow-up is scheduled for any open items.

### Checklist

1. [C1] Changing room décor fresh in appearance and to a high standard (modern, high-end finish); décor defects reported to Engineering.
2. [C2] Walls, ceilings, and floors clean and well maintained; defects reported.
3. [C3] All lighting fully illuminated throughout — vanity areas, shower cubicles, locker zones, and common areas.
4. [C4] Showers, sinks, and toilets clean and in good working order; faulty fixtures reported and area restricted.
5. [C5] Shampoo, conditioner, and soap dispenser present, filled, and functioning in every shower cubicle.
6. [C6] Lockers attractive, clean, and fully functional; locking mechanism faults reported immediately.
7. [C7] Shaving/make-up mirror well lit, accessible, and smear-free.
8. [C8] Weighing scales present, clean, and fully functional.
9. [C9] Hairdryer present, meets minimum specification (1875W, Ionic/lamination/Tourmaline), and in good working order.
10. [C10] Clean towels, bathrobes, and footwear available and presented to standard.
11. [C11] Full amenity range stocked (body lotion, deodorant, hair care, razors, shaving cream); all in large-format refillable/recyclable bottles; no plastic wrapping.
12. [C12] All faults logged in [MAINTENANCE LOG]; Engineering notified; affected areas restricted if safety concern.

### Brand Notes

- **No legacy SOP baseline.** Authored from scratch against LQA 19.36–19.48. No Template-SOPs.md equivalent exists for this stream.
- **LQA 19.36 "fresh in appearance and of a high standard (i.e. modern high end finish)":** This is a presentation standard, not a cleaning standard. It requires the space to look current, well-appointed, and premium in design. Worn décor, dated finishes, or visible age must be escalated as a facility issue — not treated as a cleaning matter.
- **LQA 19.40 dispensers:** Standard requires shampoo, conditioner, and soap dispenser "present in the shower cubicle" — confirmed as large-format per LQA 19.47 (no miniature format permitted).
- **LQA 19.42 towels, bathrobes, footwear:** Applied at P.10–P.11. "Clean good quality" is the standard — property must confirm presentation method and quality threshold at rollout. Footwear may be single-pair hygienic packaging per property standard.
- **LQA 19.45 hairdryer specification:** "1875 wattage minimum; Ionic, lamination, or Tourmaline fusion" — this is a quality minimum, not just a safety standard. A hairdryer that fails the wattage or technology specification is non-compliant even if functional. Property must verify against current stock at rollout.
- **LQA 19.46 amenity range minimum:** Body lotion, deodorant, hair care items, razors, shaving cream. Property may expand this list. Items may be dispensed (large-format per 19.47) or individually packaged (plastic-free per 19.48).
- **LQA 19.47 large-format bottles:** Miniature format is explicitly excluded. "Refillable/recyclable" is the stated standard — property must confirm supplier and refill protocol at rollout.
- **LQA 19.48 "free of plastic wrapping":** Applies to all bathroom amenities. Individual sachets, plastic wrap, or plastic film packaging are non-compliant. Remove all plastic packaging before placing amenities in the guest area.
- **[MAINTENANCE LOG]** — consistent with SOP-OPR-FAC-001 and all FIT SOPs. Property specifies platform/format.
- **[LOST AND FOUND PROTOCOL]** — property-specific. Referenced at closing (P.17) for items left in lockers.
- **Changing room gender separation:** If the property operates separate male and female changing rooms, both must meet these standards independently. This SOP applies to each changing room facility individually.
